# Episode Description

How does a raw recording become a polished, discoverable podcast episode? In this episode, Maya Chen — who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems — walks through the five-stage podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

We cover the pain points small teams face first — messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready copy. Maya explains why keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts matters, where automation genuinely helps, and where human judgment remains irreplaceable. We also dig into title strategy, the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and a practical five-step plan any small show can implement this month.

The core takeaway: use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but never abdicate responsibility for what goes live. A reliable pipeline isn't about elaborate tooling — it's about consistency.
