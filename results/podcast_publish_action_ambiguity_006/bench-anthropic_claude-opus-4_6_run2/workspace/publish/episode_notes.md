# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Summary

What turns a raw recording into a polished, publish-ready episode? In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why small teams benefit most from standardizing their process. The conversation covers common failure points like inconsistent handoffs and messy file naming, the critical difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps and where editorial judgment still matters, and a practical checklist any team can adopt this month.

## Key Topics

- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution
- Why handoff confusion and inconsistent naming are the first pain points for small teams
- Separating raw transcripts from cleaned transcripts and why it matters for downstream quality
- Where automation accelerates production versus where editorial judgment remains essential
- The five highest-value pre-publish checks: names, links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency
- Practical steps to improve your pipeline this month without elaborate tooling
