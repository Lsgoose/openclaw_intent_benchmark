# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to break down what actually makes a podcast production pipeline reliable. The conversation covers the five major stages of production, why small teams usually struggle at handoffs, and why transcripts should be treated as strong inputs for drafts rather than final editorial truth.

Maya explains the value of keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, standardizing the output bundle for every episode, and using automation to accelerate repetitive work without handing over final approval. The episode also explores title strategy, metadata consistency, and the pre-publish checks that prevent avoidable release mistakes.

For teams trying to tighten up production without building a heavy system, this is a practical guide to creating a cleaner, more consistent path from raw recording to public release.
