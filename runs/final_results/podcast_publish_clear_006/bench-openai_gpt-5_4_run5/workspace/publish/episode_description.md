# Episode 14 Description

In Episode 14, the conversation breaks down what it actually takes to turn a raw recording into a reliable, polished podcast release. Maya Chen explains the five stages of a strong production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The discussion covers where small teams usually struggle first, why transcripts are powerful but not automatically publish-ready, and how simple systems can prevent avoidable mistakes.

The episode also explores where automation genuinely helps, where human editorial judgment still matters, and why titles, links, metadata, and sponsor language all need deliberate review. The practical takeaway is a lightweight framework any small show can adopt: standardize folders, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, define a repeatable output bundle, and make one person own the release checklist.
