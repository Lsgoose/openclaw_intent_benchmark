# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Episode Summary

What does it actually take to get a podcast episode from raw recording to published release? In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen break down the five-stage production pipeline that separates consistent shows from chaotic ones — and why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems. She walks through the full arc: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Along the way, she explains why handoffs break small teams, why a clean transcript is not the same as publish-ready content, and where automation helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable.

Key themes include the importance of separating raw and cleaned transcripts, defining a standard output bundle for every episode, and treating titles as a contract with the listener rather than a label. Maya also covers the five highest-value pre-publish checks and offers a practical five-step improvement plan for small teams.

## Key Takeaways

- A podcast production pipeline has five stages: **capture → post-production → content extraction → review & approval → distribution**
- The pipeline is the product — inconsistency in production creates inconsistency in listener experience
- Keep both a **raw transcript** (for traceability) and a **cleaned transcript** (for repurposing and summarization)
- A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth — facts, links, names, and claims still need human review
- Define a **standard output bundle** for every episode: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, and a publish log
- Titles are a contract with the listener — generate multiple candidates from different angles before choosing
- Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms; keep humans responsible for what goes live
- Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy is underrated and immediately felt by listeners

---

## Five Pre-Publish Checks (from Maya Chen)

1. **Names and affiliations** — Verify guest name, title, and company
2. **Links and references** — Confirm every linked destination is correct
3. **Sponsor compliance** — Required sponsor language is present where it should be
4. **Sensitive edits** — Anything the guest wanted removed is actually removed
5. **Metadata consistency** — Title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy all describe the same episode

---

## Five-Step Pipeline Improvement Plan

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to surface delays, confusion, and repeated errors
