In this episode, we unpack why your podcast production pipeline is just as important as the conversation itself. Consultant Maya Chen joins us to walk through a five‑stage pipeline: capture, post‑production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

We explore where small teams feel the most pain—messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and over‑reliance on raw transcripts—and how to design a lightweight but reliable workflow using a standard episode workspace, clear naming conventions, and a fixed output bundle.

Maya explains why transcripts should be treated as rich input rather than final editorial truth, when automation genuinely helps, and where human judgment still has to own what goes live. We also cover practical review checkpoints, from verifying names and links to keeping titles, descriptions, and show notes in sync.

If you’re ready to turn your podcast from a heroic effort into a repeatable system, this episode will help you tighten up your pipeline without drowning in process.
