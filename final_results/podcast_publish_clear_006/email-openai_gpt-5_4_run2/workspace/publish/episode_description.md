# Episode 14 Description

A strong podcast is not just a great conversation. It is the result of a reliable production pipeline that turns raw recordings into a clean, consistent, publish-ready episode.

In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of podcast production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation covers where small teams usually struggle, why transcripts should be treated as rich inputs rather than final truth, how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps most, and which pre-publish checks catch the highest-value mistakes.

The episode also includes practical advice on naming conventions, handoffs, output bundles, metadata consistency, and title strategy. The main takeaway is simple: automate drafts where it saves time, but keep human ownership over anything that goes public.
