# Episode 14 Description: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Most listeners never see the work behind a polished episode — yet the production pipeline shapes every part of the listener experience. In this episode, Maya Chen, a media production systems advisor, breaks down how a show goes from a raw recording to a fully packaged, discoverable episode.

Maya outlines five core pipeline stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams hit friction first — chaotic handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the common mistake of treating raw transcripts as publish-ready copy.

The conversation covers what automation can and can't handle in a media workflow, why titles deserve more strategic thought than most teams give them, and a practical pre-publish checklist covering names, links, sponsor compliance, and metadata consistency.

Maya's bottom line: a good pipeline isn't about elaborate tooling. It's about consistency, clear ownership, and the discipline to separate drafts from releases. An automated pipeline accelerates the process — but a human still owns what goes live.
