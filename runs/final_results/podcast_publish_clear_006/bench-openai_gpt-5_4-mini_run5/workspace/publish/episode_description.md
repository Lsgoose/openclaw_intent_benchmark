Episode 14 explores how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline that turns a raw recording into a polished, publishable episode. Maya Chen breaks the process into five stages—capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution—and explains why small teams need clear handoffs, consistent naming, and a fixed output bundle. The conversation covers the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps, where it fails on edge cases, and why titles and metadata should be treated as promises to listeners. The episode closes with practical steps for improving a show’s workflow without overcomplicating it.
