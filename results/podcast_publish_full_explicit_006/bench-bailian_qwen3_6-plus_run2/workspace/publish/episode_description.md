# Episode 14 — Episode Description

How does a raw recording become a polished, discoverable podcast episode? In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five-stage production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product, not just the conversation.

We cover the three pain points that trip up small teams first: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready. Maya argues for keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, standardizing your output bundle, and using automation to accelerate drafts — never to replace human approval.

You'll also hear practical advice on title strategy, a five-point pre-publish review checklist, and five concrete steps any small team can implement this month to make their pipeline more reliable. If you run a show and want fewer last-minute fires and more consistent quality, this episode is for you.
