# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen ([Profile](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen))  
*Media systems advisor helping creator teams build sustainable production workflows.*

---

## [00:00] Introduction: The Pipeline Is the Product

Maya Chen joins the show to discuss how podcast production pipelines shape listener experience. While conversations are the heart of a show, the systems that transform raw recordings into polished episodes—from audio cleanup to metadata management—determine whether audiences click, stay, and return.

> *"This is one of those topics people don't always notice until something breaks, and then suddenly everyone realizes the pipeline is the product."*

---

## [01:20] The Five Stages of Production

Maya outlines a framework that mirrors software build pipelines:

1. **Capture** – Recording plus companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links, references)
2. **Post-Production** – Audio cleanup, mistake trimming, editorial decisions
3. **Content Extraction** – Transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. **Review & Approval** – Fact-checking, sponsor language verification, legal review
5. **Distribution** – Hosting platform, newsletter, social channels, archives

> *"You've got source material, transformations, validation steps, packaging, and deployment."*

---

## [02:33] Where Small Teams Break Down

Three recurring pain points for lean operations:

- **Handoff gaps** – No single source of truth when recording, editing, and copywriting are split across people
- **Asset chaos** – Inconsistent naming (*final_v2_real_final.wav*) destroys version confidence
- **Transcript assumptions** – Raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified statements that require judgment to clean

---

## [03:40] Transcripts as Input, Not Truth

Maya distinguishes between raw and cleaned transcripts, emphasizing what automation can and cannot handle:

| Suitable for Automation | Requires Human Review |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| First-draft summaries | Facts, names, affiliations |
| Chapter suggestions | Links and references |
| Quote candidates | Sponsor compliance language |
| Social post options | Sensitive edits |
| Searchability | Claims and dates |

> *"The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."*

---

## [04:26] Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams

For teams of two to three people, Maya recommends:

- One folder/workspace per episode with clear naming conventions
- A publish checklist with defined handoff points
- A fixed **output bundle** for every episode: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, and publish log
- Separate raw and cleaned transcripts (the former for traceability, the latter for downstream use)

---

## [05:57] Automation Boundaries

Two areas where teams consistently overestimate automation:

1. **Editorial judgment** – Agents can propose titles but lack audience positioning context unless explicitly encoded
2. **Exception handling** – Most episodes flow smoothly, but edge cases (name corrections, legal review, sensitive anecdotes, sponsor constraints) break fully automated systems

> *"Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms... but don't give up responsibility for what goes live."*

---

## [06:57] Title Strategy

Titles are contracts with listeners. Maya advises generating candidates from multiple angles:

- Problem-oriented
- Guest-oriented  
- Outcome-oriented
- Curiosity-driven

> *"Frequency is not strategy. The most repeated phrase in a conversation is often not the most compelling framing."*

The workflow target: three title candidates, one short description, one long description, and a final approved version logged before publish.

---

## [08:20] Pre-Publish Review Checklist

Five high-value checks before going live:

1. **Names and affiliations** – Verify guest details
2. **Links and references** – Confirm every "we'll link that" promise
3. **Sponsor compliance** – Check required language placement
4. **Sensitive edits** – Confirm requested removals are complete
5. **Metadata consistency** – Title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy must describe the same episode

---

## [09:23] Action Plan: Improve Your Pipeline This Month

Five steps for immediate gains:

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with a single owner
3. Separate raw transcript from cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors

Bonus: Start automation by automating drafts, not approvals.
