# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen  
**Runtime:** ~10 minutes

## Summary

A conversation with Maya Chen about how podcast production pipelines shape the listener experience. We cover the five stages every show goes through—from capture to distribution—and the practical systems small teams can use to reduce friction without adding complexity.

## Key Takeaways

- **The pipeline is the product.** Clean audio, accurate transcripts, consistent metadata, and polished titles all contribute to how listeners perceive your show.

- **Five stages:** capture → post-production → content extraction → review/approval → distribution.

- **Raw vs. clean transcripts:** Keep both. Raw for traceability, cleaned for downstream use. Don't let disfluencies and false starts pollute your summaries and social clips.

- **Automation wisdom:** Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms (transcription cleanup, first drafts, formatting). Don't abdicate editorial judgment—someone still owns what goes live.

- **Titles matter:** They're not just labels, they're contracts with the listener. Generate multiple candidates and choose based on audience intent, not keyword frequency.

## Maya's 5-Step Action Plan

For small teams who want better pipelines this month:

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot delays and repeated errors
