# Episode Description

What makes a podcast feel polished isn’t just the conversation—it’s the production pipeline behind it. In this episode, Maya Chen joins the show to break down how strong systems turn a raw recording into a reliable release.

The conversation covers the five stages of a podcast production pipeline, why small teams struggle with handoffs and version chaos, and why a transcript should be treated as a rich input rather than editorial truth. Maya also explains the difference between raw and clean transcripts, where automation helps, where human review still matters, and how better title and metadata decisions shape listener trust.

The episode closes with a practical checklist for small teams: standardize folders, separate transcript types, define a fixed output bundle, and make one person own the publish checklist.
