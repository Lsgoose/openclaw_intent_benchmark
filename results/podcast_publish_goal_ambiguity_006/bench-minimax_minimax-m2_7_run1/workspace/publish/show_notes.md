# Episode Show Notes

## Timestamped Sections

**[00:00:00] Intro**
Welcome back to the show. Today we unpack the podcast production pipeline—how a show goes from raw recording to polished episode with audio, transcript, chapters, clips, title, description, and metadata.

**[00:00:27] Guest Introduction**
Maya Chen joins to discuss production systems for media teams and creator businesses.

**[00:00:36] What is a Podcast Production Pipeline?**
Maya breaks production into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Compares it to a software build pipeline—source material, transformations, validation, packaging, deployment.

**[00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle**
Three common pain points: handoffs without a single source of truth; inconsistent file naming ("final_v2_real_final.wav"); and transcript-driven tasks that require more judgment than expected.

**[00:03:34] Raw Transcript vs. Clean Transcript**
A transcript can contain filler, false starts, unverified links, and corrected statements. "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." Maya recommends keeping both versions.

**[00:05:23] Automation and AI Tools**
Automation excels at repetitive transforms: cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions. Humans own editorial judgment and exception handling. "Use automation for acceleration, not abdication."

**[00:06:52] Title Strategy**
Titles do significant work—they're a contract with the listener. Generate candidates from multiple angles: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven. Compare against audience intent, not word frequency.

**[00:08:20] Pre-Release Quality Checks**
Five critical checks: guest names and affiliations; verified links and references; sponsor compliance; sensitive edit removal; and metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, and chapters.

**[00:09:13] Practical Takeaways**
Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention. Create a publish checklist with one owner. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts. Define a required output bundle. Keep a lightweight episode log to identify recurring issues.

**[00:10:05] Sponsor & Close**
Thanks to SoundLayer. Maya Chen, thanks for joining. Until next week.
