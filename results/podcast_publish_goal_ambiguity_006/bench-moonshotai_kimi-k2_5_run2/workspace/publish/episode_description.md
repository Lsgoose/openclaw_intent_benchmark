Maya Chen joins the show to break down podcast production pipelines: the five stages from capture to distribution, why raw transcripts aren't publish-ready, and how small teams can build reliable workflows without over-automating. We cover folder structure, naming conventions, the critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, and where automation helps versus where human judgment still matters. Maya's practical framework focuses on consistency over complexity—standardized output bundles, clear handoff points, and keeping humans in the approval loop while letting machines handle drafts. If your episodes have ever suffered from "final_v2_real_final.wav" confusion or mismatched metadata, this one's for you. Includes a look at SoundLayer for team collaboration.
