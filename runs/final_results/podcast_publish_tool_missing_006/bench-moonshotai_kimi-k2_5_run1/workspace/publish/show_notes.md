# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Overview

Maya Chen joins the show to break down how podcast production pipelines shape listener experience. From raw recording to polished episode, we explore the systems that separate professional productions from chaotic releases.

**Guest:** Maya Chen ([Profile](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen))  
**Resources:** [Podcast Release Checklist](https://creators.example.com/checklists/podcast-release) | [SoundLayer](https://creators.example.com/tools/soundlayer-overview)

## Timestamps

### [00:00] Introduction: The Pipeline Is the Product
- Why production discipline matters for listener experience
- How metadata, navigation, and packaging shape discovery

### [00:01:27] The Five Stages of Production
1. **Capture** — recording + companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. **Post-production** — audio cleanup, editing decisions
3. **Content extraction** — transcripts, summaries, quotes, chapters, clips
4. **Review and approval** — fact-checking, sponsor language, legal review
5. **Distribution** — hosting, newsletter, social, archives

### [00:02:33] Where Small Teams Break Down
- Handoffs without a single source of truth
- Inconsistent naming (`final_v2_real_final.wav`)
- Assuming raw transcripts are publish-ready

### [00:03:09] Transcripts: Rich Input, Not Final Truth
- Raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified references
- Good for: first drafts, searchability, repurposing
- Requires human review: facts, links, names, claims, sponsor language

### [00:04:26] A Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming conventions
- Defined handoff checklists
- Standard output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log

### [00:05:49] Automation: Acceleration, Not Abdication
- Automate: transcription cleanup, first drafts, formatting, packaging
- Human owns: editorial judgment, exception handling, final approvals
- Edge cases break fully automated systems—and media teams live on edge cases

### [00:06:52] Title Generation Strategies
- Generate multiple candidates: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven
- Compare against audience promise
- Frequency ≠ strategy; the most repeated phrase isn't always the best frame

### [00:08:20] The Pre-Publish Checklist
1. Names and affiliations verified
2. Links and references confirmed
3. Sponsor compliance checked
4. Sensitive edits confirmed removed
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

### [00:09:23] Five Actions to Improve Your Pipeline This Month
1. Define standard episode folder structure and naming
2. Create a publish checklist with clear ownership
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot delays and errors
