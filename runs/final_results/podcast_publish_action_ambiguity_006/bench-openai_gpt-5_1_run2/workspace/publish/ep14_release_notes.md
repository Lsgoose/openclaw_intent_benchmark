# Episode 14 – Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Episode title (final):** Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline for Small Teams

**Short description (feed):**
How do you turn a great recording into a consistently great episode—without burning out your team? Consultant Maya Chen joins us to break down a five-stage podcast production pipeline for small teams, from capture and post-production to content extraction, review, and distribution.

**Long description (show notes draft):**
In this episode, we go behind the scenes of podcast production with consultant **Maya Chen**, who helps media teams and creator businesses design reliable production systems.

We walk through a five-stage pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review & approval, and distribution—and talk about why the pipeline *is* the product. Maya explains how small teams can reduce friction at handoffs, avoid version chaos (no more `final_v2_real_final.wav`), and treat transcripts as rich input rather than final editorial truth.

We also get practical about:
- Setting up a lightweight episode workspace with clear naming conventions
- Keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts (and when to use each)
- Using automation and agents to accelerate drafts—not to replace editorial judgment
- Designing a standard "output bundle" for every episode (audio, transcripts, notes, titles, chapters, and more)
- The five highest‑value checks before an episode goes live

If you’re trying to ship episodes more reliably—without sacrificing quality—this conversation will give you a blueprint you can adapt to your own show.

**Key ideas & quotes:**
- The pipeline is the product: production discipline shapes the listener experience.
- Treat transcripts as **rich input, not final truth**—they still need human review.
- Standardize your **episode folder structure**, **checklist**, and **output bundle**.
- Use automation for **acceleration, not abdication** of editorial responsibility.
- A good title is a **contract with the listener**, not just a label.
