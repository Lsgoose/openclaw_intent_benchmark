# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Summary
This episode examines how a show moves from raw recording to a polished release. Host and Maya Chen frame podcast production like a build pipeline: source material, transformations, validation steps, packaging, and deployment. They focus on practical systems small teams can adopt without adding unnecessary complexity.

## Key takeaways
- A reliable pipeline has five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.
- The pipeline breaks first at handoffs, inconsistent naming, and unclear sources of truth.
- A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth.
- Keep both raw and cleaned transcripts: raw for traceability, cleaned for summarization and repurposing.
- Standardize the output bundle for every episode: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail if used, and a publish log.
- Use automation to accelerate drafts and repetitive transforms, not to replace approval and accountability.
- Review should verify names, affiliations, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.
