- **Guest:** Maya Chen (media and creator business production advisor)

- **Status:** Release ready

## Summary

Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains why small teams benefit more from consistency and checklists than from complexity. Key themes include treating the transcript as a rich input rather than editorial truth, generating multiple title candidates before choosing, and automating drafts while keeping approvals human.

## Show Notes

**5 Stages of the Pipeline**
1. Capture — recording and collecting companion materials (guest bios, sponsor copy, links, references)
2. Post-production — audio cleanup, mistake trimming, editorial decisions
3. Content extraction — transcript, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. Review and approval — fact-check, sponsor language, names, links, editorial/legal review
5. Distribution — hosting platform, newsletter, social, archives

**Key Quotes**
- "A transcript is a rich input, not final editorial truth."
- "Automate drafts, not approvals."
- "A title is not just a label. It's a contract with the listener."

**Practical Takeaways for Small Teams**
1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist owned by one person
3. Separate raw transcript from cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot recurring delays and errors

**Automation Guidance**
Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms: transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, formatting, asset packaging, metadata prefill. Do not automate editorial judgment or exception handling.
