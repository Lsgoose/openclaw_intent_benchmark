# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to break down what actually makes a podcast production pipeline reliable. The conversation covers the five major stages of a release workflow, why small teams usually fail at handoffs and versioning, and why a transcript should be treated as a rich input rather than final editorial truth. Maya explains the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps most, and where human judgment still matters—especially for titles, links, sensitive edits, and release quality. The episode also lays out a practical framework for small teams: standardize your episode workspace, define a consistent output bundle, use a publish checklist with a clear owner, and automate drafts without automating approvals. It’s a useful guide for anyone trying to make podcast production more repeatable without making it brittle.
