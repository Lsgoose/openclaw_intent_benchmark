## Timestamps

### [00:00:00] Introduction: Why the Pipeline Is the Product
- Opening: The podcast production pipeline shapes the entire listener experience
- Guest introduction: Maya Chen on production systems for media teams
- Key insight: "The pipeline is the product" — people notice production when it breaks

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Production Pipeline
1. **Capture** — Recording + companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links, references)
2. **Post-production** — Audio cleanup, trimming, editorial decisions
3. **Content extraction** — Transcripts, summaries, quotes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. **Review and approval** — Fact-checking, sponsor compliance, editorial review
5. **Distribution** — Hosting platform, newsletter, social channels, archives

### [00:02:28] Common Pain Points for Small Teams
- Handoffs between roles without a single source of truth
- Inconsistent naming and asset storage ("final_v2_real_final.wav")
- Transcript-driven tasks requiring more judgment than expected

### [00:03:08] Transcript Strategy: Raw vs. Clean
- Raw transcript = internal reference and traceability
- Clean transcript = publish-ready, formatted, disfluencies removed
- **Quote**: "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth"
- Automation works best when noise is reduced at the source

### [00:05:56] Automation: Where to Accelerate vs. Abdicate
- **Accelerate**: Repetitive transforms — transcription cleanup, first drafts, formatting, metadata prefill
- **Human owns**: Editorial judgment, exception handling, edge cases, final approvals
- Healthy mindset: "Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what becomes public."

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
- Generate candidates from multiple angles: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven
- A title is a contract with the listener — not just a label
- **Avoid**: Mechanically picking the most repeated phrase (frequency is not strategy)

### [00:08:13] Pre-Publish Review Checklist
1. Names and affiliations — verify guest details
2. Links and references — confirm destinations mentioned
3. Sponsor compliance — check required language is present
4. Sensitive edits — confirm requested removals are complete
5. Metadata consistency — title, description, transcript, chapters should align

### [00:09:19] Quick Wins for Small Shows
1. Define standard folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with clear owner
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot delays and repeated errors
