# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing an episode? Production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams most often break down: handoffs without a shared source of truth, inconsistent file naming, and the dangerous assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready content. They cover what automation handles well (first-draft summaries, chapter suggestions, formatting) versus where human judgment is still essential (editorial calls, exception handling, anything that goes live under your name).

You'll also get a practical five-step starter plan for tightening your own pipeline this month, a framework for generating title candidates that actually serve your audience, and the five highest-value checks to run before every episode goes live.

If you run a small show and want a more consistent, less stressful release process, this episode is the place to start.
