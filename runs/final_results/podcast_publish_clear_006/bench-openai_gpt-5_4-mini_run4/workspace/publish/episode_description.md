Episode 14 looks at how a podcast moves from raw recording to a reliable release pipeline. Maya Chen maps the core stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then shows where small teams usually break down. The discussion covers why transcripts are useful but not final truth, how to keep raw and cleaned versions separate, and why titles, descriptions, chapters, and social copy must stay aligned. It also explains where automation helps and where human judgment still matters, especially for links, sponsor language, names, and sensitive edits. The episode closes with a practical checklist for building a cleaner, more consistent production workflow.
