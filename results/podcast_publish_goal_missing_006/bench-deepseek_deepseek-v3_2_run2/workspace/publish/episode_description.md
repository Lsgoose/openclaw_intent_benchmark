Why do title tests often fail to deliver lasting results? In this episode, we explore the common mistake of confusing click-through rate with audience fit. Guest Jordan Kim, who helps podcast networks run title experiments and packaging reviews, explains that a title test matrix is only useful when each option maps to a real audience promise—otherwise you're just measuring random novelty.

Teams frequently over-index on thumbnail packaging, click-through rate, and novelty while forgetting listener intent. Jordan shares practical solutions: keep a test log that records the hypothesis, audience segment, winning frame, and why it won. A healthy workflow includes one shared title board, a short review loop, and a final approved headline before distribution.

The key lesson isn't to "test everything forever" but to "learn which promise actually matches the episode." Jordan reveals the most common mistake: running title tests without recording context, which leads to winning headlines being reused in the wrong places and performance dropping. For small teams looking to improve this month, start a simple experiment log, write down the audience promise for each option, and stop treating click-through rate as the whole strategy.
