## Release Summary
This episode breaks down the production pipeline as a series of disciplined handoffs: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation emphasizes that a clean transcript is a rich input, not final truth, and that reliable publishing depends on consistent naming, versioning, metadata alignment, and human approval for anything that goes public.

## Final Episode Description
A strong podcast is more than a good conversation. It depends on the systems that turn a raw recording into a publish-ready episode.

In this episode, we talk about the five stages of a reliable production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. We cover why transcript cleanup matters, how to separate raw and cleaned material, what to verify before publish, and why automation should speed up drafts without replacing human judgment.

You'll also hear practical guidance on naming conventions, output bundles, chapter markers, metadata consistency, and review checks that keep small teams from shipping avoidable mistakes.

## Long Description
This episode explores the operational side of podcasting: the folders, checklists, handoffs, and validation steps that keep a show reliable.

Maya Chen outlines five stages of a healthy podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The discussion highlights why transcript cleanup matters, how to keep raw and cleaned transcripts separate, and why metadata consistency is essential across titles, descriptions, chapter markers, and social copy.

For small teams, the biggest wins come from consistency: a standard episode folder structure, a fixed output bundle, a clear publish checklist, and a lightweight episode log. The goal is not to automate approvals, but to automate drafts so humans can approve what goes live.
