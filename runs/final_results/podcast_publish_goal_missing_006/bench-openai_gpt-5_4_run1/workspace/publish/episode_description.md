# Episode Description

How does a podcast actually go from raw recording to polished release? This episode breaks down the production pipeline behind a reliable show. Host and guest Maya Chen walk through the five stages of podcast production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

They discuss where small teams usually struggle, why transcripts should be treated as rich inputs instead of final editorial truth, and how a consistent output bundle can reduce errors across titles, descriptions, chapter markers, and social assets. The conversation also covers where automation helps most, where human judgment still matters, and why exception handling is often where fully automated systems fail.

If a show feels inconsistent across audio, metadata, and promotion, the problem is often the pipeline rather than the conversation itself. This episode offers a practical framework for building a lightweight, dependable workflow without overcomplicating the system.
