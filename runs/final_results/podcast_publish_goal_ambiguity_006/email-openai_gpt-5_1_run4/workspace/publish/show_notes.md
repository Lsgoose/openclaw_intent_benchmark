## [00:01:20] The five core stages of a podcast production pipeline
- Capture: recording plus companion materials (guest bios, sponsor copy, links, references).
- Post-production: audio cleanup, trims, and editorial decisions about what stays in.
- Content extraction: transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapters, titles, clips.
- Review and approval: fact checks, sponsor language, names, links, legal or editorial issues.
- Distribution: packaging for the host platform, newsletter, social channels, and archives.

## [00:02:29] Common failure modes for small production teams
- Handoffs without a single source of truth across recording, editing, and copy.
- Inconsistent naming and storage (final_v2_real_final.wav) eroding confidence in the source of record.
- Over-trusting raw transcripts as publish-ready content.

## [00:04:15] Designing a lightweight but reliable pipeline for small teams
- One folder or workspace per episode with a clear naming convention.
- A checklist with explicit handoff points and ownership.
- Defining a fixed output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, and publish log.

## [00:09:13] Practical steps to improve your pipeline this month
- Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention.
- Create a publish checklist with a clear owner.
- Separate raw and cleaned transcripts in your workflow.
- Standardize the required output bundle for every episode.
- Keep a lightweight episode log to spot delays, confusion, and repeated errors.
- Start automating drafts, not approvals, for safer experimentation.
