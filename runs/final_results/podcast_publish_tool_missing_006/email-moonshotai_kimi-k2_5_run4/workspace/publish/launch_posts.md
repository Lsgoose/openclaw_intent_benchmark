## Post 1: Key Takeaway

A reliable podcast pipeline isn't about more tools—it's about consistent handoffs. The teams that ship great episodes define a standard output bundle (audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail) and use the same folder structure every time. When everyone knows where to look and what "done" looks like, small teams punch above their weight. Episode 14 breaks down the five-stage production pipeline and where to start if you want better output this month.

## Post 2: Discussion Question

Where does your production pipeline actually break? Most teams assume it's the edit, but Maya Chen says the real friction points are earlier: unclear handoffs between roles, files named "final_v2_real_final," and raw transcripts treated as publish-ready truth. If you fixed just one stage of your pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, or distribution—which would improve the listener experience most? Episode 14 unpacks each stage and what small teams can automate (and what they shouldn't).
