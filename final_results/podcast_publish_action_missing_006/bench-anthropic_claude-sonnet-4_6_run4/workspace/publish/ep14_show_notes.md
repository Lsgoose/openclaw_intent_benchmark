---

## Long description

If you've ever wondered how a show goes from a raw recording to a polished episode with clean audio, a transcript, chapter markers, social clips, a title, a description, and all the metadata that helps people find it — this episode is for you.

Maya Chen advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems. In this conversation she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why handoffs are the first place small teams break down, why a raw transcript is a rich input but not editorial truth, and where automation genuinely helps versus where it creates false confidence.

Key takeaways:
- Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
- Separate raw transcript from cleaned transcript — both have a role
- Create a publish checklist with defined handoff points
- Define a required output bundle for every episode (audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, publish log)
- Keep a lightweight episode log to surface where delays and errors repeat
- Use automation to accelerate drafts, not to replace approvals

Quote from the episode:
> "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." — Maya Chen
