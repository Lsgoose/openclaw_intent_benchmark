# Episode 14 Description

A great podcast is not just a great conversation—it is a reliable production system. In this episode, Maya Chen joins the show to break down how small teams can build a podcast production pipeline that turns raw recordings into polished releases without relying on memory, heroics, or messy handoffs.

The conversation covers the five core stages of production, the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps, where human review still matters, and the highest-value checks to make before an episode goes live. It also digs into title strategy, metadata consistency, and why standardizing your output bundle makes every release easier.

If a show has ever felt harder to publish than it should, this episode offers a practical blueprint for making the process more dependable.
