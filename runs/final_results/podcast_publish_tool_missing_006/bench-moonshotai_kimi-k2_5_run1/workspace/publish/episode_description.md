Maya Chen joins the show to unpack how a reliable podcast production pipeline shapes listener experience—from raw recording to polished release.

We break down the five stages every episode moves through (capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution), why small teams struggle with handoffs and inconsistent naming, and why raw transcripts are a starting point—not a source of truth.

Maya shares practical advice for teams of two or three: standardize your folder structure, define a fixed output bundle, and treat automation as acceleration, not abdication. We also cover title generation strategies, the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and how to build consistency without elaborate systems.

If you edit, publish, or manage content ops, this episode is for you.
