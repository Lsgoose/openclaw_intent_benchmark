# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Maya Chen joins the show to unpack how small teams can build dependable systems for turning raw recordings into polished episodes. The conversation covers the five stages of production—from capture through distribution—and identifies where teams typically struggle: handoffs without clear ownership, inconsistent file naming, and treating transcripts as finished content rather than raw material.

Maya emphasizes separating *raw* and *clean* transcripts, using automation to accelerate repetitive tasks while keeping humans responsible for editorial judgment, and treating titles as contracts with listeners rather than mere labels. The episode includes a practical five-point checklist for pre-launch review and actionable steps small shows can implement immediately to reduce friction and errors.

Also discussed: why fully automated systems fail on edge cases, how to generate better title candidates by framing from multiple angles, and the importance of metadata consistency across all surfaces.
