# Episode 14 — The Podcast Production Pipeline

Maya Chen joins the show to break down the podcast production pipeline — the often-invisible system that turns a raw recording into a polished, published episode. She outlines five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

Maya explains where small teams struggle most: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready. She recommends keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, using the cleaned version as the source for summaries and repurposing while preserving the raw version for traceability.

The conversation covers what to automate (drafts, formatting, metadata prefill) and what to keep human (editorial judgment, exception handling). Maya shares a title strategy that generates candidates from multiple angles, a five-point pre-publish review checklist, and five practical steps any small team can implement this month to build a more reliable pipeline.
