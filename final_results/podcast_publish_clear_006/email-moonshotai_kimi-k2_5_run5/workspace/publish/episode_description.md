# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to break down what makes a podcast production pipeline reliable—from raw recording to published episode. We walk through the five stages every show passes through (capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution) and identify where small teams typically get stuck: handoffs without source of truth, inconsistent file naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready copy.

Maya explains the critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, why automation should accelerate rather than replace human judgment, and how to treat titles as contracts with listeners. You'll come away with a practical five-step checklist to tighten your own pipeline this month, plus a framework for using AI tools responsibly in content workflows.
