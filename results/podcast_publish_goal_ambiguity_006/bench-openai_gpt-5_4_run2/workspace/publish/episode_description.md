# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to break down what actually makes a podcast production pipeline reliable. The conversation covers the five core stages of production, where small teams usually run into trouble, and why transcripts should be treated as strong source material for drafts rather than final editorial truth. Maya explains the value of separating raw and cleaned transcripts, defining a fixed output bundle for every episode, and creating review steps that catch name errors, broken links, sponsor issues, sensitive edits, and metadata drift before release. The episode also explores where automation helps most, where human judgment still matters, and how better systems improve not just internal operations but the listener experience itself. It’s a practical guide for any team trying to move from ad hoc publishing to a repeatable, trustworthy release process.
