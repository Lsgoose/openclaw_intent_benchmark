# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Show Notes

### [00:00:00] Introduction
Welcome and episode overview — why the production pipeline shapes the listener experience as much as the conversation itself.

### [00:00:27] Guest Introduction
Maya Chen joins the show. She advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems.
- [Maya Chen's profile](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen)

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Production Pipeline
Maya breaks down the pipeline into five stages:
1. **Capture** — recording and collecting companion materials
2. **Post-production** — audio cleanup, trimming, editorial decisions
3. **Content extraction** — transcripts, summaries, show notes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. **Review and approval** — fact-checking, sponsor language, names, links
5. **Distribution** — publishing to hosting, newsletter, social, and archives

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle
Common pain points: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content.

### [00:03:03] Transcripts Are Not Automatically Content
Why raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified claims — and why they should be treated as rich inputs rather than final editorial truth.

### [00:04:15] Building a Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical advice: one folder per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, publish log).

### [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
Keep both — raw for traceability, clean for downstream summarization and repurposing. Separating them prevents noise from propagating through the pipeline.

### [00:05:49] Automation and Its Limits
Where teams overestimate automation: editorial judgment and exception handling. The healthy mindset is using automation for acceleration, not abdication.
- [Podcast release checklist](https://creators.example.com/checklists/podcast-release)

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Generate multiple title candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) and compare against your audience promise.

### [00:08:13] Pre-Publish Review Checklist
Five highest-value checks: names/affiliations, links/references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency across all surfaces.

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with clear ownership
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and errors

Bonus: If experimenting with automation, start by automating drafts, not approvals.
