# Episode 14 Release Package

## Episode
- **Number:** 14
- **Title:** Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Summary
A practical conversation on how small teams can turn raw recordings into reliable, high-quality episode releases. The episode breaks the production pipeline into capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then focuses on where small teams usually struggle: handoffs, inconsistent asset naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts. The core theme is that production discipline shapes listener experience, and that automation should accelerate repetitive draft work without replacing human editorial ownership.

## Key Takeaways
- A podcast production pipeline covers capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.
- The pipeline becomes fragile when it depends on memory, heroics, or unclear handoffs.
- Small teams benefit from one workspace per episode, clear naming conventions, a defined checklist, and a fixed output bundle.
- Raw and cleaned transcripts should be kept separately: raw for traceability, clean for summarization and repurposing.
- Transcripts are strong inputs for drafts, but not final editorial truth.
- Review should prioritize names and affiliations, verified links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.
- Automation works best for repetitive transforms and first drafts, not for final approvals.

## Notable Quotes
- “The pipeline is the product.”
- “The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth.”
- “Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms... But don’t give up responsibility for what goes live.”
- “A title is not just a label. It’s a contract with the listener.”
