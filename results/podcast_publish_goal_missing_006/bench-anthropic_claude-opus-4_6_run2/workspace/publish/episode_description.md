# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline — Episode Description

A raw recording is only the beginning. In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen break down the five stages every podcast passes through before it reaches listeners: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya explains why small teams struggle most with invisible handoffs and the false assumption that a transcript equals publish-ready content. The conversation covers how to build a lightweight but reliable pipeline — standardized folders, a fixed output bundle, separated raw and clean transcripts, and an owned publish checklist. They also discuss where automation helps (drafting summaries, suggesting chapters) and where human judgment remains essential (editorial positioning, exception handling, sponsor compliance). The episode closes with five concrete actions any small show can implement this month to reduce errors and ship more consistently.
