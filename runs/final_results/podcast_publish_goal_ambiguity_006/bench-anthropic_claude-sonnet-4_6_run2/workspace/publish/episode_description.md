# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline surrounding it matters just as much — and breaks in ways that are easy to miss until the damage is done.

In this episode, Maya walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams typically struggle first — handoffs without a single source of truth, inconsistent file naming, and the mistaken assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready content.

Maya draws a sharp distinction between what automation handles well (transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, metadata prefill) and where human judgment is irreplaceable (editorial positioning, exception handling, and final release quality). She also covers what makes a title work, why metadata consistency across all surfaces matters, and the five pre-publish checks that catch the most costly mistakes.

If you run a small show or manage content ops for one, this is a practical guide to building a pipeline that holds under pressure.
