# Episode 14 — Launch Posts

---

## Post 1 — Key Takeaway

Episode 14 is out. One line from this week stuck with us: "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." Raw transcripts carry filler, false starts, and unverified references. Treating them as publish-ready copy is how low-quality show notes, broken quote cards, and misleading clips happen. The fix is simple — keep a raw transcript for traceability and produce a separate clean version as the source for everything downstream. Small discipline, big difference in output quality.

## Post 2 — Discussion Question

Episode 14 is live, and it raises a question worth sitting with: where in your production workflow do you still rely on memory or heroics instead of a documented process? The pipeline stages that feel "obvious" are usually the ones with no checklist, no named owner, and no standard output. What's the one handoff in your show's process that would cause the most chaos if the person who owns it was suddenly unavailable?
