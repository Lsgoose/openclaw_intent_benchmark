# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems operator advising creator businesses on production workflows

## Overview

A conversation about the invisible system behind every polished episode. Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline, where small teams stumble, and how to separate raw transcripts from publish-ready content.

## Key Topics

- **Five pipeline stages:** capture → post-production → content extraction → review & approval → distribution
- **Common pain points:** handoff gaps, inconsistent file naming, treating raw transcripts as editorial truth
- **Raw vs. cleaned transcripts:** keep both; the cleaned version drives summaries and repurposing, the raw version preserves traceability
- **Automation boundaries:** automate repetitive transforms (transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, formatting), but keep a human in the loop for editorial judgment and exception handling
- **Title strategy:** generate candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) — frequency is not strategy
- **Pre-publish checklist:** names & affiliations, links & references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency across all surfaces

## Notable Quotes

## Practical Takeaways

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot recurring delays or errors
6. Automate drafts, not approvals
