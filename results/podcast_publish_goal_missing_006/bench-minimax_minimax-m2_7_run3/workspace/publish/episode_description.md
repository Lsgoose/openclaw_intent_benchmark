# Episode Description — Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media production systems advisor

A podcast episode is more than a conversation. It is an edited, packaged, labeled, and distributed product — and the pipeline that builds it shapes the entire listener experience.

In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why raw transcripts are rich inputs but not final editorial truth, how to design a lightweight but consistent workflow for small teams, and where automation can accelerate without replacing human judgment.

Maya shares the five highest-value pre-release checks — from guest names to link verification to sponsor compliance — and makes a case for treating titles as audience promises rather than mechanical label strings. The episode closes with a practical checklist any small show can implement this month, and a reminder: make the machine produce options, make the human approve what goes live.

Whether you're running a solo show or a three-person team, this episode is a guide to production discipline that actually scales.
