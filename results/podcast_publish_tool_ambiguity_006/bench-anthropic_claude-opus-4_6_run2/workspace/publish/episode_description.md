# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen unpack the systems that turn a raw recording into a polished, publishable episode. Maya breaks podcast production into five stages — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why small teams fail at handoffs, file naming, and treating transcripts as finished content.

The conversation covers practical pipeline design for two- or three-person teams: standardized episode folders, fixed output bundles, separated raw and clean transcripts, and a publish checklist with clear ownership. Maya and the host also discuss where automation helps (drafting notes, suggesting titles, formatting metadata) and where human judgment remains essential (editorial tone, legal review, exception handling).

Key takeaway: automate drafts, not approvals. Let tools produce options; let humans own what goes live.
