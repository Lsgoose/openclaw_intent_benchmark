In this episode, we unpack what it actually takes to build a reliable podcast production pipeline that scales beyond memory and heroics.

Maya Chen, advisor to media teams and creator businesses, joins us to break down the five core stages of production: capture, post‑production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. We talk about why a consistent output bundle (from final audio and clean transcripts to titles, chapter lists, and publish logs) can transform both listener experience and team sanity.

You’ll hear how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation and agents really help, and where human editorial judgment is still essential. We also cover practical review checks for names, links, sponsors, and metadata alignment, plus concrete first steps you can take this month to tighten your own pipeline.

If you run or support a small show and want fewer last‑minute scrambles, this episode will help you turn your production process into a dependable system rather than a recurring fire drill.
