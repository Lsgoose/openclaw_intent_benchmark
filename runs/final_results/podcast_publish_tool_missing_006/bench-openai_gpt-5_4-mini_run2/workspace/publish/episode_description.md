Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. Host and guest Maya Chen break down the major stages of capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then zoom in on the places small teams usually break down: handoffs, naming chaos, transcript cleanup, metadata drift, and inconsistent publishing checks. The conversation makes a clear case for keeping raw and cleaned transcripts separate, using automation for drafts rather than approvals, and treating titles, descriptions, chapter markers, and links as part of one cohesive release system. If you care about making podcast production more consistent, scalable, and trustworthy, this episode is a practical blueprint.
