In this episode, we break down how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns raw recordings into a polished release. Maya Chen explains the five core stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation covers why transcripts are powerful but not final truth, how small teams can standardize folders, naming, and output bundles, and why automation should speed up drafts without replacing human approval. It also explores title strategy, metadata consistency, and the checks that keep episodes accurate, clear, and ready for publish.
