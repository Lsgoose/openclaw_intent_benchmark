Episode 14 looks at the production pipeline behind a reliable podcast release, from capture and post-production to transcription, review, and distribution. Maya Chen explains why the pipeline itself is part of the product, how small teams get tripped up by handoffs and sloppy file practices, and why a cleaned transcript should be treated as a working asset rather than final truth. The conversation covers practical safeguards like fixed output bundles, consistent naming, metadata checks, and keeping raw and cleaned transcripts separate. It also explores where automation helps, where it fails, and why the best systems automate drafts but keep humans responsible for approvals. A useful episode for creators, editors, and content ops teams looking to make their release process more dependable.
