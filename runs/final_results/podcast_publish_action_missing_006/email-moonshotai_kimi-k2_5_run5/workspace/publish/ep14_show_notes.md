# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen  
**Topic:** Production systems for podcast teams

## Episode Summary

Maya Chen joins us to unpack how podcast production pipelines actually work—and why they matter more than most creators realize. We walk through the five stages of a reliable pipeline, from capture to distribution, and explore where small teams typically struggle.

Key topics covered:
- The five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review/approval, and distribution
- Why handoffs are often the first failure point in small teams
- Separating raw transcripts from cleaned transcripts for downstream quality
- Where automation helps (draft generation) vs. where humans must own decisions (editorial judgment)
- A practical checklist for improving your pipeline this month

Maya's core insight: *"The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."*

## Key Takeaways

1. **Define a standard episode structure** — One folder per episode, clear naming conventions
2. **Create a publish checklist** with a single owner
3. **Separate raw and cleaned transcripts** — Raw for traceability, cleaned for downstream use
4. **Define a standard output bundle** — Final audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters
5. **Automate drafts, not approvals** — Let machines generate options; humans approve what goes public
