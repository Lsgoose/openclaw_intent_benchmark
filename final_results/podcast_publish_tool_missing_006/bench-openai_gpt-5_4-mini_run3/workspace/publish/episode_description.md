Episode 14 looks at how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline, from capture and post-production to transcript cleanup, review, and distribution. Maya Chen explains why the pipeline is the product: when handoffs, naming, transcript quality, and metadata management break down, the listener experience breaks down with them. The discussion covers clean versus raw transcripts, the limits of automation, how to generate better title options, and the highest-value checks before an episode goes live. The episode closes with practical steps small teams can use immediately: standardize folders, tighten checklists, separate source materials, and make automation draft the work while humans approve what gets published.
