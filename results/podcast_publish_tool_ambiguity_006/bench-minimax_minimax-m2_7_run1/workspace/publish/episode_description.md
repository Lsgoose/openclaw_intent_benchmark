# Episode 14 Description

What does it actually take to ship a podcast episode reliably — week after week, without things falling through the cracks? This episode unpacks the production pipeline from first recording to final distribution, breaking it into five concrete stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya Chen walks through the most common failure points small teams hit, why a raw transcript is never the final source of truth, and how to build a lightweight pipeline that actually holds up under real production pressure. Topics include transcript management (raw vs. cleaned), the editorial judgment that still belongs to humans, title strategy, the pre-publish checklist, and a practical roadmap for improving your pipeline starting this month. Whether you are a solo creator or running a team of three, this episode is about building systems that make consistency automatic.
