# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline (with Maya Chen)

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. But the pipeline that turns a raw recording into a published episode — with clean audio, a transcript, chapter markers, show notes, titles, and social copy — is what actually shapes the listener experience.

In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why raw transcripts are rich inputs but not editorial truth, how small teams can build a lightweight but consistent workflow, and where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable.

Maya also covers the highest-value pre-publish checks, how to think about title strategy, and why metadata consistency across all surfaces matters more than most teams realize.

If you run a small show and want a more reliable, repeatable production process, this episode gives you a practical place to start.
