# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems advisor to creator businesses and small media teams

---

## Episode Summary

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product. In this episode, she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains why small teams that skip production discipline end up with compounding, invisible errors.

The conversation covers the difference between raw and clean transcripts, where automation genuinely helps versus where it creates false confidence, how to write titles that function as a contract with the listener, and the five highest-value checks before any episode goes live. The episode closes with a practical five-step improvement plan any small show can act on this month.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **The pipeline is the product.** Clean audio is necessary but not sufficient — titles, transcripts, chapter markers, metadata, and social copy all shape the listener experience.
- **Five pipeline stages:** Capture → Post-production → Content extraction → Review & approval → Distribution.
- **Raw vs. clean transcript.** Keep both. The raw transcript is for traceability; the cleaned transcript is the preferred source for summaries, quotes, and repurposing.
- **Transcripts are rich inputs, not final truth.** Facts, links, names, affiliations, and sponsor language still require human review before publication.
- **Automate drafts, not approvals.** Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms; keep a human responsible for what goes live.
- **Titles are a contract.** Generate candidates from multiple angles — problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven — then choose based on audience intent, not keyword frequency.
- **Five pre-publish checks:** names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency.
- **Standard output bundle:** final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log — every episode, every time.
