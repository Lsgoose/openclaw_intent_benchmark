Production pipelines shape the entire listener experience, yet most teams only notice them when something breaks. In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. She explains why raw transcripts are inputs—not editorial truth—and how small teams can build lightweight systems using consistent naming, defined handoffs, and standard output bundles. The discussion covers where automation accelerates workflows versus where human judgment remains essential, why titles are contracts with your audience, and the five high-value checks that prevent embarrassing mistakes. If you produce podcasts with a small team, this episode provides a practical framework for making your pipeline more consistent without adding bureaucracy.
