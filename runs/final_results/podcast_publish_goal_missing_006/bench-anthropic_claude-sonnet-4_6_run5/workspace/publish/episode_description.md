# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy. They cover what automation handles well (first drafts, formatting, asset packaging) versus what still demands human judgment (editorial calls, exception handling, anything that goes public). The episode closes with a practical six-step checklist any small show can act on immediately.

If you own editing, publishing, or content ops for a podcast, this episode is for you.
