# Launch Posts — Episode 14

## Post 1 — Key Takeaway

A raw transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth. Reliable pipelines separate raw and cleaned transcripts, automate drafts, and keep humans in the loop for public-facing decisions. Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what goes live.

## Post 2 — Discussion Question

What's the one step in your podcast production pipeline that consistently causes delays or errors — and what would it take to fix it? Is it handoffs between team members, inconsistent file naming, or something else entirely?
