# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to unpack the podcast production pipeline — the often-invisible system that turns a raw recording into a polished, discoverable episode. She breaks the process into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

Small teams typically struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content. Maya argues that transcripts are rich inputs for first drafts but still require human review for facts, links, sponsor language, and names. She recommends a lightweight but reliable setup: standardized episode folders, clear naming conventions, a publish checklist, and a consistent output bundle for every release.

The conversation also covers where automation helps versus where it falls short, title strategy as a listener contract, and the five highest-value pre-publish checks. The key takeaway: automate drafts, not approvals.
