# Show Notes

## 00:00:00 — Opening: why the pipeline is the product
The episode frames podcast production as more than recording: the pipeline shapes the listener experience from raw audio to publish-ready assets.

## 00:01:20 — The five pipeline stages
Capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

## 00:02:29 — Common small-team failure points
Hand-offs, inconsistent naming/storage, and the false assumption that a transcript is already publish-ready content.

## 00:03:40 — What transcripts can and cannot do
Use transcripts as rich inputs for summaries, chapter ideas, quotes, and social drafts, but verify facts, links, sponsor language, names, affiliations, dates, and claims.

## 00:04:26 — Lightweight pipeline design
Keep one folder per episode, a clear naming convention, a defined checklist, and a fixed output bundle.

## 00:04:59 — Raw transcript vs. cleaned transcript
Keep both: raw for traceability, cleaned for readability and repurposing.

## 00:05:57 — Automation boundaries
Automate repetitive transforms, but keep humans responsible for editorial judgment, exception handling, correctness, tone, and release quality.

## 00:06:57 — Title strategy
Generate multiple title angles and choose the one that best matches listener intent; frequency is not strategy.

## 00:08:20 — Pre-publish review checks
Verify names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy.

## 00:09:13 — Practical takeaways for small shows
Define folder structure and naming, assign one publish checklist owner, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, standardize the output bundle, and keep a lightweight episode log.
