# Episode Description

Episode 14 breaks down the podcast production pipeline — from raw recording to published episode. Guest Maya Chen, who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems, maps the pipeline into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

The conversation covers where small teams struggle first — handoffs without a single source of truth, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready. Maya argues that transcripts are rich inputs for first drafts but never the final source of editorial truth. Facts, links, names, and claims still need human review.

For teams of two or three, the recommendation is simple: one folder per episode, a clear naming convention, a publish checklist, and a fixed output bundle. Keep both raw and cleaned transcripts. Automate repetitive transforms like transcription cleanup and metadata prefill, but never automate approvals.

The episode also covers title strategy — generate candidates from multiple angles and remember that frequency is not strategy — and a five-point pre-publish review checklist covering names, links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.
