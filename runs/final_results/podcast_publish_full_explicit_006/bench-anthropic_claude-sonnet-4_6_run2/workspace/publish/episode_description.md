# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the conversation — it's the production pipeline behind it. In this episode, host and media systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the mistaken belief that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content. She makes the case for keeping both a raw and a cleaned transcript, standardizing an output bundle for every episode, and treating automation as a tool for accelerating drafts — not replacing editorial judgment.

The conversation covers title strategy, pre-publish review checklists, and the edge cases that fully automated systems routinely miss. The core message: consistency and clear ownership matter more than elaborate tooling.

Whether you run a two-person show or are scaling a media operation, this episode offers a practical framework for building a pipeline that holds up under pressure.
