# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to break down what makes a podcast production pipeline reliable — and why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience. The conversation maps the full workflow from capture and post-production through transcript cleanup, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Along the way, Maya explains where small teams usually struggle: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, weak source-of-truth discipline, and the mistaken belief that a raw transcript is ready to become public-facing content. She makes the case for keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, defining a standard output bundle for every episode, and treating automation as a draft accelerator rather than a substitute for editorial judgment.

The episode also covers title strategy, metadata consistency, final pre-publish checks, and a practical five-step plan for improving a production workflow without building an overly complicated system.
