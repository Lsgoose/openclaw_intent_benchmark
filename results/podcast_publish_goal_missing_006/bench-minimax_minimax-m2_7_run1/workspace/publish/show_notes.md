# Show Notes — Episode 15: Title Tests

## [00:00:00] Opening
Today we're talking about why title tests fail when teams confuse click-through rate with audience fit.

## [00:00:18] Guest Introduction
Joining me is Jordan Kim, who helps podcast networks run title experiments and packaging reviews.

## [00:00:34] Core Insight — The Title Test Matrix
A title test matrix is useful only if each option maps to a real audience promise. Otherwise you're just measuring random novelty.

## [00:00:58] The Problem — CTR vs. Listener Intent
So title testing is not a slot machine.
Teams over-index on thumbnail packaging, click-through rate, and novelty, then forget listener intent.

## [00:01:33] What to Track Instead
Keep a test log: the hypothesis, the audience segment, the winning frame, and why it won.

## [00:02:04] Healthy Workflow
One shared title board, a short review loop, and a final approved headline before distribution. The lesson is not "test everything forever." It's "learn which promise actually matches the episode."

## [00:02:36] Practical Mistake
People run title tests without recording the context. Then a winning headline gets reused in the wrong place and performance drops.

## [00:03:05] Action for Small Teams This Month
Start a simple experiment log, write down the audience promise for each option, and stop treating click-through rate as the whole strategy.
