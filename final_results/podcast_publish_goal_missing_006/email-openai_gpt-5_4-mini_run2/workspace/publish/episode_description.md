This episode explores why the podcast production pipeline is the real product behind a polished show. The conversation walks through the five core stages of production, from capture and post-production to content extraction, review, and distribution. It also explains why raw transcripts are useful but never authoritative on their own, and why clean transcripts, verified links, and consistent metadata matter so much for release quality. The guest shares a practical framework for small teams: standardize folder structure, define a fixed output bundle, separate drafts from approvals, and treat automation as a way to accelerate routine work rather than replace editorial judgment. A strong episode for anyone building a reliable publishing workflow.
