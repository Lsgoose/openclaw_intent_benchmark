# Episode Description — Episode 14: Podcast Production Pipeline

Most listeners never see the work behind the episodes they love. In this episode, producer and media systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the podcast production pipeline: the five stages from raw capture to published episode, and where small teams get tripped up most often.

Maya explains why treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy creates downstream problems, why handoffs without a single source of truth quietly erode quality, and why automation is most effective as an accelerant for drafts — not a replacement for human approval on what goes public. She shares practical guidance on structuring a lightweight pipeline for a two- or three-person team, including standardized output bundles, naming conventions, and the five pre-publish checks that matter most: names, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.

A standout moment: her framing that a transcript is a "rich input, not final editorial truth" — a principle that changes how you approach AI-assisted content generation. The episode closes with concrete first steps for improving your pipeline this month, and a closing thought worth bookmarking: make the machine produce options; make the human approve what becomes public.
