In this episode, we break down what it really takes to build a reliable podcast production pipeline, from raw recording to a polished release. Consultant Maya Chen joins to map out the five key stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

We dig into where small teams usually struggle—messy handoffs, chaotic file naming, and treating raw transcripts as if they were ready-to-publish copy. Maya explains why a transcript should be a rich input, not editorial truth, and how separating raw and cleaned transcripts improves everything you build on top of them.

You’ll hear practical advice on designing a lightweight but consistent workflow, standardizing your episode output bundle, and using automation for acceleration rather than abdication. We also cover how to approach titles as a contract with the listener and the five highest-value checks before you hit publish.

If you want fewer last-minute edits and more confidence in every episode you ship, this conversation will help you tighten up your pipeline.
