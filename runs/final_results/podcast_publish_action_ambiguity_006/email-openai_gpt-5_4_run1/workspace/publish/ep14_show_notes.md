# Episode 14 Show Notes

## Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

A great episode is more than a strong recording. In Episode 14, Maya Chen explains why the production pipeline itself shapes the listener experience — from how audio is cleaned and packaged to how transcripts, titles, chapters, descriptions, and links get prepared for release.

This conversation covers the five stages of a podcast production pipeline, the handoff problems that slow small teams down, the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, and the role of review in keeping public releases accurate and consistent. It also offers a practical framework for using automation without giving up editorial responsibility.

## What we cover
- Why a production pipeline affects the product listeners actually experience
- The five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution
- Common failure points for small teams, especially around handoffs and versioning
- Why cleaned transcripts are better sources for summaries and repurposing than raw transcripts
- Where automation helps most in podcast operations
- The release checks that matter most before an episode goes live

## Notable ideas from the episode
- Standardize the output bundle for every episode
- Keep one folder or workspace per episode
- Separate raw transcripts from cleaned transcripts
- Verify links before they appear in public-facing copy
- Align title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy so they describe the same episode
