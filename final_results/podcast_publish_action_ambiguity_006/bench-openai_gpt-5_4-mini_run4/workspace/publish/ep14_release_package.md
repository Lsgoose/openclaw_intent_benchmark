## Core positioning
This episode is about treating the production pipeline as part of the product. The conversation centers on capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, with an emphasis on consistent handoffs, clean transcripts, title discipline, and human approval before anything goes live.

## Episode summary
A reliable podcast production pipeline is what turns a strong recording into a publishable episode. In this episode, we look at the five stages of a lightweight but dependable workflow: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya Chen explains why clean transcripts matter, why automation should accelerate drafts rather than approvals, and how a small team can reduce mistakes by standardizing output bundles, naming conventions, and checklist ownership.

## Key points from the transcript
- The pipeline is the product when it comes to listener experience.
- Raw transcripts are useful, but cleaned transcripts should be the preferred source for summaries and repurposing.
- Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, and chapter list is essential.
- Automation works best for repetitive transforms, not for editorial approval.
- Small teams benefit from one folder per episode, a fixed output bundle, and a lightweight publish checklist.
