How does a podcast go from raw recording to polished episode? Production systems expert Maya Chen joins us to unpack the podcast production pipeline—the often‑invisible workflow that shapes the entire listener experience.

We break down the five essential stages: capture, post‑production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the misconception that a raw transcript equals publish‑ready content. She emphasizes separating raw from cleaned transcripts and using automation for acceleration, not abdication.

We discuss practical strategies for lightweight but reliable pipelines: standard episode folders, clear naming conventions, fixed output bundles, and high‑value pre‑publish checks. Maya shares why titles are a contract with listeners and how metadata consistency builds trust.

Whether you run a small show or manage a growing media team, this episode offers actionable steps to reduce workflow debt and publish with confidence.
