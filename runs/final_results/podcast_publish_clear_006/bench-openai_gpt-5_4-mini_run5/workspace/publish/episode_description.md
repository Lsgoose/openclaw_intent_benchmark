Episode 14 explores how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline that turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen outlines the five core stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The conversation digs into where small teams usually break down, especially handoffs, naming chaos, and the mistaken idea that a transcript is automatically publish-ready. It also covers the value of keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, using automation for drafts instead of approvals, and treating titles as a contract with the listener. The episode closes with a practical checklist for improving consistency: standardize episode folders, define a publish owner, log every release, and verify names, links, sponsor language, and metadata before going live.
