# Episode 14 – How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

In this episode, we talk with **Maya Chen**, who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems, about why your podcast production pipeline is just as important as the conversation itself.

From capture to post‑production, content extraction, review, and distribution, we walk step‑by‑step through the systems that make a show feel consistent and trustworthy.

## Episode Overview

**Total runtime:** ~10 minutes

We cover:

- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post‑production, content extraction, review/approval, and distribution
- Common pain points for small teams: handoffs, unclear ownership, messy file naming, and transcript misuse
- Why treating the transcript as clean truth leads to weak or misleading derivative content
- How to keep both raw and cleaned transcripts, and when to use each
- Where automation and agents help most (and where you still need human judgment)
- Simple title strategy for aligning what you promise with what you deliver
- High‑value pre‑publish checks: names, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency
- A lightweight blueprint for a repeatable small‑team pipeline
