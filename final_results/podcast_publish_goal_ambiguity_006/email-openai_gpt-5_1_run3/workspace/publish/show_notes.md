# Show Notes

## [00:00:00] Building a podcast production pipeline
- Why the podcast production pipeline shapes the listener experience
- Maya Chen joins to discuss production systems for media teams
- How titles, transcripts, chapters, and metadata affect discovery and quality

## [00:01:20] The five core stages of production
- Capture: recording plus collecting bios, sponsor copy, links, and references
- Post-production: cleaning audio, trimming mistakes, deciding what stays
- Content extraction: transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, titles, clips
- Review and approval: fact checks, links, sponsor language, and legal/sensitivity review
- Distribution: shipping the final package to feeds, newsletter, social, and archives

## [00:02:29] Common failure points for small teams
- Fragility when workflows rely on memory and individual heroics
- Painful handoffs and lack of a single source of truth
- Messy file naming and asset storage leading to confusion
- Why raw transcripts are not automatically publish-ready

## [00:03:34] Using transcripts as rich inputs, not final truth
- What transcripts are great for: first-draft summaries, chapters, quotes, social options
- Why authoritative facts, names, dates, and links still need human review
- "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth"

## [00:04:26] Designing a lightweight pipeline for small teams
- One folder or workspace per episode with clear naming conventions
- A checklist with defined handoff points and a fixed output bundle
- Standard outputs: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, and publish log

## [00:04:54] Raw vs. cleaned transcripts
- Keeping the raw transcript for traceability and internal reference
- Producing a cleaned transcript with fixed speaker labels and formatting
- How cleaned transcripts improve summarization and repurposing quality

## [00:05:49] Where automation and agents help (and don’t)
- Using automation for repetitive transforms and first drafts
- Why editorial judgment and exception handling should stay human-owned
- How edge cases and sensitive content break fully automated systems

## [00:06:52] Crafting effective episode titles
- Generating multiple title candidates from different angles
- Examples of problem-oriented, guest-oriented, and outcome-oriented titles
- Why frequency of phrases in the transcript is not a strategy for titles
- Treating titles as a contract with the listener

## [00:08:13] High-value pre-publish checks
- Verifying guest names and affiliations
- Checking all promised links and references
- Ensuring sponsor language is present and compliant
- Confirming requested sensitive edits are applied
- Keeping title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy consistent

## [00:09:13] Practical improvements you can make this month
- Define a standard folder structure and naming convention per episode
- Create a publish checklist with a clear owner
- Separate raw and cleaned transcripts in your workflow
- Standardize the output bundle for every episode
- Maintain a lightweight episode log to spot recurring issues
- Start by automating drafts, not approvals
