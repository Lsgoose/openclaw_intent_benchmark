Building a reliable podcast production pipeline means treating release work like a system, not a scramble. In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five core stages of podcast production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The conversation digs into common failure points for small teams, including messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the mistake of treating raw transcripts as final truth. It also covers how to use automation well, where human judgment still matters, why titles should be framed as listener promises, and the highest-value checks before publishing. The takeaway is simple: standardize the outputs, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, and automate drafts without automating approval.
