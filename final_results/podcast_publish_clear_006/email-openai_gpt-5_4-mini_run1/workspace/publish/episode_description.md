Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. The conversation covers the five core stages of production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya Chen explains why small teams run into trouble when handoffs are unclear, file naming drifts, or transcripts are treated as final copy instead of rich source material.

The episode also breaks down a practical workflow for small teams: keep raw and cleaned transcripts separate, standardize the required output bundle, and use a publish checklist to verify names, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency. The takeaway is simple: use automation to accelerate drafts and formatting, but keep humans responsible for editorial judgment and release quality.
