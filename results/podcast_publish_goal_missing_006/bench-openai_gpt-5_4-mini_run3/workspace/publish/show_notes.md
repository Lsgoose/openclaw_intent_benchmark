[00:00:00] Pipeline as the product
Today’s episode opens on the idea that the podcast pipeline shapes the listener experience just as much as the conversation itself.

[00:01:20] Five stages of production
Capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution form the core workflow.

[00:03:03] Transcript is not the final truth
A transcript is a rich input for drafts and repurposing, but facts, links, names, sponsor language, and claims still need human review.

[00:04:26] Standardize the output bundle
A reliable team workflow includes final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, and publish log.

[00:05:57] Automate drafts, not approvals
Automation can speed up cleanup, notes, chapter suggestions, and metadata prep, but public release still needs human ownership.

[00:06:52] Titles as a contract
Strong titles frame the episode around audience intent instead of simply repeating the most common phrase.

[00:08:13] Highest-value review checks
Before publish, verify names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.

[00:09:23] Practical takeaway for small teams
Set a folder structure, own the checklist, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, define the output bundle, and keep a lightweight episode log.
