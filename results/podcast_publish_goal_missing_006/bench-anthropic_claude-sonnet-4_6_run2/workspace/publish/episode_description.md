# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself shapes listener experience just as much as the conversation inside it.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready content. They cover how to build a lightweight but reliable system, where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and what a solid pre-publish review checklist looks like.

Key takeaway: use automation to accelerate drafts, not to replace approvals. The machine produces options; the human decides what goes public.
