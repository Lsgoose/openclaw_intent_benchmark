## Descriptions

### Short Description

A practical conversation with production systems advisor Maya Chen on how small teams can build a reliable podcast pipeline—from capture and editing to transcripts, checklists, and final publish.

### Long Description

In this episode, we look behind the scenes at the systems that shape every listener’s experience: your podcast production pipeline.

Host and guest Maya Chen break down the five key stages of a healthy pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and show how even small teams can make their process far more reliable with a few deliberate conventions.

Along the way, they cover why transcripts are powerful *inputs* but not automatically ready-to-publish copy, how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, where agents and automation can safely speed things up, and why titles should be treated as contracts with your listeners rather than afterthoughts.

You’ll come away with a concrete upgrade plan: a clearer folder structure, a simple publish checklist, a standard output bundle for each episode, and a lightweight episode log to track where your pipeline breaks.
