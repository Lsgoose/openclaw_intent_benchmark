Maya Chen joins the show to unpack how podcast production pipelines shape listener experience. Most teams treat production as invisible infrastructure—until something breaks. We walk through five pipeline stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya explains why raw transcripts are inputs, not sources of truth, and where automation helps (repetitive tasks) versus where it fails (editorial judgment and edge cases). We cover lightweight systems for small teams: standard folder structures, defined output bundles, and separation of raw and cleaned materials. The conversation also explores how titles function as audience contracts, why consistency beats complexity, and what belongs on a pre-publish checklist. If you produce, edit, or manage podcast operations, this episode offers a practical framework for making your pipeline more reliable without adding overhead.
