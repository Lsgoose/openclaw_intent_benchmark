## Release summary
This episode explains how a podcast production pipeline shapes the listener experience from raw recording to publish. The core message: the pipeline is the product, and reliability comes from standardization, review, and clear ownership.

## Key talking points
- The pipeline includes capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.
- Clean transcripts should be separated from raw transcripts for traceability and repurposing.
- Small teams benefit from one folder per episode, consistent naming, and a fixed output bundle.
- Automation should accelerate drafts and formatting, not replace approval or judgment.
- Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy is essential.

## Notable quotes
- "the pipeline is the product"
- "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."
- "Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms... But don’t give up responsibility for what goes live."
- "Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what becomes public."
