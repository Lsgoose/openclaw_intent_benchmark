# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates amateur podcasts from professional productions isn't just better microphones—it's the pipeline. In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down how raw recordings transform into polished, discoverable episodes through five critical stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

We explore why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the misconception that transcripts equal publish-ready content. Maya shares practical frameworks for building lightweight but reliable systems, including the essential distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, and why automation should accelerate—not replace—human editorial judgment.

From generating title candidates that actually convert to the five-check review process every episode needs before going live, this conversation is essential listening for anyone who owns editing, publishing, or content operations. Whether you're a team of one or ten, the pipeline you build determines the product your audience experiences.
