## Core episode framing
This episode explains how a podcast moves from raw recording to a polished, publishable release. The guest emphasizes that the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience and that reliability comes from consistent stages, clear handoffs, and disciplined review.

## Key takeaways from the transcript
- The pipeline has five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.
- The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth.
- Clean transcript and raw transcript should both exist, with the cleaned version preferred for repurposing.
- Automation should accelerate repetitive work, not replace approval.
- Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapter list, and social copy is essential.
- Small teams benefit from standard folder structures, naming conventions, a publish checklist, and a fixed output bundle.

## Suggested episode summary
