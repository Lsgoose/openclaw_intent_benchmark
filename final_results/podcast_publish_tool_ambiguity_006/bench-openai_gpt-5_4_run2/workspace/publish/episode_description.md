# Episode Description

A great podcast is not just a great conversation. It is the result of a reliable production pipeline.

In Episode 14, Maya Chen joins the show to break down the systems that turn a raw recording into a polished release. The conversation covers the five stages of a podcast production pipeline, why small teams struggle with handoffs and inconsistent assets, and why transcripts should be treated as rich inputs rather than final editorial truth.

The episode also explores where automation helps most, where human review still matters, how to think about title generation, and which pre-publish checks create the biggest quality gains.

If a show depends too much on memory, heroics, or scattered files, mistakes stack up fast. This episode offers a practical framework for building a lighter, cleaner, and more dependable process.
