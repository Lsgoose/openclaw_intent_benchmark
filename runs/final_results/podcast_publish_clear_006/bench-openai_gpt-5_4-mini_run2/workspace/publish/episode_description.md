Episode 14 looks at how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a complete, publish-ready release. Host and guest Maya Chen break the workflow into capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution, then dig into the practical problems that trip up small teams: handoffs, naming chaos, transcript cleanup, title choice, and metadata consistency.

The episode makes a clear case for keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, using automation for drafts and repetitive transforms, and reserving human judgment for facts, links, sponsor language, and final approval. It closes with a simple operating model for small shows: standardize the folder structure, own the checklist, define the output bundle, and keep a lightweight episode log.
