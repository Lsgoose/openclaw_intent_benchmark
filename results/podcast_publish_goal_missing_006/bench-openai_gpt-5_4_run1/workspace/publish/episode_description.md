# Episode Description

Why do so many title tests produce misleading wins? This episode breaks down the difference between chasing click-through rate and finding real audience fit. Host and guest Jordan Kim explore why title options need to reflect a clear audience promise, how novelty can distort test results, and why listener intent matters more than surface metrics alone.

The conversation also covers the operating system behind better headline decisions: keeping a test log, documenting the audience segment and hypothesis, running a short review loop, and approving a final headline before distribution. Along the way, they unpack a common mistake—reusing a winning title without preserving the original context.

A practical episode for podcast teams, creators, and marketers trying to improve packaging without turning title testing into guesswork.
