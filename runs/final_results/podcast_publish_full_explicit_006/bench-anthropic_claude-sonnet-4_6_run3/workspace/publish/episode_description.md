# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, production systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the common mistake of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready content. They cover how to structure a lightweight but reliable workflow, what automation can and can't do well, how to write titles that serve the audience rather than just label the content, and the five pre-publish checks that prevent the most common release errors.

The core message: consistency beats complexity. A standard output bundle, a clear checklist, and a human in the approval seat will take a small show further than any elaborate automated system.
