# Episode 15: Why Title Tests Fail

## Timestamps

**[00:00:00]** Introduction: Why title tests fail when teams confuse click-through rate with audience fit.

**[00:00:18]** Guest introduction: Jordan Kim, who helps podcast networks run title experiments and packaging reviews.

**[00:00:34]** Core insight: A title test matrix is useful only if each option maps to a real audience promise. Otherwise you're just measuring random novelty.

**[00:00:58]** Title testing is not a slot machine.

**[00:01:04]** Common mistake: Teams over-index on thumbnail packaging, click-through rate, and novelty, then forget listener intent.

**[00:01:33]** What should teams track instead?

**[00:01:39]** Solution: Keep a test log with the hypothesis, audience segment, winning frame, and why it won.

**[00:02:04]** What does a healthy workflow look like?

**[00:02:09]** Healthy workflow: One shared title board, a short review loop, and a final approved headline before distribution. The lesson is not "test everything forever" but "learn which promise actually matches the episode."

**[00:02:36]** Practical mistakes seen every week?

**[00:02:41]** Common error: People run title tests without recording the context. Then a winning headline gets reused in the wrong place and performance drops.

**[00:03:05]** What should a small team do this month?

**[00:03:10]** Actionable advice: Start a simple experiment log, write down the audience promise for each option, and stop treating click-through rate as the whole strategy.
