# Episode Description

In Episode 14, the show breaks down what makes a podcast production pipeline reliable from end to end. Host and guest Maya Chen walk through the five major stages of production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Along the way, they explain why small teams often struggle with handoffs, messy file naming, transcript misuse, and inconsistent publishing practices.

The conversation also explores the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation can genuinely help, and why humans still need to own editorial judgment, exception handling, and final approvals. Maya shares practical guidance on standardizing episode folders, defining a fixed output bundle, improving review workflows, and tightening metadata consistency across every public surface.

If the goal is to move from ad hoc publishing to a repeatable release system, this episode offers a clear blueprint for doing it without overcomplicating the process.
