Episode 14 explores what it really takes to turn a raw conversation into a polished, publish-ready podcast episode. Host and guest Maya Chen break down the five core stages of a reliable production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. They cover where small teams usually struggle, why transcripts are valuable but not editorial truth, how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, and where automation helps versus where human judgment still matters. The conversation also digs into title strategy, metadata consistency, review checklists, and the minimum output bundle every episode should produce. If a show feels inconsistent, the issue often is not the conversation itself but the system around it. This episode is a practical guide for creators and small media teams who want fewer release mistakes, smoother handoffs, and a production process they can trust every week.
