Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen breaks the workflow into capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then shows where small teams usually stumble: handoffs, naming, transcript cleanup, and inconsistent approval steps. The conversation highlights why raw and cleaned transcripts should stay separate, how automation can speed up drafting without replacing editorial judgment, and why titles and metadata need to tell the same story everywhere. The episode closes with practical advice for small shows: standardize folders, assign checklist ownership, define a required output bundle, and log recurring issues so the process gets better over time.
