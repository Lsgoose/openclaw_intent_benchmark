# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Guest
**Maya Chen** — Media systems advisor for creator businesses and media teams. [Profile](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen)

---

## Topics by Timestamp

### [00:00:00] Introduction: Why the Pipeline Is the Product
- The production pipeline shapes the entire listener experience
- From raw recording to polished episode: audio, transcript, chapters, clips, metadata

### [00:00:36] The Invisible System
- Production discipline matters even for small teams
- Bad titles = fewer clicks. Sloppy transcripts = worse downstream content. Bad chapters = navigation fails.

### [00:01:27] The Five Stages of a Production Pipeline
1. **Capture** — recording + companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. **Post-production** — audio cleanup, editing decisions
3. **Content extraction** — transcripts, summaries, quotes, chapters, titles, clips
4. **Review and approval** — fact-checks, sponsor language, legal review
5. **Distribution** — hosting, newsletter, social, archives

### [00:02:33] Where Small Teams Struggle
- Handoffs without a single source of truth
- Inconsistent naming: "final_v2_real_final.wav"
- Transcript-driven tasks look easy but require human judgment

### [00:03:09] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
- Raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, jokes that don't read, unverified "we'll link that" references
- Transcripts are **rich input, not final editorial truth**

### [00:04:26] A Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming convention
- Checklist with defined handoff points
- Fixed output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log

### [00:04:59] Why Keep Both Raw and Clean Transcripts
- Raw = traceability, verification
- Clean = readable source for summaries and repurposing
- Separation prevents downstream noise inheritance

### [00:05:57] Automation: Where Teams Overestimate
- Editorial judgment (agents don't know your audience positioning)
- Exception handling (guest corrections, legal concerns, sensitive edits)
- **Mindset: Use automation for acceleration, not abdication**

### [00:06:52] Titles as Promise
- Generate candidates from multiple angles: problem, guest, outcome, curiosity
- A title is a **contract with the listener** — not just a label
- Frequency ≠ strategy; most repeated phrase isn't always the best frame

### [00:08:20] The Five Pre-Publish Checks
1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references (verify what "we'll link that" actually points to)
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits (confirm requested removals)
5. Metadata consistency (all surfaces describe the same episode)

### [00:09:23] Five Things to Fix This Month
1. Standard episode folder structure + naming convention
2. Publish checklist with clear owner
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define required output bundle
5. Lightweight episode log for tracking delays and errors

### [00:09:50] The Automation Rule
- Automate drafts, not approvals
- Machine produces options; human approves what goes public
