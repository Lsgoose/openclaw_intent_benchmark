# Episode 14 — Episode Description

What separates a podcast that feels polished from one that feels chaotic isn't the conversation itself — it's what happens after the recording stops. In this episode, Maya Chen, media production systems advisor, walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why raw transcripts are rich inputs for first drafts but not final editorial truth, how small teams can standardize their output bundles, and where automation genuinely helps versus where it overreaches. The conversation covers common pipeline pain points, the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and a practical checklist any small team can implement this month. Whether you're running a solo show or a three-person operation, Maya's framework will change how you think about production discipline — and about what automation should and shouldn't be trusted to do on its own.
