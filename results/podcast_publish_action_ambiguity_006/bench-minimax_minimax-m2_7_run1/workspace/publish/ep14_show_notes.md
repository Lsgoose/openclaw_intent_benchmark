### Episode Summary
Maya Chen and the host unpack the five-stage podcast production pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution—and explain where small teams stumble first. Key themes include treating the pipeline as a product, separating raw from cleaned transcripts, generating title candidates strategically rather than by frequency, and using automation to accelerate drafts while keeping humans in charge of approvals.

### Practical Takeaways (from the episode)

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot recurring delays and errors
6. Start automating drafts, not approvals
