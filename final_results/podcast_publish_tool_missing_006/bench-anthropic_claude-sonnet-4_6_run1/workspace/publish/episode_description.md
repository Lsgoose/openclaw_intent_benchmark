# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. Maya Chen argues the production pipeline deserves equal attention — because it's the pipeline that determines whether a great recording actually reaches listeners as a great episode.

In this episode, Maya breaks down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams most often break down — inconsistent handoffs, chaotic file naming, and over-reliance on raw transcripts — and what a lightweight but reliable system actually looks like.

Key topics include the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, how to build a standard output bundle for every episode, where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and the five highest-value checks before any episode goes live.

The episode closes with a practical six-step action list for small shows looking to tighten their pipeline this month.
