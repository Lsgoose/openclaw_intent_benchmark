# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Every podcast episode travels through a pipeline — from raw recording to polished, published package — and the reliability of that pipeline directly shapes listener experience. In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages every show moves through: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya explains where small teams struggle most (handoffs, file naming chaos, and mistaking raw transcripts for publish-ready content) and offers a practical framework for building consistency without heavy tooling. The conversation covers how to separate raw and clean transcripts, where automation helps versus where editorial judgment remains essential, and the five checks every episode should pass before going live.

Whether you run a two-person show or manage a growing media operation, this episode gives you a concrete starting point: define your output bundle, own your checklist, and automate drafts — not approvals.
