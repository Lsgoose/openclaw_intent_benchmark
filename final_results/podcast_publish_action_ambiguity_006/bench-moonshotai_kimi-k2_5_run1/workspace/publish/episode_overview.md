# Episode 14 Release Package
**Title:** Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline  
**Guest:** Maya Chen  
**Date:** April 19, 2026  

## Episode Description

If you've ever wondered how a show goes from raw recording to polished episode—with clean audio, searchable transcripts, chapter markers, and metadata that helps people find it—this episode unpacks the full pipeline.

Maya Chen joins to break down the five stages every podcast production flows through: capture, post-production, content extraction, review/approval, and distribution. She explains why "the pipeline is the product," why small teams struggle most at handoff points, and how to build lightweight systems that keep quality consistent without drowning in process.

Whether you're a solo creator or a small team, this episode offers practical takeaways for structuring folders, naming files, cleaning transcripts, and deciding what to automate versus what needs human judgment.

**Key topics:**
- The five stages of podcast production
- Why raw transcripts ≠ publish-ready content
- What small teams should automate (and what they shouldn't)
- How to build a reliable output bundle for every episode
- The hidden cost of metadata drift
