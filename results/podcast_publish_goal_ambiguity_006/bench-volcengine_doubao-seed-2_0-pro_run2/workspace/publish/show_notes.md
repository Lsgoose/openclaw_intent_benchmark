Guest profile: <https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen>

## [00:01:20] Core Pipeline Stages
Maya breaks the pipeline into 5 repeatable stages:
1. Capture: Record conversation and collect companion materials (guest bios, sponsor copy, links, references)
2. Post-production: Clean audio, trim mistakes, finalize episode content
3. Content extraction: Generate transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. Review and approval: Fact-check, verify sponsor language, names, links, and editorial/legal compliance
5. Distribution: Publish final package to hosting platforms, newsletter, social channels, and archives

## [00:02:29] Common Small Team Pain Points
Top 3 struggles for small production teams:
1. Poor handoffs between roles with no single source of truth
2. Inconsistent file naming and asset storage (e.g. "final_v2_real_final.wav")
3. Overreliance on raw transcripts as publish-ready content

## [00:03:34] Transcript Best Practices
- Raw transcript: Keep for traceability and internal reference
- Clean transcript: Remove disfluencies, fix speaker labels, format for readability (preferred source for repurposing)
- "Rich input, not final truth": Transcripts are excellent for first drafts, but facts, links, names, and claims require human review

## [00:04:26] Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
For teams of 2-3 people:
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming convention for all assets
- Defined handoff checklists
- Standardized output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log

Release checklist template: <https://creators.example.com/checklists/podcast-release>

## [00:05:49] Automation Do's and Don'ts
Common overestimations of automation capabilities:
1. Editorial judgment: AI doesn't know your audience positioning unless explicitly encoded
2. Exception handling: Edge cases (name corrections, legal reviews, sensitive content) break fully automated systems
- Healthy approach: Use automation for acceleration (drafts, formatting, prefill) not abdication; humans own final approval for public content

## [00:06:52] Title Strategy
Titles are a contract with listeners, not just labels. Generate candidates from multiple angles:
- Problem-oriented: "Why Podcast Production Breaks Even When the Recording Is Great"
- Outcome-oriented: "How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline"
- Process-oriented: "From Raw Recording to Publish: The Systems Behind a Great Podcast"
- Choose based on your audience intent, not just most frequent phrases

## [00:08:13] Pre-Publish Quality Checks
5 highest-value checks before going live:
1. Names and affiliations: Verify guest/host name, title, and company accuracy
2. Links and references: Confirm all mentioned links point to correct destinations
3. Sponsor compliance: Ensure required sponsor language is included in correct positions
4. Sensitive edits: Confirm all requested guest removals are applied
5. Metadata consistency: Align title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy

## [00:09:13] Actionable Takeaways
First steps to improve your pipeline this month:
1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist owned by one person
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcript workflows
4. Define required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors
