# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline (with Maya Chen)

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product.

In this episode, Maya — who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems — walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why raw transcripts are rich inputs but not editorial truth, how small teams can standardize their output bundle, and where automation genuinely helps versus where it quietly fails.

The conversation covers practical ground: separating raw from clean transcripts, running the five highest-value pre-publish checks, writing titles that function as a contract with the listener, and building a lightweight checklist system a two-person team can actually maintain.

Key takeaway: use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but keep a human in the loop for anything that goes public.

Whether you're running a solo show or a small team operation, this episode gives you a concrete framework for tightening your pipeline without overengineering it.
