# Episode 14 – Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Episode Title

**How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline**

## Short Description (for podcast apps)

A practical conversation with production systems advisor **Maya Chen** on how small teams can build a reliable podcast production pipeline—from raw recording to polished, publish-ready episodes. We walk through the five key stages (capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution), common failure points, and simple structures that keep every episode consistent.

## Long Description (for show notes / website)

In this episode, we dig into why your podcast production pipeline *is* the product.

Host and guest **Maya Chen** break down how a show moves from raw recording to a polished release with clean audio, transcripts, titles, descriptions, and social assets—and why discipline in that pipeline directly shapes the listener experience.

We cover:

- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review/approval, and distribution
- Where small teams usually struggle first: handoffs, asset naming, and relying too heavily on raw transcripts
- Why a transcript is a "rich input, not the final source of editorial truth"
- The difference between raw transcripts and cleaned transcripts, and when to use each
- How to define a lightweight but reliable pipeline for a 2–3 person team
- Using automation and agents for acceleration, not abdication—what to automate and what still needs human judgment
- Practical title strategy: generating multiple angles and treating the title as a contract with the listener
- High‑value pre‑publish checks: names, links, sponsor copy, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency
- Simple ways to log episodes so you can spot delays and repeated errors over time

If you run a small show—or are spinning up a new one—this episode will help you design a repeatable production system that reduces errors, keeps your metadata aligned, and makes future episodes easier to ship.

### Key Takeaways

- Treat the pipeline as part of the product: the way you title, package, and distribute episodes shapes listener trust as much as the conversation itself.
- Maintain both a raw transcript (for traceability) and a cleaned transcript (as the preferred source for summaries and reuse).
- Standardize your episode output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, and a simple publish log.
- Use automation to draft summaries, chapters, and social posts—but keep humans responsible for approvals and edge cases.
- A reliable pipeline is less about fancy tools and more about consistent structure, naming, and ownership.
