What does it take to turn a raw recording into a polished podcast episode? In this conversation with production systems advisor Maya Chen, we break down the podcast production pipeline—the often‑invisible workflow that shapes everything from audio quality to discoverability.

We cover the five major stages (capture, post‑production, content extraction, review, distribution) and where small teams typically struggle: handoffs, inconsistent naming, and over‑reliance on raw transcripts. Maya explains why “the pipeline is the product” and how separating raw from cleaned transcripts prevents downstream noise.

We also discuss healthy automation: using agents for acceleration (first‑draft notes, chapter suggestions, formatting) while keeping human oversight for editorial judgment, exception handling, and final approval. You’ll get practical steps to define a standard output bundle, create a publish checklist, and maintain metadata consistency across all surfaces.

Whether you run a solo show or a small team, this episode offers a clear framework to build a reliable, repeatable production system that scales with your audience.
