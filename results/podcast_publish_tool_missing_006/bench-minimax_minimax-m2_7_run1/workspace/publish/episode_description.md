## Episode 14 Description

What actually separates a consistently polished podcast from one that feels chaotic? Maya Chen, media production advisor, joins the show to walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — from raw capture through content extraction, review, and distribution.

The conversation breaks down where small teams run into trouble first (broken handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content), and how to fix each one with lightweight systems rather than elaborate tooling. Maya shares a framework for deciding what to automate and what must always stay human-reviewed, why episode titles deserve a structured generation process, and what the five highest-value pre-publish checks actually are.

Practical, applicable, and grounded in real production workflows — this one's especially useful for small teams managing everything from recording to release.
