# Show Notes: Episode 15 — Title Tests

## [00:00:00] Introduction
Host introduces the topic: why title tests fail when teams confuse click-through rate with audience fit.

## [00:00:18] Guest Introduction
Host introduces Jordan Kim, who helps podcast networks run title experiments and packaging reviews.

## [00:00:34] The Problem with Title Testing
Guest explains that a title test matrix is useful only if each option maps to a real audience promise. Otherwise, you're just measuring random novelty.

## [00:00:58] Click-Through Rate vs. Strategy
Host: "So title testing is not a slot machine."

Guest confirms and expands: Teams over-index on thumbnail packaging, click-through rate, and novelty, then forget listener intent.

## [00:01:33] What to Track Instead
Guest recommends keeping a test log that captures: the hypothesis, the audience segment, the winning frame, and why it won.

## [00:02:04] Healthy Workflow
Guest describes a healthy title testing workflow:
- One shared title board
- A short review loop
- A final approved headline before distribution

Key lesson: It's not "test everything forever" — it's "learn which promise actually matches the episode."

## [00:02:36] Common Mistakes
Guest highlights a frequent error: running title tests without recording context, then reusing winning headlines in the wrong places where performance drops.

## [00:03:05] Actionable Takeaway for Small Teams
Guest's recommendation for small teams this month:
- Start a simple experiment log
- Write down the audience promise for each option
- Stop treating click-through rate as the whole strategy
