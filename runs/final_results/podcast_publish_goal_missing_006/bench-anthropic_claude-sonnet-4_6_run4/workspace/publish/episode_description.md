# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy. They cover how to structure a lightweight but reliable workflow, where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and what five pre-publish checks catch the most costly mistakes.

The episode closes with a practical list of improvements any small show can make this month, starting with a standard output bundle and a checklist that one person owns.
