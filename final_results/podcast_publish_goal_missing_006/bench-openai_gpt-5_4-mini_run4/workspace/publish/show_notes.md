# Episode Show Notes

[00:00:00] Intro — The podcast production pipeline shapes the listener experience, from raw recording to polished release.
[00:01:20] Major stages — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, distribution.
[00:02:29] Common failures — handoffs, naming/storage drift, and treating transcripts as publish-ready truth.
[00:04:26] Reliable small-team setup — one workspace per episode, clear naming, fixed output bundle, and a publish log.
[00:04:59] Clean vs raw transcript — keep both; use the cleaned transcript for repurposing, and retain raw for traceability.
[00:05:57] Automation boundaries — automate repetitive transforms, not editorial judgment or release approval.
[00:06:57] Title strategy — generate multiple title candidates; frequency is not strategy.
[00:08:20] Pre-publish checks — verify names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.
[00:09:23] Practical takeaway — standardize folder structure, ownership, output bundle, and episode logging.
[00:10:05] Sponsor mention — SoundLayer for transcript, asset version, and review workflow organization.
