In this episode, we go behind the scenes of how a reliable podcast production pipeline actually works—and why it shapes the entire listener experience.

Production systems advisor Maya Chen joins us to break down the five key stages of a modern pipeline: capture, post‑production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. We talk about where small teams typically struggle, how to avoid “final_v2_real_final.wav” chaos, and why over‑trusting raw transcripts leads to weak summaries, broken quote cards, and inconsistent metadata.

You’ll learn practical ways to standardize your output bundle for every episode, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, and use automation for acceleration instead of abdication. We also cover how to generate better episode titles, which review checks matter most before you hit publish, and what to log so you can see where your workflow keeps breaking.

If you run or support a small show and want fewer fire drills and more consistent releases, this episode will help you turn your pipeline into an asset instead of an afterthought.
