# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Show Notes

### [00:00:00] Introduction
Welcome to Episode 14, where we unpack how a podcast goes from raw recording to polished release, and why production systems shape the entire listener experience.
Guest: Maya Chen, media production systems advisor <https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen>

### [00:01:20] Core Pipeline Stages
The 5 foundational stages of a production pipeline:
1. Capture: Record conversation + collect companion materials
2. Post-production: Clean audio + trim content
3. Content extraction: Generate transcripts, summaries, chapters, social assets
4. Review and approval: Validate facts, links, sponsor language, edits
5. Distribution: Publish to hosting, newsletter, social channels, archives

### [00:02:29] Common Small Team Pain Points
Top 3 failure points for small production teams:
1. Poor handoffs between team members with no single source of truth
2. Inconsistent file naming and asset storage ("final_v2_real_final.wav" problem)
3. Overreliance on raw transcripts as editorial truth instead of input material

### [00:03:34] Transcript Best Practices
- Raw transcript: Keep for traceability and internal reference
- Cleaned transcript: Remove disfluencies, fix speaker labels, format for readability – this is the preferred source for all downstream content
- Key principle: Transcripts are rich inputs, not final editorial truth

### [00:04:26] Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
For 2-3 person teams, prioritize:
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming conventions
- Defined handoff points in a shared checklist <https://creators.example.com/checklists/podcast-release>
- Standardized output bundle (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, publish log)

### [00:05:49] Automation Do's and Don'ts
What automation can handle well:
- Transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions
- Formatting, asset packaging, metadata prefill
What automation shouldn't replace:
- Editorial judgment and audience positioning
- Exception handling for edge cases (name corrections, legal review, sensitive content)
- Rule: Use automation for acceleration, not abdication

### [00:06:52] Title Strategy
Titles are a contract with listeners, not just labels. Generate 3-4 candidates from different angles:
1. Problem-oriented: "Why Podcast Production Breaks Even When the Recording Is Great"
2. Outcome-oriented: "How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline"
3. Curiosity-driven: "From Raw Recording to Publish: The Systems Behind a Great Podcast"

### [00:08:13] Pre-Publish Validation Checklist
5 highest-priority checks before going live:
1. Verify guest names, titles, and affiliations
2. Confirm all referenced links point to correct destinations
3. Validate sponsor language is present and accurate
4. Ensure sensitive/requested edits have been applied
5. Confirm metadata consistency across title, description, chapters, and social copy

### [00:09:13] Actionable First Steps
If you want to improve your pipeline this month:
1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist owned by one person
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcript workflows
4. Define required output assets for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track recurring errors and delays
