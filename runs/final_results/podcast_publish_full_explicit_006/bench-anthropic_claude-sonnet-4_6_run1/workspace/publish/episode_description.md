# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the conversation — it's the production pipeline behind it.

In this episode, host and media systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya explains why handoffs break small teams, why raw transcripts are inputs rather than finished content, and how to design a lightweight but consistent workflow that scales.

The conversation covers practical topics including standard output bundles, the difference between raw and clean transcripts, where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and the five highest-value checks before any episode goes live.

Whether you run a two-person show or are building toward a larger operation, this episode offers a clear framework for turning production discipline into a competitive advantage.
