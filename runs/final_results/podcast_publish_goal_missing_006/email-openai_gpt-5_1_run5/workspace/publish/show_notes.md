# Show Notes – Pipeline Podcast, Episode 15: Why Title Tests Fail

[00:00:00] **Opening & Topic Overview**  
- Host introduces the episode: why title tests fail when teams confuse click-through rate with audience fit.  
- Sets the focus on designing experiments that reflect real audience promises, not random novelty.

[00:00:18] **Guest Introduction – Jordan Kim**  
- Host introduces guest Jordan Kim and their work helping podcast networks run title experiments and packaging reviews.  
- Brief context on how large networks manage title testing and packaging across multiple shows.

[00:00:34] **What Makes a Title Test Matrix Useful**  
- Jordan explains that a title test matrix only works when each option maps to a real audience promise.  
- If options are just different flavors of novelty, the team ends up measuring random clicks instead of learning about listener intent.  
- Emphasis on aligning titles with what the episode actually delivers.

[00:00:58] **Title Testing Is Not a Slot Machine**  
- Host and Jordan push back on treating title tests as a high-volume slot machine.  
- Discussion of teams over-indexing on thumbnail packaging, click-through rate, and novelty while neglecting deeper measures of fit and satisfaction.

[00:01:33] **What To Track Instead of Raw CTR**  
- Jordan suggests keeping a test log that captures:  
  - The hypothesis behind each title option.  
  - The audience segment being targeted.  
  - The winning frame and why it resonated.  
- How this log helps teams avoid repeating shallow experiments and instead build durable insight.

[00:02:04] **Healthy Title Testing Workflow for Teams**  
- Jordan outlines a healthy workflow: one shared title board, a short review loop, and a final approved headline before distribution.  
- Why the lesson is not “test everything forever” but “learn which promise actually matches the episode.”  
- How this approach reduces chaos and last‑minute title churn.

[00:02:36] **Common Mistakes in Title Experiments**  
- Examples of teams running tests without recording context, then reusing a winning headline in the wrong environment.  
- How misapplied “winners” lead to performance drops and confusion about what actually works.

[00:03:05] **Action Steps for Small Teams This Month**  
- Jordan’s concrete recommendations:  
  - Start a simple experiment log for every title test.  
  - Write down the audience promise for each option.  
  - Stop treating click-through rate as the entire strategy and focus on fit with listener intent.  
- Closing encouragement to treat title tests as a learning engine, not just a growth hack.
