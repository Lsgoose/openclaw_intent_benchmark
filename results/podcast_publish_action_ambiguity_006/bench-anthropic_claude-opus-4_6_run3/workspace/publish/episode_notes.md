# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Show Notes

In this episode, we sit down with Maya Chen — an advisor to media teams and creator businesses on production systems — to break down what actually goes into a reliable podcast production pipeline. From raw recording to polished publish, we cover the five stages every team should define, where small teams struggle first, how to think about transcripts as inputs rather than finished content, and where automation helps versus where it quietly fails.

### What We Cover

- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution
- Why handoffs, inconsistent naming, and transcript overreliance are the first pain points for small teams
- The critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts
- Where automation accelerates production — and where editorial judgment still matters
- How to generate and evaluate title candidates from different angles
- The five highest-value checks before any episode goes live
- A practical starting checklist for improving your pipeline this month

### Key Takeaway

"The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." — Maya Chen

### Practical Advice

If you run a small show and want to improve your pipeline:

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors

And from the show: if you're experimenting with automation, start by automating drafts, not approvals.
