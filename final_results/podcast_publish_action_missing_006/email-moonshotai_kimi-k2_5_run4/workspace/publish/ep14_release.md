## Descriptions

### Short Description (≤ 280 chars)
Maya Chen joins us to break down the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline—from capture to distribution—and why the pipeline is the product.

### Long Description
If you've ever wondered how a show goes from raw recording to polished episode with clean audio, transcript, chapters, and metadata—this episode is for you.

Maya Chen, who advises media teams on production systems, joins us to unpack the five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. We discuss why small teams struggle with handoffs, the difference between raw and clean transcripts, where automation helps (and where it fails), and practical steps to improve your pipeline this month.

Key topics:
- The five stages every pipeline needs
- Raw vs. clean transcripts as source material
- Why titles are contracts with listeners
- The healthy mindset: automation for acceleration, not abdication
