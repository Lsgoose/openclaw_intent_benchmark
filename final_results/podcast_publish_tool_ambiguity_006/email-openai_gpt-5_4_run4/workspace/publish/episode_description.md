# Episode 14 Description

Episode 14 breaks down how to build a podcast production pipeline that stays reliable as a show grows. Maya Chen joins the conversation to map the workflow from capture and post-production through content extraction, review, and distribution, then explains why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience.

The episode covers the failure points that trip up small teams first: messy handoffs, confusing file versions, and treating raw transcripts like publish-ready truth. It also lays out a lightweight operating model built around one workspace per episode, a standard output bundle, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, and a clearly owned release checklist.

There’s also a practical look at automation—where it genuinely helps, where editorial judgment still matters, and why the safest approach is to automate drafts, not approvals.
