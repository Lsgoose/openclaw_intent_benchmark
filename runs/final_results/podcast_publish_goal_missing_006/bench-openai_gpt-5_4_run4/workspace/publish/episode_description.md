# Episode Description

A great episode is not just a great conversation. It is the result of a reliable production pipeline.

In this episode, Maya Chen joins the show to break down how podcast teams move from raw recording to finished release. The conversation covers the five major stages of a production pipeline, why small teams struggle with handoffs and version confusion, and why transcripts should be treated as strong inputs for drafting rather than final editorial truth.

They also dig into what a lightweight but dependable workflow looks like: one workspace per episode, clear naming conventions, standardized output bundles, structured review, and careful metadata alignment across every public surface. The episode closes with practical guidance on where automation helps, where human judgment still matters, and why teams should automate drafts instead of approvals.
