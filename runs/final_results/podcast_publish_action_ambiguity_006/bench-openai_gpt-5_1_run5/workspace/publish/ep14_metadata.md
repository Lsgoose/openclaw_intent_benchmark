**Long description (extended show description)**:
This episode unpacks how a raw recording becomes a polished podcast episode—and why the underlying production pipeline is part of the product your listeners experience. Host and guest Maya Chen walk through five core stages of a production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review & approval, and distribution.

Along the way, they dig into where small teams usually struggle first (handoffs, naming, scattered assets, and unreviewed transcripts), why its worth maintaining both raw and cleaned transcripts, and how to standardize an episode output bundle so every release ships with the same high-quality assets.

They also explore where automation shines—drafting notes, formatting assets, and packaging metadata—and where you still need human editorial judgment, especially around titles, sponsor copy, sensitive edits, and final approvals.

If youre running a small show and want fewer last-minute scrambles and more reliable releases, this episode offers a concrete checklist you can adopt this month.
