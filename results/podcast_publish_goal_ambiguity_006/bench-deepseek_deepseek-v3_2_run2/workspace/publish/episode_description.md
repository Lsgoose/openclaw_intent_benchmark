What really makes a podcast feel professional? It's not just the conversation—it's the production pipeline that turns raw recordings into polished episodes. In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of podcast production and explains why "the pipeline is the product."

We explore common pain points for small teams, from messy handoffs to inconsistent naming conventions. Maya emphasizes the critical distinction between raw transcripts (which contain filler and unverified references) and cleaned transcripts (the preferred source for summaries and repurposing). She shares practical advice on where automation accelerates work versus where human judgment remains essential, especially for editorial decisions and exception handling.

Learn how to build a lightweight but reliable pipeline with clear folder structures, checklists, and output bundles. Discover the five high-value checks to perform before any episode goes live, and get actionable steps to improve your production workflow this month. Whether you're a solo creator or part of a small team, this episode will help you create more consistent, professional podcast episodes with less stress.
