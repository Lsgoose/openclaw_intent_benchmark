# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline — Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to unpack what really happens between hitting "stop record" and clicking "publish." She breaks the podcast production pipeline into five stages — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as finished content.

The conversation covers how to build a lightweight but reliable workflow: one folder per episode, a fixed output bundle, separated raw and clean transcripts, and a publish checklist with a single owner. Maya and the host also discuss where automation helps (drafting summaries, suggesting titles) and where human judgment remains essential (editorial decisions, sponsor compliance, edge cases).

Key takeaway: "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." Use machines to accelerate drafts — but keep a human between the pipeline and the public.
