# Release Overview — Episode 14

- Title: Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline
- Subtitle: How small teams move from raw recording to a consistent, publish-ready episode package.
- Short description: Host and Maya Chen break down the five-stage podcast production pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and explain why small teams should automate drafts, not approvals.
- Long description: In Episode 14, the show looks at what actually turns a raw podcast recording into a reliable listener experience. Maya Chen joins to explain why the pipeline itself becomes part of the product: titles, transcripts, chapter markers, metadata, links, and review discipline all shape what listeners get. The conversation covers the five major stages of production, common failure points for small teams, why cleaned transcripts matter, where automation helps, and the checks that matter most before release. The practical takeaway is straightforward: standardize the episode workspace, separate raw and cleaned transcript assets, define a fixed output bundle, and keep one human accountable for what goes live.
