Episode 14 explores how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline that turns a raw recording into a polished, consistent release. Maya Chen breaks the workflow into five stages—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and explains why small teams often struggle with handoffs, naming, and transcript-driven work. The conversation covers why cleaned transcripts matter, where automation helps most, and where human judgment still has to lead. It also gets practical about titles, metadata consistency, review checklists, and the minimum output bundle every episode should produce. If you care about making publishing smoother without sacrificing quality, this episode is a clear blueprint.
