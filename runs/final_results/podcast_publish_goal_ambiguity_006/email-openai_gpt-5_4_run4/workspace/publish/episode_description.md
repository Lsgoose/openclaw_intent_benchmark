# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to break down what actually makes a podcast production pipeline reliable. The conversation covers the five core stages of production, where small teams usually lose consistency, and why transcripts should be treated as strong inputs for drafts rather than final editorial truth.

They discuss the difference between raw and clean transcripts, the value of standard output bundles, how to approach titles strategically, and which pre-publication checks matter most before an episode goes live. The episode also explores where automation can genuinely help—cleanup, formatting, packaging, and draft generation—and where human review still has to stay in the loop.

A practical episode for anyone running a small podcast team and trying to ship cleaner, more consistent releases without building an overly complex system.
