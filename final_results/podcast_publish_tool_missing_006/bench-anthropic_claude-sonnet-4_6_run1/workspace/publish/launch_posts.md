## Post 1 — Key Takeaway

Episode 14 is out. One line from this conversation that stuck: "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." Raw transcripts carry filler, false starts, and unverified references. Treating them as publish-ready copy is how low-quality or misleading content slips through. The fix is simple — separate raw from cleaned, and keep a human in the loop before anything goes live.

---

## Post 2 — Discussion Question

New episode on podcast production pipelines. Here's a question worth sitting with: at what point in your production process does a human actually review what goes public? Transcription, summaries, titles, descriptions, social copy — a lot of that can be drafted automatically now. But "drafted" and "approved" are different things. Where's the line in your workflow?
