Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

What happens after you hit stop on a podcast recording? Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five-stage production pipeline that transforms raw audio into a polished, discoverable episode.

We walk through capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—plus the common pitfalls small teams face around handoffs, file naming, and transcript assumptions. Maya explains why clean transcripts beat raw ones for downstream work, where automation helps (and where it fails), and why a title is a contract with your listener, not just a label.

The episode closes with five practical steps any small team can take this month to build a more reliable pipeline, from standard folder structures to human-in-the-loop approval workflows.
