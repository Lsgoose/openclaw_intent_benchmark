# Episode 14 — Descriptions

---

## Short Description (≤ 100 words)

Maya Chen joins to break down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — from capture to distribution — and explain why production discipline matters as much as the conversation itself. Topics include raw vs. clean transcripts, where small teams break down, how to think about automation without giving up editorial control, and the five checks every episode needs before it goes live.

---

## Long Description

If you've ever wondered how a show goes from a raw recording to a polished episode — clean audio, a transcript, chapter markers, social clips, a title, a description, and all the metadata that helps people find it — this episode is for you.

Maya Chen advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems. She joins to walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

Along the way, Maya covers:

- Why the pipeline is the product, not just the conversation
- The three places small teams break down first (handoffs, asset naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts)
- Why a transcript is a rich input but not the final source of editorial truth
- What a standard output bundle should include for every episode
- How to generate title candidates that reflect audience intent rather than keyword frequency
- The five highest-value checks before an episode goes live
- Where automation helps — and where it still needs a human in the loop

Whether you're running a two-person show or advising a larger media operation, this episode offers a practical framework for building consistency into your production process.
