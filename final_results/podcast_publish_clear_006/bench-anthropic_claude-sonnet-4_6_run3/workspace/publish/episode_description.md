# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the conversation — it's the system behind it.

In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why raw transcripts are a rich input but not a source of editorial truth, how small teams can build a lightweight but consistent workflow, and where automation genuinely helps versus where it quietly fails.

Key topics include: standard output bundles, the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, title strategy, and the five highest-value pre-publish checks. The throughline: use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but keep a human responsible for what goes live.

If you run a small show and want a more reliable pipeline, this episode gives you a concrete starting point.
