# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Media advisor Maya Chen joins the show to break down what actually separates amateur productions from professional ones: the pipeline that transforms raw recordings into polished, discoverable episodes. Chen maps the five stages of production—from capture through distribution—and explains why the pipeline itself is the product, not just the conversation it carries.

The episode tackles common failure points for small teams: chaotic file handoffs, inconsistent naming conventions, and the dangerous assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready content. Chen makes the case for separating raw and cleaned transcripts, treating automation as acceleration rather than abdication, and building lightweight but rigorous review checklists.

Key insights include generating multiple title candidates from different angles (problem, guest, outcome, curiosity), the five essential pre-publish checks, and a practical framework for improving any production workflow this month without over-engineering. A must-listen for anyone responsible for editing, publishing, or content operations.
