# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline — Show Notes

**Guest:** Maya Chen — production systems advisor for media teams and creator businesses
**Guest profile:** <https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen>

---

## [00:00:00] Introduction & Framing

The host introduces the topic: how a podcast goes from raw recording to polished, published episode. Maya Chen joins to discuss production systems.

## [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Pipeline

Maya breaks down the pipeline into five stages:

1. **Capture** — recording plus companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. **Post-production** — audio cleanup, trimming, editorial decisions
3. **Content extraction** — transcripts, summaries, show notes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. **Review & approval** — fact-checking, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits
5. **Distribution** — publishing to hosting, newsletter, social, and archives

## [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle

Common pain points: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content.

## [00:03:03] Transcripts Are Inputs, Not Final Truth

Why raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified claims. The transcript is a "rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."

## [00:04:15] Building a Lightweight Reliable Pipeline

Practical advice for small teams: one folder per episode, clear naming, a checklist with handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, publish log).

## [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts

Keep both: raw for traceability, clean for downstream summarization and repurposing. Separating them prevents noise from propagating.

## [00:05:49] Automation: Acceleration, Not Abdication

Where teams overestimate automation — editorial judgment and exception handling. Use machines to produce drafts; humans approve what goes public.

## [00:06:52] Titles as Listener Contracts

Generate multiple title candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven). Frequency is not strategy.

## [00:08:13] The Five Pre-Publish Checks

1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits confirmed removed
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

## [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways

Five steps to improve your pipeline this month:

1. Define a standard episode folder structure
2. Create a publish checklist with one owner
3. Separate raw and clean transcripts
4. Define a required output bundle
5. Keep a lightweight episode log for spotting delays and errors
