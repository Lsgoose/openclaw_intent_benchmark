In this episode, Maya Chen joins the show to break down the podcast production pipeline and why it matters as much as the recording itself. We cover the full path from capture and post-production to transcript cleanup, show notes, title selection, review, and distribution. Maya explains why a transcript is a powerful input but not the final editorial truth, and why teams should separate raw and cleaned transcripts to keep downstream assets accurate. The conversation also digs into the limits of automation, the importance of metadata consistency, and the checks that prevent small release mistakes from compounding. If you care about building a reliable podcast workflow, this episode is a practical guide to turning a raw conversation into a polished public release.
