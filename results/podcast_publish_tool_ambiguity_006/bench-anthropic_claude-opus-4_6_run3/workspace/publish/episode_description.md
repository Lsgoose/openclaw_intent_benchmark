# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

How does a raw recording become a polished episode with clean audio, accurate show notes, and consistent metadata? In Episode 14, host and guest Maya Chen unpack the five stages of podcast production—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and explain why the pipeline itself shapes listener experience as much as the conversation does.

Maya shares where small teams break down first (handoffs, file naming, and treating transcripts as finished content) and offers a practical framework: standardize your episode folder, separate raw and clean transcripts, define a fixed output bundle, and keep a lightweight episode log. The pair also discuss where automation helps—drafting titles, summaries, and social clips—and where human judgment remains essential for editorial quality and edge-case handling.

Key takeaway: automate drafts, not approvals. Make the machine produce options; make the human approve what becomes public.
