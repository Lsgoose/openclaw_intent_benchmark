# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## [00:00] Intro
Welcome to the show! Today we're unpacking how a podcast goes from raw recording to polished published episode, and how your production pipeline shapes the entire listener experience.
Guest: Maya Chen, media production systems advisor <https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen>

## [01:27] The 5 core stages of a podcast production pipeline
1. **Capture**: Record conversation and collect companion materials (guest bios, links, sponsor copy)
2. **Post-production**: Clean audio, trim mistakes, finalize episode content
3. **Content extraction**: Generate transcripts, summaries, show notes, chapter markers, clips, titles
4. **Review and approval**: Fact checking, link verification, legal/editorial signoff
5. **Distribution**: Publish to hosting platforms, newsletters, social channels, and archives

## [02:29] Common pain points for small teams
- Broken handoffs between team members with no single source of truth
- Inconsistent file naming and asset storage ("final_v2_real_final.wav" problem)
- Overreliance on raw transcripts as publish-ready content
> "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."

## [04:26] Lightweight pipeline for small teams (2-3 people)
- One dedicated folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming convention for all assets
- Defined handoff points in a shared checklist <https://creators.example.com/checklists/podcast-release>
- Standard output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log
- Keep both raw (for traceability) and cleaned (for repurposing) transcripts

## [05:49] Automation best practices
- Don't overestimate: AI/agents struggle with editorial judgment and edge cases (guest corrections, legal concerns, sponsor constraints)
- Use automation for acceleration, not abdication: let tools handle first drafts, formatting, metadata prefill, repetitive transforms
- Keep human signoff for all public-facing content quality and correctness

## [06:57] Title strategy
Titles are a contract with your listener, not just a label. Generate candidates from multiple angles:
- Problem-oriented: "Why Podcast Production Breaks Even When the Recording Is Great"
- Outcome-oriented: "From Raw Recording to Publish: The Systems Behind a Great Podcast"
- Audience-focused: "How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline"
Avoid picking titles based on phrase frequency alone – prioritize audience intent and value promise.

## [08:20] Pre-publish priority checks
1. Correct guest names, titles, and affiliations
2. Verify all linked resources match what was promised
3. Confirm sponsor compliance and required language is present
4. Ensure all requested sensitive edits have been applied
5. Validate metadata consistency across title, description, chapters, and social copy

## [09:23] Actionable first steps to improve your pipeline this month
1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist owned by a single team member
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcript assets
4. Define your required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track recurring errors or delays
