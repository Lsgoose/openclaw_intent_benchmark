# Episode 14 Description

How does a podcast go from a raw recording to a polished release without things falling through the cracks? In Episode 14, Maya Chen joins the show to break down what a reliable podcast production pipeline actually looks like for small teams.

The conversation covers the five major stages of production, why transcript quality affects everything downstream, where handoffs usually break, and which parts of the workflow benefit most from automation. Maya also explains why titles are strategic framing decisions, not just labels, and why review still matters even when tools can generate summaries, chapter ideas, and social drafts.

This episode is a practical guide for creators, producers, and media teams who want a cleaner, more consistent release process. The main theme is simple: automate drafts, not accountability.
