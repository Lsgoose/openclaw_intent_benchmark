# Episode Description
Maya Chen joins the show to break down what actually makes a podcast production pipeline reliable. The conversation covers the five core stages of moving from raw recording to published episode, why small teams usually struggle at handoffs and version control, and why a transcript is useful as a draft input but not as final editorial truth. Maya explains when automation helps, where it fails on judgment and edge cases, and how teams can standardize outputs without overbuilding their process. The episode also covers title strategy, metadata consistency, pre-release checks, and the practical habits that make publishing smoother over time. For any small media team trying to reduce mistakes and ship cleaner episodes, this is a grounded walkthrough of the systems behind a polished release.
