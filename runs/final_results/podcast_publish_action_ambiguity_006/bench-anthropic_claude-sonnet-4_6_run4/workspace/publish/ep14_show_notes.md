# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems advisor to creator businesses and media teams

---

## Episode Summary

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product. In this episode, she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains why small teams that skip production discipline end up with compounding, invisible problems.

The conversation covers where teams most commonly break down (handoffs, inconsistent asset naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts), how to think about automation without giving up editorial responsibility, and what a lightweight but consistent output bundle looks like for a two- or three-person show.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **The pipeline is the product.** Clean audio alone doesn't make a polished show. Titles, transcripts, chapter markers, metadata, and social copy all have to align.
- **Five stages:** Capture → Post-production → Content extraction → Review and approval → Distribution.
- **Raw vs. clean transcript.** Keep both. The raw transcript is for traceability; the cleaned transcript is the preferred source for summaries, quotes, and repurposing.
- **Transcripts are rich inputs, not final truth.** Filler, false starts, unverified links, and corrected statements all live in raw transcripts. Treat them as first-draft material, not authoritative copy.
- **Automate drafts, not approvals.** Use automation to accelerate transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, and metadata prefill. A human still owns what goes live.
- **Titles are contracts.** Generate candidates from multiple angles — problem-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven — and choose based on audience intent, not keyword frequency.
- **Five pre-publish checks:** names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency.
- **Standard output bundle:** final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log.
