# Episode Description

Media advisor Maya Chen joins us to unpack the invisible systems behind every great podcast. While listeners experience the conversation, the production pipeline—capture, editing, content extraction, review, and distribution—shapes whether anyone clicks, stays, or comes back.

We walk through the five stages of a reliable pipeline, from raw recording to published episode. Maya explains why small teams struggle with handoffs and version chaos, why transcripts are "rich input, not final truth," and where automation helps versus where it fails. We cover practical tactics: standard folder structures, raw versus clean transcripts, the essential pre-publish checklist, and how to craft titles that actually match listener intent.

The core theme: use automation for acceleration, not abdication. Let machines generate drafts; humans own what goes live.
