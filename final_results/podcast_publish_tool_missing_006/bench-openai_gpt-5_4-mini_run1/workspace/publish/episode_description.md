Episode 14 looks at how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns raw recordings into a polished release. The conversation covers the major stages of capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then gets practical about what small teams can standardize to reduce mistakes. The guest explains why clean transcripts should be treated as rich inputs rather than final truth, how to separate raw and cleaned versions, and why automation is best used to accelerate drafts and formatting instead of replacing editorial judgment. The episode also breaks down title strategy, metadata consistency, and the highest-value checks before an episode goes live. If you want a simpler, sturdier workflow for editing, publishing, and content ops, this one is a useful blueprint.
