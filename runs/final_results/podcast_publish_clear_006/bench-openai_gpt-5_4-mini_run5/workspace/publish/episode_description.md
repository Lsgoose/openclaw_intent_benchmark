Episode 14 explores how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline from raw recording to public release. Maya Chen breaks down the work into capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then explains where small teams usually break down: messy handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating transcripts as final truth. The episode covers the value of keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, using automation for drafts rather than approvals, generating better title options, and running a final review for names, links, sponsor language, and metadata consistency. It closes with a practical checklist for teams that want a simpler, more dependable release process.
