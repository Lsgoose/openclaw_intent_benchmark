# Episode 14 Description

How does a podcast go from raw recording to polished release without quality slipping through the cracks? In Episode 14, the show breaks down what makes a podcast production pipeline reliable.

Host and guest Maya Chen walk through the five core stages of production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. They unpack why small teams often struggle with handoffs, messy file naming, inconsistent metadata, and the mistaken belief that a transcript is automatically publish-ready.

The conversation also explores where automation helps most, where human editorial judgment still matters, and why separating raw transcripts from cleaned transcripts improves everything downstream. The practical takeaway is a lightweight system any small team can adopt: standard folder structure, clear naming conventions, a fixed output bundle, a publish checklist, and human approval for anything public.

If the recording is only the beginning, this episode is about building the system that makes the final release trustworthy.
