Episode 14 explores how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline that turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen breaks the workflow into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The conversation digs into common failure points for small teams, especially handoffs, naming chaos, and the false assumption that transcripts are automatically publish-ready.

The episode also covers why it helps to keep both raw and cleaned transcripts, how automation can accelerate drafts without replacing editorial judgment, and what to check before publishing: names, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency. It closes with practical steps for creating a simpler, more dependable system for every episode.
