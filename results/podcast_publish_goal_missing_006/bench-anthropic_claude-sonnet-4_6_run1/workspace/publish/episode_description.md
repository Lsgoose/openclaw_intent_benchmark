# Episode 14 – Description

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — from capture and post-production to content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content. They cover how to structure a lightweight but reliable workflow, when automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and what the five highest-value pre-publish checks are.

The episode closes with a practical list of steps any small show can take this month to tighten their process — no elaborate system required, just consistency.
