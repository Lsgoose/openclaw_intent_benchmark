# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen  
**Runtime:** ~10 minutes  
**Release Date:** April 19, 2026

## Summary

Maya Chen joins us to discuss how podcast production pipelines shape the entire listener experience. From raw recording to polished episode, the systems behind a show matter as much as the conversation itself.

This episode unpacks the five stages of production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya explains why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating transcripts as publish-ready without human review.

## Key Topics

- **Five-stage production pipeline:** capture → post-production → content extraction → review → distribution
- **Why handoffs kill quality:** One person records, another edits, someone else writes descriptions—without a single source of truth
- **Raw vs. clean transcripts:** The raw transcript is for internal reference; the cleaned version becomes the source for summaries and repurposing
- **Automation boundaries:** Use automation for acceleration (first drafts, formatting, packaging), not abdication (approvals, editorial judgment)
- **Title strategy:** Generate multiple candidates from different angles, then pick based on audience promise, not phrase frequency
- **The five critical pre-publish checks:** names/affiliations, links/references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency

## Notable Quotes

> "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." — Maya Chen

> "Use automation to accelerate. Don't give up responsibility for what goes live." — Maya Chen

> "A title is not just a label. It's a contract with the listener." — Maya Chen

## Actionable Takeaways

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define a required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors
