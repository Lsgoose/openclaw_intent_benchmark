# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy. They cover how to structure a standard output bundle, when automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and how to write titles that function as a contract with the listener rather than just a label.

The episode closes with a five-step checklist any small show can act on this month, plus a clear principle for anyone experimenting with AI tools in their workflow: automate drafts, not approvals.
