# Show Notes: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline with Maya Chen

## Episode Overview
Guest: Maya Chen, production systems advisor for media teams and creator businesses
Runtime: ~10 minutes

---

## Timestamps & Topics

[00:00:00] Introduction to podcast production pipelines
- How production systems shape the entire listener experience beyond just the raw conversation
- Guest intro: Maya Chen, media production systems advisor

[00:01:20] The 5 core stages of a podcast production pipeline
1. Capture: Record conversation + collect companion materials (guest bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. Post-production: Audio cleanup, editing, content selection
3. Content extraction: Transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapters, titles, clips
4. Review & approval: Fact checks, sponsor verification, editorial/legal review
5. Distribution: Publish to hosting platforms, newsletters, social channels, archives

[00:02:29] Common pain points for small teams
- Handoff gaps between team members with no single source of truth
- Inconsistent file naming and asset storage (e.g. "final_v2_real_final.wav")
- Overreliance on raw transcripts as publish-ready content

[00:03:03] Transcript best practices
- Raw transcripts are not automatically usable: contain filler, false starts, uncorrected statements, unverified links
- Use transcripts as first-draft inputs for summaries, chapters, quotes, and social posts
- Always review authoritative content: facts, links, sponsor language, names, affiliations, dates, claims

[00:04:26] Lightweight pipeline for small teams (2-3 people)
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming convention for all assets
- Defined handoff points via a shared checklist
- Standardized output bundle for every episode: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log
- Keep both raw and cleaned transcripts: raw for traceability, cleaned for repurposing

[00:05:49] Automation and AI in production pipelines
- Common overestimates: editorial judgment and exception handling
- Healthy mindset: Use automation for acceleration, not abdication
- Good automation use cases: transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, formatting, asset packaging, metadata prefill
- Human ownership is still required for correctness, tone, and release quality for public-facing content

[00:06:52] Title strategy
- Titles are a contract with the listener, not just labels
- Generate multiple candidates from different angles: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven
- Avoid mechanical title selection based on most repeated phrases; prioritize audience intent

[00:08:13] Pre-publish quality checks
1. Names and affiliations: Verify guest name, title, and company
2. Links and references: Confirm all mentioned links point to correct destinations
3. Sponsor compliance: Ensure required sponsor language is included correctly
4. Sensitive edits: Confirm guest-requested removals are implemented
5. Metadata consistency: Align title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy

[00:09:13] Actionable takeaways to improve your pipeline this month
- Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
- Create a publish checklist owned by one person
- Separate raw and cleaned transcript assets
- Define required output bundles for every episode
- Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays, confusion, and repeated errors
- Bonus: Automate drafts first, not approvals — let machines generate options, humans approve public content
