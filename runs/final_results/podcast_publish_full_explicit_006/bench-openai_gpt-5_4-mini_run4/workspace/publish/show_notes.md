# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

[00:00:00] The episode frames the podcast production pipeline as the part of the show that shapes the listener experience from raw recording to polished release.

[00:01:20] Maya breaks the pipeline into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

[00:02:29] The discussion turns to the most common small-team failures: handoffs, inconsistent naming and storage, and treating transcript-driven work as automatically publish-ready.

[00:03:09] The transcript is described as a rich input, not the final source of truth. It is useful for drafts, summaries, quote candidates, and chapter suggestions, but facts, links, names, sponsor language, dates, and claims still need review.

[00:04:15] For a lightweight but reliable setup, Maya recommends one folder per episode, a clear naming convention, a checklist with handoff points, and a fixed output bundle.

[00:04:59] Raw and cleaned transcripts should both be kept. The cleaned transcript becomes the preferred source for summarization and repurposing, while the raw transcript remains for traceability and verification.

[00:05:57] The episode warns against overestimating automation in editorial judgment and exception handling. Automation should accelerate repetitive transforms, not replace ownership of what goes live.

[00:06:57] Title strategy is treated as a deliberate editorial decision. Multiple title candidates should be generated from different angles, then evaluated against the audience promise rather than frequency alone.

[00:08:13] Before publishing, the highest-value checks are names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapter list, and social copy.

[00:09:13] The practical takeaway is to standardize folder structure, assign checklist ownership, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, define the required output bundle, and keep a lightweight episode log.
