Maya Chen joins the show to unpack how podcast production pipelines shape the entire listener experience. She breaks down the five stages—from capture through distribution—and explains why small teams struggle most at handoffs, naming conventions, and transcript-driven workflows. We explore the critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, why automation should accelerate rather than replace editorial judgment, and how to approach titles as contracts with your audience. Maya also shares her five highest-value pre-publish checks and a practical five-step plan any small team can implement this month to make their pipeline more reliable. Plus, a look at where teams typically overestimate what can be automated, and why edge cases will always require human ownership.
