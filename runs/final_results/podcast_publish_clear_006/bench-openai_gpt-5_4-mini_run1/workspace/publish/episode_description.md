Episode 14 breaks down how a podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished, publish-ready release. Maya Chen explains the five major stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The conversation focuses on where small teams usually get stuck, why raw transcripts and cleaned transcripts should stay separate, and how automation can speed up drafts without replacing editorial judgment. It also covers title strategy, metadata consistency, and the highest-value checks before an episode goes live. The core message is simple: a reliable pipeline is less about heroic effort and more about clear ownership, consistent outputs, and human approval where it matters most.
