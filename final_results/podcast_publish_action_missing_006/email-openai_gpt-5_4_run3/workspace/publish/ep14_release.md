## Short Description
Host and guest Maya Chen break down how small podcast teams can build a reliable production pipeline, from capture and cleanup to review, metadata, and distribution.

## Long Description
In Episode 14, the show looks at what actually turns a raw recording into a polished podcast release. Host and Maya Chen walk through the major stages of a production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

The conversation focuses on where small teams usually get into trouble: invisible handoffs, messy file naming, unverified links, metadata drift, and the mistaken belief that a raw transcript is ready-to-publish truth. Maya argues for a lightweight but disciplined system built around a standard episode workspace, a clean transcript alongside the raw transcript, a fixed output bundle, and a clear publish checklist.

The episode also covers where automation helps most, where human judgment still matters, and why reliable release quality depends on consistency more than complexity.

## Show Notes
Episode 14 explores how to build a podcast production pipeline that small teams can actually maintain.

Key ideas from the episode:
- Treat the pipeline like a media build system with source material, validation, packaging, and deployment.
- Keep one workspace per episode with clear naming conventions and handoff points.
- Maintain both raw and cleaned transcripts so downstream work uses cleaner source material without losing traceability.
- Use automation for drafts and repetitive transforms, not for final responsibility.
- Run a final review for names, affiliations, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.
