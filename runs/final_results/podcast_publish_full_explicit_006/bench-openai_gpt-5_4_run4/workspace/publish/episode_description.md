# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

A great podcast is not just a great conversation. In this episode, the host talks with Maya Chen about the systems that turn a raw recording into a polished, trustworthy release. They break down the five stages of a reliable production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

The conversation covers why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and overreliance on raw transcripts. Maya explains why cleaned transcripts should power summaries and repurposing, while humans still own fact-checking, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and final approval.

They also discuss where automation helps most, where it fails on editorial judgment and edge cases, and how to think about titles as audience promises rather than labels. The episode closes with a practical five-step plan for improving any small-show workflow without overcomplicating it.
