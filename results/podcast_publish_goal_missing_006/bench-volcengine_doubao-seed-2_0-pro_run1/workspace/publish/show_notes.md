# Show Notes: Podcast Production Pipeline

[00:00:00] Introduction
Welcome to the episode about podcast production pipelines, covering how raw recordings become polished, distributed episodes. Guest: Maya Chen, media production systems advisor.

[00:01:20] Pipeline Core Stages
Five core stages of production:
1. Capture: Record conversation and collect companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. Post-production: Clean audio, trim mistakes, finalize episode content
3. Content extraction: Generate transcripts, summaries, show notes, chapters, titles, clips
4. Review and approval: Verify facts, sponsor language, links, names, legal compliance
5. Distribution: Publish to hosting platforms, newsletter, social channels, archives

[00:02:29] Small Team Common Pain Points
Top three challenges for small production teams:
1. Poor handoffs between team members with no single source of truth
2. Inconsistent file naming and asset storage (e.g. "final_v2_real_final.wav")
3. Overreliance on raw transcripts as publish-ready content

[00:03:34] Transcript Best Practices
- Raw transcript: Keep for traceability and internal reference
- Clean transcript: Remove disfluencies, fix speaker labels, format for readability – use as source for summarization/repurposing
- Key principle: Transcripts are rich inputs, not final editorial truth

[00:04:26] Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming convention for all assets
- Defined handoff checklists
- Standard output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log

[00:05:49] Automation Limitations
Common overestimations of automation capabilities:
1. Editorial judgment: AI doesn't know your audience positioning without explicit encoding
2. Exception handling: Edge cases (name corrections, legal concerns, sponsor constraints) break fully automated systems
- Healthy mindset: Use automation for acceleration, not abdication – automate repetitive tasks, keep human approval for public-facing content

[00:06:52] Title Strategy
- Generate multiple title candidates from different angles: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven
- Titles are contracts with listeners, not just labels – match framing to audience intent
- Avoid mechanical title selection based on phrase frequency

[00:08:13] Pre-Publish Quality Checks
Five high-priority pre-launch checks:
1. Correct guest names, titles, and affiliations
2. Verified links and references
3. Sponsor compliance and required language
4. Sensitive edit confirmation (remove requested content)
5. Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy

[00:09:23] Actionable Takeaways
First steps to improve your pipeline:
1. Define standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist owned by one person
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcript files
4. Define required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors
