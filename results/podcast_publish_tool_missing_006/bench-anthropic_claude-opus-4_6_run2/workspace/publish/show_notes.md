# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline — Show Notes

## Guest

Maya Chen — production systems advisor for media teams and creator businesses
[Maya Chen bio](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen)

---

## Timestamped Sections

### [00:00:00] Introduction
Welcome and framing: why the production pipeline shapes the listener experience.

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Production Pipeline
Maya breaks down the pipeline: capture → post-production → content extraction → review & approval → distribution.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Common pain points: handoff confusion, inconsistent file naming, and treating raw transcripts as finished content.

### [00:03:34] What to Extract vs. What Needs Human Review
Transcripts are great for first drafts (summaries, chapters, quotes) but facts, links, names, and claims still need editorial review.

### [00:04:15] Building a Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical setup: one folder per episode, clear naming, a checklist with handoff points, and a standardized output bundle.

### [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
Why keeping both matters — traceability from the raw version, quality from the cleaned version.

### [00:05:49] Automation: Acceleration, Not Abdication
Where teams overestimate automation (editorial judgment, exception handling) and the healthy mindset for using agents in production.

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Generate candidates from multiple angles (problem, guest, outcome, curiosity) and choose based on audience intent, not keyword frequency.

### [00:08:13] The Five Pre-Publish Checks
1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits confirmed
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Five steps to improve your pipeline this month: standard folder structure, owned publish checklist, separated transcripts, defined output bundle, and a lightweight episode log.
