## Timestamped Sections

### [00:00:00] Introduction — Why the Pipeline Is the Product
The host frames the episode's premise: a podcast production pipeline shapes the listener experience far more than most teams realize. Maya Chen joins as guest.

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of Podcast Production
Maya breaks down the pipeline into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Discussion of common pain points: handoff confusion, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready content.

### [00:03:03] Transcripts Are Inputs, Not Truth
Why treating a transcript as clean structured truth leads to low-quality derivative content. The "rich input, not final truth" principle.

### [00:04:15] A Lightweight but Reliable Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical advice: one folder per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log).

### [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
Why teams should maintain both a raw transcript for traceability and a cleaned version for downstream summarization and repurposing.

### [00:05:49] Where Automation Gets Overestimated
Two areas where teams overestimate automation: editorial judgment and exception handling. Edge cases in media production resist full automation.

### [00:06:26] Automation for Acceleration, Not Abdication
The healthy mindset: use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but keep humans responsible for what goes live.

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Why titles matter, how to generate candidates from multiple angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven), and why frequency is not strategy.

### [00:08:13] The Five Highest-Value Pre-Publish Checks
1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits confirmed removed
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Five steps to improve your pipeline this month: standard folder structure, owned publish checklist, separated transcripts, defined output bundle, and a lightweight episode log.
