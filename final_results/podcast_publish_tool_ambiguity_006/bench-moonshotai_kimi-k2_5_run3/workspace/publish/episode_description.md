Episode 14 explores how small teams can build podcast production pipelines that don't break under pressure. Media systems advisor Maya Chen joins to map the five stages every episode travels through—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and explains why the pipeline itself shapes listener experience more than most creators realize.

The conversation digs into where lean teams typically stumble: invisible handoffs between roles, inconsistent file naming that destroys version confidence, and the common error of treating raw transcripts as publish-ready truth. Maya makes the case for separating raw transcripts (for traceability) from cleaned transcripts (for downstream content generation), and cautions against abdicating editorial judgment to automation.

Key takeaways include a practical five-item improvement checklist for teams ready to upgrade their workflows this month, strategies for generating stronger episode titles, and a pre-publish review framework covering names, links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.

Whether you're running a solo show or coordinating a small team, this episode offers concrete systems to reduce errors without adding bureaucracy.
