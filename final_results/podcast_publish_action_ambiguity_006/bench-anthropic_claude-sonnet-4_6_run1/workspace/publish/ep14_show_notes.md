# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems advisor to creator businesses and media teams

---

## Episode Summary

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product. In this episode, she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains why small teams that skip production discipline end up with compounding, invisible errors.

The conversation covers where small teams break down first (handoffs, inconsistent asset naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts), how to think about automation without abdicating editorial responsibility, and what a lightweight but consistent output bundle looks like for a two- or three-person show.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **The pipeline is the product.** Clean audio is necessary but not sufficient. Titles, transcripts, chapter markers, metadata, and social copy all shape the listener experience.
- **Five stages:** Capture → Post-production → Content extraction → Review & approval → Distribution.
- **Handoffs are the first failure point.** Without a single source of truth, publishing quality becomes luck.
- **Raw transcript ≠ clean transcript.** Keep both. The raw version is for traceability; the cleaned version is the preferred source for summaries, quotes, and repurposing.
- **Automation accelerates; humans approve.** Use automation for first drafts, formatting, and asset packaging. Don't give up responsibility for what goes live.
- **Titles are a contract with the listener.** Generate candidates from multiple angles — problem-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven — then choose based on audience intent, not keyword frequency.
- **Five pre-publish checks:** names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency.
- **Standard output bundle:** final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log.
