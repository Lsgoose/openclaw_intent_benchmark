Episode 14 explores how a podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a reliable public release. Maya Chen breaks the workflow into capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then shows where small teams usually break down: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, unclean transcripts, and weak approval steps. The conversation highlights why raw and cleaned transcripts should stay separate, how automation can accelerate drafting without replacing editorial judgment, and why titles, descriptions, show notes, and chapter markers need to stay aligned. The episode closes with practical steps for building a lighter but more dependable system: standardize folders, define a checklist, keep a fixed output bundle, and automate drafts instead of approvals.
