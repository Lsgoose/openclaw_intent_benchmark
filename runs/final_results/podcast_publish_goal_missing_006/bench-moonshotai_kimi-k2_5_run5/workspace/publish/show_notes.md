# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

## Show Notes

### [00:00] Introduction: Why the Pipeline Matters
- Host introduces the podcast production pipeline — the system that transforms raw recordings into polished, discoverable episodes
- Guest Maya Chen joins to discuss how production systems shape the listener experience

### [00:00:36] The Pipeline Is the Product
- Why listeners notice production quality only when something breaks
- The connection between clean audio, accurate transcripts, correct chapter markers, and consistent publishing checklists

### [00:01:27] The Five Stages of Production
Maya outlines the complete pipeline:
1. **Capture** — recording and collecting companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. **Post-production** — audio cleanup, trimming, editorial decisions
3. **Content extraction** — transcripts, summaries, quotes, chapter markers, titles, clips
4. **Review and approval** — fact-checking, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits
5. **Distribution** — publishing to hosting, newsletter, social channels, and archives

### [00:02:33] Where Small Teams Struggle
Three common pain points:
- Handoffs without a single source of truth
- Inconsistent file naming ("final_v2_real_final.wav")
- Treating raw transcripts as publish-ready copy

### [00:03:09] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
- Why raw transcripts contain noise: filler, false starts, jokes that don't read well, unverified links
- Recommendation: maintain both raw (for traceability) and cleaned (for downstream use) versions
- Key principle: *"The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."*

### [00:04:26] A Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming conventions
- Defined handoff checkpoints
- Standardized output bundle for every episode: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log

### [00:05:49] Automation Boundaries
Where teams usually overestimate automation:
- **Editorial judgment** — agents can propose titles but don't know audience positioning
- **Exception handling** — edge cases (name corrections, legal review, sensitive anecdotes) require human oversight
- Healthy mindset: use automation for acceleration, not abdication

### [00:06:57] Title Generation Strategy
- Generate candidates from multiple angles: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven
- A title is a contract with the listener, not just a label
- Frequency is not strategy — the most repeated phrase isn't necessarily the best title

### [00:08:20] The Pre-Publish Checklist
Five highest-value checks:
1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references (verify every "we'll link that")
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits (confirm requested removals)
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

### [00:09:23] Five Things to Do This Month
Maya's practical roadmap for small shows:
1. Define standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with a single owner
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to identify bottlenecks
