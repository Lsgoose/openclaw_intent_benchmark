## Long Description

Most podcast teams focus on the conversation. Maya Chen argues the real product is the system that turns a raw recording into a polished, consistent episode — every single time.

In this episode, Maya walks through the five stages of a reliable production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why handoffs are the first place small teams break down, why a raw transcript is a rich input but not editorial truth, and what a standard output bundle should look like for every episode.

We also get into where automation genuinely helps (first-draft transcription cleanup, chapter suggestions, metadata prefill) and where it tends to fail (editorial judgment, exception handling, and anything that needs to be correct before it goes public).

Key takeaways:
- Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
- Separate raw transcript from cleaned transcript — both have a role
- Build a publish checklist with defined handoff points
- Standardize your output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, and a publish log
- Use automation to accelerate drafts, not to replace approvals
