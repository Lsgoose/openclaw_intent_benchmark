# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to explore how podcast production pipelines shape listener experience. What sounds like an operational topic—moving from raw recording to polished episode—turns out to be central to how audiences discover, navigate, and trust a show.

We walk through the five stages of production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya identifies where small teams typically struggle (handoffs, inconsistent naming, treating transcripts as publish-ready) and offers a lightweight framework for teams of two or three people.

The conversation covers what automation can accelerate (drafts, formatting, packaging) versus what still requires human judgment (editorial framing, exception handling, final approvals). Maya also shares practical tactics for title generation, the essential pre-publish review checklist, and why your metadata consistency across surfaces matters as much as your audio quality.

If you run a small show and want a sharper pipeline without building a bureaucracy, this episode offers a place to start.
