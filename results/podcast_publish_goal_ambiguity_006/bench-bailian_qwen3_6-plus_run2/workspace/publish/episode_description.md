# Episode Description

How does a raw recording become a polished, discoverable podcast episode? Maya Chen joins the show to break down the podcast production pipeline — the five-stage process that turns conversation into content. From capture and post-production to content extraction, review, and distribution, Maya explains why the pipeline itself is the product, not just the audio.

Small teams face three recurring pain points: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready. Maya argues for keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, standardizing output bundles, and using automation to accelerate drafts — never to replace human approval.

The episode covers practical advice for teams of two or three: folder structures, publish checklists, title strategy, and a five-point pre-publish review. The takeaway is simple but powerful: you don't need an elaborate system to get meaningful gains. You need consistency.
