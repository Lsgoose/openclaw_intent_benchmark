This episode dives into the nuts and bolts of building a reliable podcast production pipeline that actually supports your creative work instead of fighting it. Consultant and systems thinker Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five key stages of production—capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution—and how small teams can make each one more resilient.

We explore why the pipeline is part of the product, why raw transcripts are “rich inputs” rather than editorial truth, and how to design a lightweight workflow with clear ownership, consistent naming, and a standard output bundle for every episode. Maya also shares where automation and agents truly help (and where they don’t), plus the highest-value checks to run before anything goes live. If you’re ready to turn your show’s behind-the-scenes chaos into a dependable system, this conversation is your starting point.
