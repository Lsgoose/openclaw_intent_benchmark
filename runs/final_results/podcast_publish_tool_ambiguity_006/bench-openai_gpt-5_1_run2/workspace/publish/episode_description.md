In this episode, we unpack how to build a podcast production pipeline that’s as reliable as your recording setup.

Consultant Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five key stages of a pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and why small teams feel the pain first when handoffs, naming, and storage aren’t well defined.

We dig into the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, how to treat transcripts as rich inputs rather than final truth, and where automation and agents can accelerate your workflow without replacing editorial judgment. Maya also shares practical guidance on crafting trustworthy titles, running high-value pre-publish checks, and defining a standard output bundle for every episode.

Whether you’re running a lean production team or experimenting with AI tools, this conversation will help you design a pipeline that reduces errors, supports consistent publishing, and ultimately improves your listener experience.
