# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems advisor to creator businesses and small media teams

---

## Episode Summary

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product. In this episode, she walks through the five stages of a reliable production workflow — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains why small teams break down at handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready content.

The conversation covers practical guidance for two- and three-person teams: how to structure episode folders, what belongs in a standard output bundle, where automation genuinely helps versus where it creates risk, and the five highest-value checks before any episode goes live.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **The pipeline is the product.** Vague titles, sloppy transcripts, and inconsistent metadata all degrade the listener experience before they ever press play.
- **Five production stages:** capture → post-production → content extraction → review & approval → distribution.
- **Keep raw and clean transcripts separate.** The raw transcript is for traceability; the cleaned version is the preferred source for summaries, quotes, and repurposing.
- **Automate drafts, not approvals.** Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms. Keep a human responsible for what goes live.
- **Titles are contracts.** Generate candidates from multiple angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) and choose based on audience intent — not keyword frequency.
- **Five pre-publish checks:** names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency.
- **Standard output bundle:** final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log.
