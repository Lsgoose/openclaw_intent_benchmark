# Episode Description

Why do title tests produce misleading wins? This episode breaks down the difference between chasing click-through rate and actually matching the audience promise of an episode. Host and guest Jordan Kim unpack why title experiments fail when teams optimize for novelty, thumbnails, or packaging cues without tracking listener intent.

The conversation covers what a title test matrix is for, what should be logged during experiments, and how small teams can build a healthier workflow with a shared title board, short review loops, and a final approved headline before distribution. It also highlights a common mistake: reusing a past winning headline without preserving the context that made it work.

If title testing has started to feel random, this episode offers a cleaner framework for learning which promise truly fits the episode.
