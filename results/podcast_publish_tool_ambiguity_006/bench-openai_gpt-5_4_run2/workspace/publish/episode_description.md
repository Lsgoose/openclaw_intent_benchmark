# Episode Description
From raw recording to final publish, Episode 14 breaks down what it actually takes to build a reliable podcast production pipeline. Host and guest Maya Chen walk through the five core stages of production, where small teams usually fail first, and why transcripts should be treated as rich inputs rather than final editorial truth.

The conversation covers handoffs, naming conventions, raw vs. cleaned transcripts, standard output bundles, title strategy, review checkpoints, and the limits of automation in public-facing media work. The big takeaway: automation can accelerate drafts and repetitive transforms, but humans still need to own approvals, edge cases, and release quality.

If a podcast workflow has started to feel fragile, messy, or overly dependent on memory, this episode offers a practical blueprint for making it more consistent.
