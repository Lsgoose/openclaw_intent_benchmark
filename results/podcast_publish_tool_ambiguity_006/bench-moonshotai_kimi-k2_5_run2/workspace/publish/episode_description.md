Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the conversation—it's what happens after the recording stops. Media advisor Maya Chen joins us to break down the five-stage production pipeline that transforms raw audio into a professional release: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. We explore why small teams struggle with handoffs and file naming chaos, why raw transcripts aren't publish-ready, and where automation helps versus where it fails. Maya shares practical fixes any team can implement this month: standard folder structures, clean transcript separation, and the "automate drafts, not approvals" rule. Whether you're a solo creator or a three-person team, this episode offers a roadmap for building consistency without bureaucracy.
