Episode 14 explores what actually makes a podcast production pipeline reliable. Maya Chen explains why small teams run into trouble around handoffs, inconsistent file naming, messy asset storage, and the mistaken assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready truth.

The conversation walks through a lightweight workflow for small shows: keep one workspace per episode, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, define a fixed output bundle, and use a review checklist that verifies names, affiliations, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency before anything goes live.

The episode also covers where automation helps most—cleanup, draft notes, chapter suggestions, formatting, and metadata prefill—and where human ownership still matters most: editorial judgment, edge cases, tone, and correctness.
