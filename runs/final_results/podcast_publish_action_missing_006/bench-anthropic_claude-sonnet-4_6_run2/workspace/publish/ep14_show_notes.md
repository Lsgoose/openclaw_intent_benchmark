# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems advisor to creator businesses and small media teams

---

## Description (short)

How does a raw recording become a polished episode? Maya Chen breaks down the five-stage podcast production pipeline — from capture to distribution — and explains why production discipline shapes the listener experience more than most teams realize.

---

## Description (long)

If you've ever wondered how a show goes from a raw recording to a finished episode with clean audio, a transcript, chapter markers, social clips, a title, a description, and all the metadata that helps people find it — this episode is for you.

Maya Chen advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems. In this conversation, she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams break down (handoffs, inconsistent naming, treating raw transcripts as publish-ready copy), what should always get human review before going live, and how to think about automation as acceleration rather than abdication.

Key topics:
- The five-stage pipeline and what each stage actually involves
- Why "the pipeline is the product" — and what breaks when it isn't treated that way
- Raw transcript vs. clean transcript, and why the distinction matters for downstream content
- What to automate (first drafts, formatting, metadata prefill) vs. what to keep human (editorial judgment, exception handling, final approval)
- The five highest-value pre-publish checks: names, links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency
- Title strategy: generating candidates from multiple angles rather than picking the most-repeated phrase
- A practical five-step improvement plan for small teams

Quote from the episode:
> "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." — Maya Chen
