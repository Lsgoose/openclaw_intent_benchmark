# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen break down what it takes to build a dependable podcast production pipeline — even with a small team. Maya outlines five core stages (capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution) and explains where most teams hit friction: unclear handoffs, messy file naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content.

The conversation covers practical strategies for standardizing episode outputs, separating raw from cleaned transcripts, crafting better titles, and running a pre-publish review checklist. Maya and the host also discuss where automation helps (drafting, formatting, metadata prefill) and where human judgment remains essential (editorial decisions, edge cases, and final approval).

Key takeaway: a reliable pipeline isn't about elaborate tooling — it's about consistency, clear ownership, and reducing noise before downstream work begins.
