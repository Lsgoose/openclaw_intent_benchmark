Key takeaway post

A reliable podcast production pipeline is built on clarity and consistency: one workspace per episode, a standard output bundle (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, chapters, titles, and logs), and automation that accelerates drafts without replacing human editorial judgment.

Discussion question post

How does your podcast production pipeline handle edge cases—like name corrections, sensitive edits, or sponsor constraints—and where could clearer ownership or a better checklist make those moments less stressful?
