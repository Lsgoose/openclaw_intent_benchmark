## Long description
What turns a raw recording into a polished episode people can trust?

In Episode 14, Maya Chen joins the show to unpack the podcast production pipeline end to end: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation focuses on where small teams usually break down first, why transcript-driven workflows need editorial judgment, and how standardized outputs make publishing smoother across every surface.

This episode also gets specific about what should be automated, what still needs human review, and why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience. If your team is juggling audio cleanup, transcripts, metadata, social copy, and approvals without a clear source of truth, this one is for you.

## Key themes
- The production pipeline is part of the product, not just back-office process.
- Small teams usually struggle first with handoffs, naming, and source-of-truth problems.
- A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth.
- Separate raw transcripts from cleaned transcripts for traceability and downstream quality.
- Use automation to speed up drafts, formatting, and packaging, not to abdicate release responsibility.
- Standardized output bundles reduce confusion and improve reliability.
- Final review should check names, affiliations, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.
