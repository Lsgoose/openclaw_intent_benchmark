What does it take to turn a raw recording into a polished podcast episode? In this conversation with production systems expert Maya Chen, we break down the reliable pipeline that separates amateur shows from professional ones.

Most podcasters focus on the conversation itself, but the real product is the entire production pipeline—the system that transforms raw audio into clean episodes with accurate transcripts, compelling titles, useful show notes, and consistent metadata. Maya explains the five key stages of production, from capture through distribution, and reveals where small teams most often stumble.

We discuss why transcripts are "rich inputs, not final truth," how to use automation wisely (accelerate drafts, not abdicate approvals), and what critical checks should happen before any episode goes live. You'll learn practical strategies for title generation, metadata consistency, and creating a lightweight but reliable workflow that scales with your team.

Whether you're a solo creator or part of a small media team, this episode provides actionable frameworks for building a production system that delivers quality consistently, episode after episode.
