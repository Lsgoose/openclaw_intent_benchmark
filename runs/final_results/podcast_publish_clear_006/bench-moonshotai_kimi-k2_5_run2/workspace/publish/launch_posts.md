# Launch Posts

## Post 1: Key Takeaway

The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth. Raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified references — useful for drafts, dangerous for authoritative claims. Before anything goes live, a human still needs to own correctness, tone, and release quality. Automation accelerates; it doesn't abdicate.

## Post 2: Discussion Question

Most podcast teams automate drafts but still handle approvals manually — yet many wish they could automate more. Here's the tension: editorial judgment and exception handling (name corrections, legal concerns, sponsor constraints) are where fully automated systems reliably fail. Where's the line for your show? What would you never automate, no matter how good the tools get?
