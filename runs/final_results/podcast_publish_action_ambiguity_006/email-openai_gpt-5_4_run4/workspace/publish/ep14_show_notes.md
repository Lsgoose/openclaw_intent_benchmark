# Episode 14 Show Notes

**Episode:** Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline  
**Guest:** Maya Chen

A reliable podcast production pipeline is not just an internal convenience. In this episode, Maya Chen explains why the pipeline shapes the listener experience itself. The conversation walks through the five major stages of production — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and shows where small teams usually run into trouble first.

Maya highlights a few recurring failure points: invisible handoffs, inconsistent asset naming, and overconfidence in transcript-driven publishing. One of the clearest distinctions in the episode is the difference between a raw transcript and a cleaned transcript. The raw version supports traceability. The cleaned version becomes the better source for summaries, notes, clips, and repurposed content.

The episode also covers how teams should think about automation. The practical advice is straightforward: automate repetitive transforms and first drafts, but keep human ownership over what becomes public. That matters most around titles, links, names, affiliations, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and final metadata consistency.

## What you’ll hear
- Why production discipline matters even for a two- or three-person team
- The five-stage model for podcast production
- How transcript quality affects every downstream asset
- Why standardized output bundles reduce publishing errors
- Where automation helps and where editorial review still matters
- A simple checklist for improving your process this month

## Key takeaway
If you want a more dependable production system, start with consistency: a standard folder structure, a naming convention, separated raw and cleaned transcripts, a fixed output bundle, and one clear owner for the publish checklist.
