# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the quality of the conversation — it's the system behind it. In this episode, host and production systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content. She makes the case for keeping both raw and clean transcripts, standardizing an output bundle for every episode, and treating automation as a tool for acceleration — not a replacement for editorial judgment.

The conversation covers title strategy, the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and a practical six-step plan for improving your pipeline this month. Whether you're a solo creator or a small team, this episode gives you a clear framework for building a production process that's consistent, traceable, and ready to scale.
