# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Production pipelines don't just move files—they shape the listener experience. In this episode, media operations advisor Maya Chen breaks down how small teams can build reliable workflows without drowning in complexity.

We walk through the five stages every episode passes through: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya explains why raw transcripts make excellent inputs but terrible final copy, where automation helps versus where it fails, and why your episode title is a contract with your listener—not just a label.

The conversation covers practical systems for teams of two or three: standardized folder structures, defined handoff points, and the fixed output bundle every episode should produce. We also dive into the pre-publish checklist that catches the errors that erode trust—names, links, sponsor compliance, and metadata alignment.

If your production process depends on memory and heroics, this episode offers a path to consistency without enterprise overhead. Recorded with support from SoundLayer.
