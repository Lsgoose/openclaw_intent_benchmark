Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen breaks down the five core stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then explains why small teams struggle with handoffs, naming, and transcript quality. The conversation covers how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, which tasks automation can speed up, and where human judgment still has to own accuracy, tone, and release quality. It also digs into title strategy, metadata consistency, and the highest-value checks before an episode goes live. If you care about making publishing calmer, faster, and less error-prone, this episode is a practical blueprint.
