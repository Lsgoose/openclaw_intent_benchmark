# Episode 14 — Episode Description

How does a raw recording become a polished, discoverable podcast episode? In this episode, Maya Chen — who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems — walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

We cover the pain points small teams face first — messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready copy. Maya explains why transcripts are "rich input, not final truth," and why keeping both raw and cleaned versions matters for downstream quality.

We also discuss where automation helps and where it falls short, how to generate title candidates that actually match your audience promise, and the five highest-value checks to run before any episode goes live. If you run a small show and want to improve your pipeline this month, the practical takeaways are simple: standardize your folder structure, define an output bundle, separate raw from cleaned transcripts, and automate drafts — not approvals.
