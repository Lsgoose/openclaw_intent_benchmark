## Short description
What takes a podcast episode from raw recording to a polished release? Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a reliable production pipeline, where small teams usually struggle, and how to use automation to accelerate drafts without giving up editorial responsibility.

## Long description
A great podcast is more than a great conversation. In this episode, Maya Chen explains why the production pipeline shapes the listener experience just as much as the recording itself.

We unpack the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Along the way, the conversation covers common failure points for small teams, why cleaned transcripts matter, how metadata drift hurts trust, and where automation helps most.

This episode is especially useful for small podcast teams building a repeatable workflow. Maya shares a practical starting point: one workspace per episode, a clear naming convention, a single publish checklist owner, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, a standard output bundle, and a lightweight episode log.

If you want a better system for turning recordings into publish-ready episodes, this one is for you.

## Show notes
In Episode 14, the show looks at the systems behind a polished episode release. Maya Chen argues that “the pipeline is the product” because editing, packaging, labeling, and distribution all shape how a listener experiences the show.

The conversation maps podcast production into five stages:
- Capture
- Post-production
- Content extraction
- Review and approval
- Distribution

A major theme is that transcripts are valuable, but not automatically publish-ready. Maya recommends keeping both a raw transcript for traceability and a cleaned transcript for readable downstream use. She also warns against treating transcript-derived copy as final editorial truth without review.

The practical advice for small teams is straightforward:
- Use one folder or workspace per episode
- Keep a clear naming convention
- Standardize the output bundle for every episode
- Assign ownership for the publish checklist
- Log repeated delays and mistakes so the process improves over time

The episode also covers where automation fits best. It can speed up repetitive transforms like cleanup, first drafts, formatting, and metadata prefill, but a human still needs to approve what becomes public.
