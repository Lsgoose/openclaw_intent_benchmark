# Episode 14 — Episode Description

What actually turns a raw recording into a polished, discoverable podcast episode? In this episode, host and production systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the full five-stage pipeline — from capture and post-production through content extraction, review, and distribution — and explain why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience just as much as the conversation does.

Maya covers the three places small teams most often break down (handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts), what a lightweight but reliable pipeline looks like for a two- or three-person team, and where automation genuinely helps versus where it quietly fails. The episode closes with five concrete actions any show can take this month to build more consistency into their release process.

Whether you're running a solo show or coordinating a small team, this episode gives you a practical framework for treating production discipline as a core part of your content quality — not just operational overhead.
