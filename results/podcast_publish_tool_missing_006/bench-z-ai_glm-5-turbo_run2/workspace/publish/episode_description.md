# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five stages every podcast team needs — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product, not just the conversation.

She covers the most common pain points small teams face: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the dangerous assumption that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content. Maya argues for keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, standardizing output bundles per episode, and treating automation as acceleration rather than abdication.

The conversation also digs into title strategy (generate multiple angles, not just the most repeated phrase), the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and a practical five-step plan any small show can implement this month to tighten their workflow.

Whether you're a solo creator or part of a small media team, this episode offers a clear framework for turning chaotic production into a reliable, repeatable system.
