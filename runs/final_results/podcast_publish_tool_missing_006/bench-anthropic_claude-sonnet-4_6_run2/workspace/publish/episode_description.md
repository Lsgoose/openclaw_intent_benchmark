# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline — Description

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. But the production pipeline — how a recording becomes a polished, distributed episode — shapes the listener experience just as much as what's said.

In this episode, media systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and overconfidence in raw transcripts — and what a lightweight but disciplined system actually looks like.

Maya and the host also dig into where automation genuinely helps (first drafts, formatting, metadata prefill) versus where human judgment is irreplaceable (editorial calls, exception handling, final approvals). Plus: why a title is a contract with the listener, and the five highest-value checks before any episode goes live.

If you run a small show and want a more consistent, less stressful release process, this episode is a practical starting point.
