# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, production systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience.

Maya covers where small teams most often break down: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the mistaken belief that a raw transcript is ready-to-use content. She makes the case for keeping raw and cleaned transcripts separate, standardizing an output bundle for every episode, and treating automation as a tool for accelerating drafts — not replacing editorial judgment.

The episode closes with a practical six-step checklist any small show can act on immediately, plus five high-value pre-publish review checks that prevent the most common release mistakes.

If your production process depends more on memory than on systems, this episode is the place to start.
