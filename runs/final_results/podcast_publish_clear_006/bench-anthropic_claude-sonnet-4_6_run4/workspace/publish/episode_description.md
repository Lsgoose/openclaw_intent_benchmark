# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a show that sounds polished from one that feels inconsistent? Usually it's not the conversation — it's the production pipeline behind it.

In this episode, host and media systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya explains where small teams most often break down — inconsistent handoffs, chaotic file naming, and over-reliance on raw transcripts — and what a lightweight but reliable system actually looks like in practice.

The conversation covers how to think about automation honestly: use it to accelerate repetitive transforms like transcription cleanup and first-draft notes, but keep humans responsible for what goes live. Maya also shares the five highest-value pre-publish checks and a practical five-step plan any small team can act on this month.

If you produce a podcast — or support someone who does — this episode is a practical guide to building a pipeline that holds up under real conditions.
