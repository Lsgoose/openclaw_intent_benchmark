# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Show Notes

**[00:00:00]** Introduction to podcast production pipelines and why they matter for listener experience.

**[00:00:27]** Guest introduction: Maya Chen, who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems.

**[00:00:47]** The pipeline is the product: How editing, packaging, labeling, and distribution shape the final experience.

**[00:01:20]** The five major stages of a podcast production pipeline:
1. Capture: Recording and collecting companion materials
2. Post-production: Audio cleaning and editing decisions
3. Content extraction: Transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapters, titles, clips
4. Review and approval: Fact-checking, sponsor language, names, links
5. Distribution: Final package to hosting platform, newsletter, social channels, archives

**[00:02:29]** Common pain points for small teams:
- Handoffs between team members
- Inconsistent naming and asset storage
- Transcripts requiring human judgment (not automatically publish-ready)

**[00:03:34]** Transcripts as rich inputs vs. final truth: What can be automated vs. what needs human review.

**[00:04:26]** Lightweight pipeline for small teams (2-3 people):
- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming conventions
- Checklist with defined handoff points
- Fixed output bundle (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log)

**[00:04:54]** Raw vs. clean transcripts: Keep both for traceability and clean downstream processing.

**[00:05:49]** Automation pitfalls: Overestimating editorial judgment and exception handling.

**[00:06:52]** Title strategy: Generate multiple candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven).

**[00:08:13]** High-value pre-publish checks:
1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

**[00:09:13]** Practical takeaways for improving your pipeline this month:
1. Define standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with clear ownership
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and errors

**[00:09:58]** Automation principle: Use automation for drafts, not approvals. Machines produce options, humans approve what becomes public.
