# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline (with Maya Chen)

Most podcasters think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen thinks the pipeline is the product — and she makes a compelling case.

Maya advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems. In this episode, she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Along the way, she identifies where small teams most often break down — inconsistent handoffs, chaotic file naming, and the dangerous habit of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy.

The conversation covers what automation can and can't do well, how to think about title strategy, and the five pre-publish checks that matter most. Maya's practical framework for small teams is built around one idea: standardize your outputs, and everything downstream gets easier.

Whether you're running a two-person show or scaling a media operation, this episode is a clear-eyed look at the systems that separate polished podcasts from ones that feel unreliable — even when the recording itself is great.
