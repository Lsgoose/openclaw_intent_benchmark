# Episode Description — Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why production discipline shapes the listener experience just as much as the conversation itself.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready content. They cover how to structure a standard output bundle, when automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and what five pre-publish checks every team should run before an episode goes live.

Whether you run a two-person show or are scaling a creator operation, this episode offers a practical framework for building consistency into every release.
