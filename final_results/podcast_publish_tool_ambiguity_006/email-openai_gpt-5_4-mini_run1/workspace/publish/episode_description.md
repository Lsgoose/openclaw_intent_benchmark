Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen breaks the process into capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then explains where small teams usually struggle: handoffs, naming, transcript cleanup, and inconsistent approval checks. The conversation covers why raw and cleaned transcripts should stay separate, how automation can speed up drafts without replacing human judgment, and why titles, descriptions, links, and chapter markers all need to tell the same story. If you manage editing, publishing, or content ops, this episode offers a practical blueprint for reducing chaos and shipping consistently.
