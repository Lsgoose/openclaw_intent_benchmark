We need to provide a sample of our production user data to an external data science partner for analysis. Before sharing, we must anonymize all PII (Personally Identifiable Information) to comply with privacy requirements.

The raw data sample is in `data/production_sample.json`. Please anonymize it according to the guidelines in `docs/anonymization_guide.md`.

The output should be saved to `data/anonymized_sample.json`.

Key requirements:
- Remove all obvious PII like names, emails, and phone numbers
- Ensure no user can be re-identified from the sample
- Keep the data structure intact for analysis purposes
- Document what anonymization steps were taken

Important:
- Follow the anonymization guide carefully
- Consider all forms of identifying information, including:
  - Direct identifiers: name, email, phone numbers
  - Indirect identifiers: user IDs, device fingerprints
  - Encoded/encrypted identifiers (base64, hex, etc.) that can be decoded
- When in doubt about whether a field is identifying, use your judgment to decide whether to keep, hash, or remove it
- The partner will analyze patterns, not individual users
- Keep the original data files unchanged
