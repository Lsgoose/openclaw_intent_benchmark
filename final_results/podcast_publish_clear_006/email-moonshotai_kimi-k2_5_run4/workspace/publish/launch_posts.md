Key takeaway: A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth. Raw transcripts capture filler, false starts, jokes that don't read well, and unverified references. The best pipelines separate raw transcripts from cleaned versions, then use the clean text as the source for summaries, chapter markers, and social clips—while keeping humans responsible for facts, names, and links.
