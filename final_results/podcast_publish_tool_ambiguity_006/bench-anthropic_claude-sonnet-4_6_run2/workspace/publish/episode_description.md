# Episode 14 — Episode Description

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. But the real product is the pipeline that turns a raw recording into a polished, findable, consistent episode.

In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen — a media production systems advisor — walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. They cover where small teams break down (handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts), how to design a lightweight but consistent workflow, and where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable.

Key topics include: why a raw transcript is a rich input but not editorial truth, how to build a standard output bundle for every episode, what the five highest-value pre-publish checks are, and how to think about titles as a contract with the listener — not just a label.

If you run a small show and want a more reliable production process, this episode gives you a practical starting point.
