# Launch Posts

## Post 1: Key Takeaway

A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth. Raw transcripts contain filler and unverified references. The best pipelines separate raw capture from cleaned content, then use the cleaned version for titles, chapters, and summaries.

---

## Post 2: Discussion Question

Where is your pipeline most fragile — handoffs, inconsistent naming, or treating transcripts as publish-ready? Pipelines fail at the gaps: between recording and editing, or the handoff to whoever writes the description. Which gap would you fix first?
