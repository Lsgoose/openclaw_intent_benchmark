## Episode Summary

Maya Chen joins the show to unpack how podcast production pipelines shape the entire listener experience. From raw recording to polished episode, we walk through the five stages every team needs, why transcripts are rich inputs (not final truth), and how to use automation for acceleration without abdication.

Key insight: "The pipeline is the product." When handoffs are invisible, publishing quality becomes luck.

## What You'll Learn

- The five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review/approval, distribution
- Why separating raw and cleaned transcripts matters for downstream automation
- Where small teams struggle most (hint: handoffs and naming conventions)
- The healthy automation mindset: accelerate drafts, never abdicate approvals
- How to generate title candidates that frame the same material for different audience intents

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention**
2. **Create a publish checklist that one person owns**
3. **Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript**
4. **Define the required output bundle for every episode** (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log)
5. **Keep a lightweight episode log** to spot delays and repeated errors

## Notable Quotes

> "The pipeline is the product."

> "Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what becomes public."
