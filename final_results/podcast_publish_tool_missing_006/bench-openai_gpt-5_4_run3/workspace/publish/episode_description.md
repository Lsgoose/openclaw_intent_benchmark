# Episode Description

A great podcast is not just a great conversation. It is the result of a reliable production pipeline. In this episode, Maya Chen joins the show to break down how small teams can move from raw recording to polished release without depending on memory, messy handoffs, or last-minute heroics.

The conversation covers the five stages of a podcast production pipeline, why transcripts should be treated as rich inputs rather than final editorial truth, how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps, and where human review still matters most. They also dig into title strategy, metadata consistency, and the highest-value checks to make before publishing.

If the goal is fewer avoidable mistakes and a smoother path from recording to release, this episode offers a practical framework for building a pipeline that stays dependable as the show grows.
