# Episode Description

A great podcast is more than a great recording. In Episode 14, Maya Chen explains how reliable production pipelines turn raw conversations into polished releases with clean audio, usable transcripts, accurate metadata, and consistent publishing workflows. The conversation breaks the process into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Along the way, Maya highlights the failure points that trip up small teams, including messy handoffs, bad file naming, overtrusting raw transcripts, and inconsistent metadata. She also explains where automation helps most — draft generation, formatting, and packaging — and where human judgment still matters, especially for facts, links, titles, sponsor language, and edge cases.

If a small team wants immediate gains, this episode offers a practical plan: standardize episode folders, separate raw and cleaned transcripts, define a fixed output bundle, and automate drafts without automating approvals.
