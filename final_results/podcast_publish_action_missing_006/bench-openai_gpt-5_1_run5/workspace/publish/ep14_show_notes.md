# Episode 14 · How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** [Maya Chen](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen) — advisor to media teams and creator businesses on production systems

**Sponsor:** [SoundLayer](https://creators.example.com/tools/soundlayer-overview) — collaboration tooling for small media teams

---

If you	run a small show, your production pipeline is part of your product.

In this episode, consultant Maya Chen joins the show to unpack what a reliable pipeline actually looks like when you don't have a big production staff. We walk through the five core stages — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and dig into where pipelines tend to break in the real world.

You
ll hear:

- Why fragile processes show up directly in the listener experience
- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline (and what can go wrong at each step)
- How to keep handoffs clean when multiple people touch an episode
- Why you should maintain both a raw transcript and a cleaned transcript
- Where automation helps (transforms and drafts) and where you still need human judgment
- A practical five-step plan to improve your pipeline this month, without rebuilding everything

Maya also shares specific advice on titles, transcripts, and review checklists, including why "a transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth" and why consistency beats complexity for small teams.
