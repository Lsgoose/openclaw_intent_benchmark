This episode examines how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen explains the five core stages—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and why small teams need clear handoffs, naming conventions, and a standard output bundle. The conversation also covers the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps, and why human review still matters for facts, links, sponsor language, and metadata. A practical episode for anyone building a more consistent publishing workflow.
