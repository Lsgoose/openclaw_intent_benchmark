Episode 14 explores how to build a reliable podcast production pipeline from raw recording to published episode. Maya Chen explains the five stages of a healthy workflow: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The conversation covers why small teams struggle with handoffs, naming, transcript cleanup, and inconsistent review, and why raw transcripts should never be treated as final truth. It also digs into automation: useful for drafts, formatting, and packaging, but not a replacement for editorial judgment. The episode closes with practical steps for tighter operations, including standardized episode folders, clear naming conventions, separated raw and cleaned transcripts, a fixed output bundle, and a lightweight publish log.
