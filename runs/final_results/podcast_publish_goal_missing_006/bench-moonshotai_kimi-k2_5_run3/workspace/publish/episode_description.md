# Episode Description

A conversation with media systems advisor Maya Chen about the hidden infrastructure behind great podcasts. We break down the five-stage production pipeline—from capture and post-production through content extraction, review, and distribution—and explore why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content.

Maya shares practical frameworks for lightweight but reliable workflows: standard folder structures, defined output bundles, and separating raw transcripts from cleaned versions. We discuss where automation helps (first drafts, formatting, asset packaging) and where it fails (editorial judgment, exception handling, final approval).

The episode also covers episode titling strategy, the five essential pre-publish checks, and how to view titles as contracts with listeners rather than mere labels. Whether you run a solo show or a small team, this conversation offers concrete steps to improve your production discipline this month without building an elaborate system.

Key takeaway: consistency beats complexity. Define your outputs, automate your drafts, and keep humans responsible for what goes live.
