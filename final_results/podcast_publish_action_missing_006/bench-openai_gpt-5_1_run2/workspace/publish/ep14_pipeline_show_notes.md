# Episode 14  Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen  
**Sponsor:** SoundLayer  

## Episode summary

This episode breaks down how small podcast teams can turn a fragile, memory-driven publishing process into a reliable production pipeline. Host and guest walk through the full journey from raw recording to a polished episode that listeners can actually find, navigate, and share.

Consultant and media systems advisor **Maya Chen** argues that "the pipeline *is* the product"  its not just what you record, but how consistently you edit, package, label, and ship it. Together, they map out a five-stage pipeline, highlight common failure modes, and show where automation helps versus where human judgment still has to own the final call.

By the end of the conversation, youll have a concrete model for your own episode workspace, the required asset bundle for every release, and a pragmatic checklist you can start using this week.

## Key ideas

- A podcast production pipeline behaves like a software build pipeline: source material, transforms, validation, packaging, and deployment.
- Most problems show up at handoffs and in asset sprawl: no single source of truth, confusing filenames, and transcripts living separately from descriptions and clips.
- Treat transcripts as **rich input, not final truth**. They are great for drafts of summaries, chapters, quotes, and social posts, but all facts, links, names, and sponsor language still need review.
- Maintain both a raw transcript (for traceability) and a cleaned transcript (for readability and downstream automation).
- Automation is for **acceleration, not abdication**. Let tools propose titles, chapters, and drafts; keep humans responsible for correctness and release quality.
- Titles are a contract with the listener. Generate options from different angles (problem-, guest-, and outcome-oriented) and pick the one that best matches your audience promise.
- A reliable pipeline produces the same core asset bundle for every episode, which makes everything downstream  from search to social  easier.

## The five stages of a podcast production pipeline

1. **Capture**  
   - Record the conversation.  
   - Collect companion materials: guest bio, sponsor copy, links, and references.

2. **Post-production**  
   - Clean and level audio.  
   - Trim mistakes and decide what actually ships in the episode.

3. **Content extraction**  
   - Generate transcripts and cleaned transcripts.  
   - Draft summaries, quotes, show notes, chapter markers, and title candidates.  
   - Identify potential clips.

4. **Review and approval**  
   - Verify facts, names, and affiliations.  
   - Check links and references against your vetted list of URLs.  
   - Confirm sponsor language and any requested guest edits.  
   - Make sure the title, description, transcript, and chapters all match.

5. **Distribution**  
   - Package final audio, metadata, and assets.  
   - Publish to your host, newsletter, social channels, and archives.  
   - Log what shipped and when for future reference.

## Practical checklist you can copy

For a small team, Maya suggests standardizing around a simple, repeatable structure:

- One folder or workspace per episode.  
- A clear naming convention for all assets.  
- A defined owner for the publish checklist.  
- A fixed output bundle that every episode produces.

A minimal output bundle for each episode might include:

- Final mixed audio file  
- Raw transcript  
- Cleaned transcript (the one youre reading now)  
- Show notes and episode description  
- Title and alternate title candidates  
- Chapter list with timestamps  
- Thumbnail or cover image (if applicable)  
- A simple publish log entry (date, version, who approved)
