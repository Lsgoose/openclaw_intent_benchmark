# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline — Show Notes

## Guest

Maya Chen — production systems advisor for media teams and creator businesses.
[Guest profile](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen)

---

## Timestamped Sections

### [00:00:00] Introduction & Why the Pipeline Matters
The host introduces the topic: how a raw recording becomes a polished, discoverable episode. Maya frames the pipeline itself as the product.

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Production Pipeline
Maya outlines the major stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Common pain points: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content.

### [00:03:03] Transcripts Are Inputs, Not Final Truth
Discussion of why raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified claims — and why they should be treated as rich inputs rather than authoritative sources.

### [00:04:15] A Lightweight but Reliable Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical advice: one folder per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, publish log).

### [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
Why keeping both versions matters — raw for traceability, clean for downstream summarization and repurposing.

### [00:05:49] Automation and Its Limits
Where teams overestimate automation: editorial judgment and exception handling. The healthy mindset is "acceleration, not abdication."

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Generate multiple title candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) and compare against audience intent.

### [00:08:13] The Highest-Value Pre-Publish Checks
Five priority checks: names/affiliations, links/references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency across all surfaces.

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Actionable steps: standardize folder structure, create a publish checklist, separate raw and clean transcripts, define a required output bundle, and keep an episode log.
