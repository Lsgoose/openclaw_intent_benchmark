# Episode Description — Episode 14

Most podcast listeners never see the system behind the episodes they love. In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five-stage production pipeline that takes a raw recording from capture to published episode — and explains where small teams most often lose control.

Maya covers the three common pain points that derail small shows: broken handoffs, messy file naming, and the trap of treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content. She shares a practical framework for teams of two or three: one folder per episode, a defined output bundle, and a clear separation between raw and cleaned transcripts.

The conversation also tackles automation honestly. Maya argues for accelerating repetitive tasks like transcript cleanup and draft generation while keeping humans in charge of editorial judgment, exceptions, and final approval. She offers practical advice on title creation, the five high-value checks before any episode goes live, and a simple starting list for shows that want to improve their pipeline this month.

Guest: Maya Chen, media production systems advisor.
