# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline — Episode Description

In this episode, production advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages every podcast passes through on its way from raw recording to published episode: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation explores where small teams struggle most — messy handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the dangerous assumption that a transcript equals publish-ready content. Maya explains why raw transcripts are rich inputs for first drafts but never the final source of editorial truth, and shares a practical framework for building a lightweight, reliable pipeline without elaborate tooling. The episode also covers how to think about titles as listener contracts, where automation helps versus where it creates risk, and a five-point pre-publish checklist that catches the errors most likely to erode audience trust. Whether you run a two-person show or manage a growing media operation, this episode offers a clear path from chaos to consistency.
