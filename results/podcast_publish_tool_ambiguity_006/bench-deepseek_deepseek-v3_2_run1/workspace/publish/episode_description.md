What does it take to turn a raw recording into a polished podcast episode? In Episode 14, we sit down with production systems expert Maya Chen to unpack the podcast production pipeline—the often-invisible workflow that shapes the entire listener experience.

We break down the five major stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the misconception that transcripts equal publish-ready content. She emphasizes separating raw from cleaned transcripts and using automation for acceleration, not abdication.

Learn practical strategies for titles (generate multiple angles, don't just repeat frequent phrases), essential pre-publish checks (names, links, sponsor compliance, metadata consistency), and how to build a lightweight but reliable system. The episode includes a clear action plan: define folder structures, create checklists, standardize output bundles, and keep episode logs.

Whether you're a solo creator or small team, this conversation provides concrete steps to reduce workflow debt and publish with confidence. Sponsored by SoundLayer, a collaboration tool for small media teams.
