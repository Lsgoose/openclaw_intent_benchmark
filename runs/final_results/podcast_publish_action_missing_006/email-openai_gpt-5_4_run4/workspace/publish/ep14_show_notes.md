# Episode 14 Show Notes

Episode 14 is a practical conversation about how to build a podcast production pipeline that stays reliable as a show grows. Host and Maya Chen focus on the systems behind release quality: how you capture source material, clean and package the episode, review the right risks before publish, and keep every public-facing asset aligned.

## What this episode covers

- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution
- Why handoffs and inconsistent asset naming create avoidable release failures
- Why a transcript should be treated as a rich input rather than final editorial truth
- Why small teams should keep both raw and cleaned transcripts
- Where automation helps most: cleanup, first drafts, formatting, asset packaging, and metadata prefill
- Where humans still need to own the release: correctness, tone, exception handling, and public quality
- The five highest-value pre-release checks: names, affiliations, links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency
- A simple improvement plan for small shows: folder structure, naming conventions, checklists, output bundles, and a lightweight episode log

## Notable lines

- "The pipeline is the product."
- "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."
- "Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but don’t give up responsibility for what goes live."
- "A title is not just a label. It’s a contract with the listener."
