# Episode 14 — Episode Description

Most podcasters think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen thinks the pipeline is the product.

In this episode, Maya — who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems — walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams most often break down (inconsistent handoffs, chaotic file naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts), and what a lightweight but disciplined system actually looks like in practice.

The conversation covers how to separate raw transcripts from cleaned ones, why automation handles repetitive transforms well but fails on edge cases, how to generate title candidates that reflect audience intent rather than keyword frequency, and which five pre-publish checks matter most before an episode goes live.

The core takeaway: use automation to accelerate drafts, not to replace the human who owns what goes public. Consistency across every surface — audio, metadata, show notes, social copy — is what makes a production feel trustworthy.
