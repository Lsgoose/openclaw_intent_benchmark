# Episode Description

Maya Chen joins the show to unpack the podcast production pipeline — the often-overlooked system that turns a raw recording into a polished, discoverable episode. She breaks the pipeline into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

The conversation explores where small teams struggle first: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready. Maya explains why transcripts are excellent inputs for drafts but still require human review for facts, links, and editorial claims. She walks through building a lightweight but reliable pipeline for teams of two or three, the importance of separating raw and cleaned transcripts, and where automation helps versus where it falls short.

The episode also covers title strategy, pre-publish review checklists, and five practical steps to improve your workflow this month. The core message: automate drafts, not approvals.
