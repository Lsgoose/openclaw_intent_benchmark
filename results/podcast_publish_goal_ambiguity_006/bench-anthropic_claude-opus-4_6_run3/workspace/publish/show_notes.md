# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline — Show Notes

**Guest:** Maya Chen — production systems advisor for media teams and creator businesses  
**Guest profile:** [Maya Chen](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen)

---

## Timestamps

### [00:00:00] Introduction & Why the Pipeline Matters
The host frames the episode topic: how a raw recording becomes a polished, published episode. Maya joins and establishes the core thesis — "the pipeline is the product."

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Production Pipeline
Maya outlines the major stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The host draws an analogy to software build pipelines.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Discussion of common pain points: handoff confusion, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content.

### [00:03:03] Transcripts as Inputs, Not Final Truth
Maya explains why raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified claims. Transcripts are excellent first-draft inputs but not the final source of editorial truth.

### [00:04:15] Building a Lightweight Reliable Pipeline
Practical advice for two- or three-person teams: one folder per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, publish log).

### [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
Why teams should maintain both versions — raw for traceability, clean for downstream summarization and repurposing.

### [00:05:49] Automation and Its Limits
Where teams overestimate automation: editorial judgment and exception handling. The healthy mindset is "acceleration, not abdication."

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Maya recommends generating multiple title candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) and comparing them against audience intent.

### [00:08:13] The Five Pre-Publish Review Checks
Priority checks before going live: names/affiliations, links/references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency across all surfaces.

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Five actionable steps: standardize folder structure, create an owned publish checklist, separate raw and clean transcripts, define a required output bundle, and keep an episode log for spotting delays and errors.
