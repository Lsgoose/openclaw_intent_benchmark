# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems advisor to creator businesses and small media teams

---

## Episode Summary

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product. In this episode, she walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline, explains why treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy is one of the most common and costly mistakes small teams make, and lays out a practical framework any two- or three-person show can adopt this month.

Topics covered:

- The five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, distribution
- Why handoffs, inconsistent naming, and transcript overconfidence are the first places small teams break
- The difference between a raw transcript and a clean transcript — and why both matter
- Where automation accelerates production and where it still requires human judgment
- How to generate and evaluate title candidates from multiple angles
- The five highest-value pre-publish checks before an episode goes live
- A five-step action plan for improving your pipeline this month

---

## Key Quote

> "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."
>
> — Maya Chen

---

## Practical Takeaways

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, publish log)
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to surface where delays and repeated errors occur

**Bonus from the host:** If you're experimenting with automation, start by automating drafts — not approvals. Make the machine produce options; make the human approve what becomes public.
