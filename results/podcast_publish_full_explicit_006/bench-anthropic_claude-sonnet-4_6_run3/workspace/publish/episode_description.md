# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What actually turns a raw recording into a polished, discoverable episode? In this conversation, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a podcast pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams most often struggle: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the mistaken belief that a raw transcript is ready-to-use content. They cover what a lightweight but reliable pipeline looks like for a two- or three-person team, where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable, and how to think about titles as a contract with the listener rather than just a label.

The episode closes with a concrete five-step action list for any show that wants to tighten its production process this month — no elaborate system required, just consistency.
