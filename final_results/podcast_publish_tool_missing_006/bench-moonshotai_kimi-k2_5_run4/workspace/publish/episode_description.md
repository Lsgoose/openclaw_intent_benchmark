Maya Chen joins the show to break down what it actually takes to move a podcast from raw recording to published episode. We walk through the five-stage production pipeline—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and identify where small teams typically get stuck: handoffs without source of truth, inconsistent file naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready copy. Maya explains why a cleaned transcript (disfluencies removed, speaker labels fixed) should be your source for summaries and repurposing, while the raw version stays internal for verification. We also cover the healthy limits of automation: use agents to accelerate repetitive tasks like transcription cleanup and first-draft notes, but keep humans responsible for editorial judgment and exception handling. The episode includes practical frameworks for generating titles from multiple angles, a five-point pre-publish review checklist, and a starter plan any small team can implement this month.
