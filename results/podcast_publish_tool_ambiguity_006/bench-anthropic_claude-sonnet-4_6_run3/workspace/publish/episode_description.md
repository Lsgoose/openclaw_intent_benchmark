# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What actually turns a raw recording into a polished, findable episode? In this conversation, host and production systems advisor Maya Chen break down the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — from capture and post-production through content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya explains why treating a raw transcript as publish-ready copy is one of the most common mistakes small teams make, and how separating raw from clean transcripts protects every downstream step. She covers where automation genuinely helps (first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, metadata prefill) and where human judgment is still irreplaceable (editorial calls, exception handling, final approval).

The episode closes with a practical six-step checklist any small show can act on immediately: standardize your folder structure, own your publish checklist, separate your transcripts, define your output bundle, log your episodes, and automate drafts — never approvals.
