Episode 14 looks at what it takes to build a reliable podcast production pipeline from raw recording to publish-ready release. The conversation breaks the process into clear stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya Chen explains why small teams run into trouble when handoffs, naming conventions, and transcript cleanup are left to memory instead of a defined system. The episode also covers why a raw transcript is not the same as a clean transcript, how automation can speed up drafts without replacing human judgment, and why metadata consistency matters across titles, descriptions, chapter markers, and social copy. The result is a practical guide for making podcast production more consistent, less fragile, and easier to scale.
