# Episode Description

How does a raw recording become a polished podcast episode? In Episode 14, Maya Chen — who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems — walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

We cover the three pain points where small teams struggle first (handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the false assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready), why transcripts should be treated as a rich input rather than the final source of editorial truth, and how to build a lightweight but dependable pipeline with a standard folder structure, a publish checklist, and a fixed output bundle.

Maya also shares where teams overestimate what automation can handle, why titles are a contract with the listener rather than a label, and the five highest-value checks to run before any episode goes live. The takeaway: automate drafts, not approvals — make the machine produce options, and make the human decide what becomes public.
