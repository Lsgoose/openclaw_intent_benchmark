Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline shapes the listener experience from raw recording to published episode. Maya Chen breaks the workflow into capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution, then shows why small teams often struggle with handoffs, naming, and treating transcripts like final truth. The discussion covers why cleaned transcripts should be separated from raw ones, how automation can speed drafting without replacing human judgment, and why titles, metadata, links, sponsor language, and sensitive edits all need deliberate review. The episode closes with practical steps for building a lighter but more reliable system: standard folders, a clear checklist, a fixed output bundle, and a simple episode log. If your team wants fewer surprises at publish time, this one is a playbook for consistency.
