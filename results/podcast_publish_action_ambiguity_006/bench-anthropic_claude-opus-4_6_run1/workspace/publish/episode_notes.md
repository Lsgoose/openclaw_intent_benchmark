# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Summary

This episode explores how small podcast teams can build a consistent, reliable production pipeline — from raw recording to polished publish. Host and guest Maya Chen break down the five stages of podcast production (capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution), discuss where teams commonly struggle, and offer practical advice on separating raw from clean transcripts, generating title candidates, automating drafts without abdicating editorial judgment, and maintaining metadata consistency across all surfaces.

## Key Takeaways

- A podcast pipeline has five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review & approval, and distribution.
- Handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready copy are the most common failure points.
- Always maintain both a raw transcript (for traceability) and a cleaned transcript (for downstream use).
- Automate drafts, not approvals — let machines produce options, let humans approve what goes public.
- Standardize your output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, and publish log.
