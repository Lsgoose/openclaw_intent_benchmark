Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the conversation—it's the production pipeline behind it. Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five stages of podcast production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. We explore why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the misconception that raw transcripts are publish-ready content.

The conversation covers practical territory: the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps versus where it fails, and why your title is a contract with the listener, not just a label. Maya shares a five-point pre-publication checklist and offers immediate, actionable steps for teams ready to tighten their workflow this month. For creators balancing quality with limited resources, this episode maps out how to build systems that scale without drowning in complexity.
