# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What actually turns a raw recording into a finished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself shapes the listener experience just as much as the conversation inside it.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the common mistake of treating a raw transcript as publish-ready content. They cover how to design a lightweight but reliable system for a two- or three-person team, where automation genuinely helps (first drafts, formatting, metadata prefill) and where human judgment is still irreplaceable (editorial calls, exception handling, final approval).

The episode closes with five concrete steps any small show can take this month to tighten their pipeline — no elaborate tooling required.
