# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Your podcast production pipeline shapes the entire listener experience — from raw recording to polished episode. In this episode, Maya Chen, who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems, breaks down the five stages every show needs: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

We cover where small teams struggle first — handoffs without a single source of truth, inconsistent file naming, and the dangerous assumption that raw transcripts are publish-ready. Maya explains why transcripts are "a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth," and how to separate raw from cleaned transcripts for better downstream results.

We also discuss where automation helps and where it falls short, title strategy as a contract with the listener, the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and five practical steps any small team can implement this month. The takeaway: use automation for acceleration, not abdication. Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what becomes public.
