# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the quality of the conversation — it's the production pipeline behind it.

In this episode, host and media systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya explains why small teams most often break down at handoffs and inconsistent asset management, and why treating a raw transcript as publish-ready content is one of the most common — and costly — mistakes in podcast production.

The conversation covers how to structure a lightweight but consistent pipeline for a two- or three-person team, where automation genuinely helps (first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, metadata prefill) and where human judgment remains essential (editorial calls, exception handling, final approvals). Maya also shares a five-point pre-publish review checklist and a practical set of first steps for any team looking to tighten their workflow this month.

Key takeaway: make the machine produce options; make the human approve what goes live.
