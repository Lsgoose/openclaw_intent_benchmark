## [00:00:00] Introduction: The Pipeline Is the Product

Maya Chen joins the show to discuss why podcast production pipelines matter more than most creators realize. The conversation explores how the systems behind a show—editing, packaging, labeling, and distribution—shape the entire listener experience.

## [00:01:27] The Five Stages of Production

Maya breaks down podcast production into five core stages:

1. **Capture** — Recording the conversation and collecting companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links)
2. **Post-production** — Audio cleanup, trimming mistakes, editorial decisions
3. **Content extraction** — Transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapters, titles, clips
4. **Review and approval** — Fact-checking, sponsor language verification, legal review
5. **Distribution** — Publishing to hosting platforms, newsletters, social channels, and archives

## [00:02:29] Common Pain Points for Small Teams

Three recurring struggles for small production teams:

- **Handoffs** — No single source of truth when multiple people touch an episode
- **Inconsistent naming** — Files like "final_v2_real_final.wav" create version confusion
- **Transcript assumptions** — Raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, and unverified claims that need human judgment

## [00:03:03] Transcripts as Rich Input, Not Final Truth

Maya explains what transcripts can and cannot do:

- **Good for:** First drafts of summaries, chapter suggestions, quote candidates, searchability, repurposing
- **Requires review:** Facts, links, sponsor language, names, affiliations, dates, claims

> "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."

## [00:04:26] A Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams

Key elements for teams of 2–3 people:

- One folder/workspace per episode
- Clear naming conventions
- A checklist with defined handoff points
- A **standard output bundle** for every episode: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, and publish log

## [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts

Maya recommends maintaining both:

- **Raw transcript** — Preserves original wording for traceability and verification
- **Clean transcript** — Edited for disfluencies, fixed speaker labels, and readable formatting; becomes the preferred source for summarization and repurposing

## [00:05:49] Automation: Acceleration, Not Abdication

Two areas where automation is commonly overestimated:

- **Editorial judgment** — Agents can propose options but don't know audience positioning without explicit encoding
- **Exception handling** — Edge cases (name corrections, legal reviews, sensitive anecdotes) occur regularly and break fully automated systems

> "Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms... but don't give up responsibility for what goes live."

## [00:06:52] Crafting Effective Titles

Titles are contracts with listeners, not just labels. Maya advises generating multiple candidates from different angles:

- Problem-oriented
- Guest-oriented
- Outcome-oriented
- Curiosity-driven

Then compare against your actual audience promise. Frequency of phrases in a transcript is not a strategy for titles.

## [00:08:13] The Pre-Publish Review Checklist

Five high-value checks before going live:

1. **Names and affiliations** — Verify guest details
2. **Links and references** — Confirm every "we'll link that" has a verified destination
3. **Sponsor compliance** — Check required language and placement
4. **Sensitive edits** — Confirm requested removals are complete
5. **Metadata consistency** — Title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy should describe the same episode

## [00:09:13] Five Steps to Improve Your Pipeline This Month

Practical takeaways for small shows:

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with a single owner
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and errors

> "Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what becomes public."
