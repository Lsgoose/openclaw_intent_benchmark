# Episode 14 Description

A great podcast episode is not just a strong conversation. It is the result of a reliable production pipeline that turns raw recordings into something listeners can trust and navigate.

In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a dependable podcast workflow: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation covers where small teams usually run into trouble, why transcripts should be treated as rich inputs rather than final editorial truth, how to separate raw and cleaned transcripts, and where automation helps versus where human judgment still matters.

The practical takeaway is simple: standardize the episode workspace, define a consistent output bundle, use checklists with clear ownership, and make humans approve what becomes public.
