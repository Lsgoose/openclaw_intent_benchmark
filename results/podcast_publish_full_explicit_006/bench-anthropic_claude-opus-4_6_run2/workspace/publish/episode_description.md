# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What turns a raw recording into a polished, discoverable episode? In this conversation, host and guest Maya Chen break down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explain why production discipline matters as much as the conversation itself.

They cover where small teams struggle first (handoffs, file naming, and treating transcripts as finished content), how to separate raw and clean transcripts for better downstream quality, where automation helps and where it gets overestimated, and the five highest-value checks before any episode goes live.

Whether you run a two-person show or manage a growing content operation, this episode offers a practical framework for shipping consistent, high-quality episodes without relying on memory and heroics.
