Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished release. Maya Chen explains the five core stages—capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution—and why small teams benefit from clear handoffs, consistent naming, and a fixed output bundle. The conversation digs into the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation helps, where it falls short, and why human review still matters for facts, links, sponsor language, and metadata consistency. The episode also covers title strategy, prepublish checks, and a simple takeaway for teams trying to reduce release-day chaos: automate drafts, not approvals.
