# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Overview
From raw recording to publish: how small teams build production pipelines that deliver consistent, professional episodes without elaborate systems.

## Guest
**Maya Chen** — Media advisor to creator businesses and media teams on production systems.  
https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen

## Episode Summary
Maya Chen joins us to break down the five stages of podcast production: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. We explore why production discipline matters (even for small teams), the critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, and where automation helps versus where human judgment remains essential.

Key insight: Treat transcripts as "rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." Raw transcripts contain disfluencies, corrections, and unverified references that can propagate downstream if not cleaned before reuse.

## Practical Takeaways
1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with clear handoff points
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript workflows
4. Define a standard output bundle for every episode (audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log)
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to identify recurring delays or errors
