Maya Chen joins the show to break down the podcast production pipeline from raw recording to publish-ready episode. The conversation covers capture, editing, transcript cleanup, show notes, chapter markers, review, and distribution, with a clear warning that a transcript is a rich input, not the final source of truth. Maya explains why small teams need consistent naming, a standard output bundle, and separate raw and cleaned transcripts. She also shares practical guidance on title strategy, metadata consistency, and where automation helps most: drafting, formatting, and packaging. The episode closes with a simple principle for modern media teams: automate the repetitive work, but keep humans responsible for what goes live.
