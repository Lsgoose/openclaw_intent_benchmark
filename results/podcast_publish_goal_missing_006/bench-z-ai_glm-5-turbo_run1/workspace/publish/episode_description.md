Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — from capture and post-production through content extraction, review, and distribution. She explains why small teams struggle with handoffs, naming conventions, and the false assumption that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content. The conversation covers when to automate versus when to keep a human in the loop, how to approach title strategy beyond keyword frequency, and the five highest-value pre-publish checks every episode should pass. Practical takeaways include standardizing your output bundle, separating raw and cleaned transcripts, and automating drafts while keeping a human owner for what goes live.
