# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

How does a raw recording become a polished, discoverable podcast episode? Maya Chen, who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems, walks through the five-stage pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

We cover the three pain points that trip up small teams first (handoffs, naming chaos, and the myth that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content), the critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, and where automation helps versus where it misleads. Maya shares a practical pre-publish review checklist, a title strategy that treats titles as listener contracts rather than labels, and five concrete steps any small show can implement this month.

The takeaway: use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but never abdicate editorial responsibility. Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what goes live.
