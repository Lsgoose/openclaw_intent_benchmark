# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Timestamps

**[00:00:00]** Introduction to podcast production pipelines and why they matter for listener experience.

**[00:00:27]** Guest introduction: Maya Chen, advisor to media teams and creator businesses on production systems.

**[00:01:20]** Defining the five major stages of a podcast production pipeline:
1. Capture - recording and collecting companion materials
2. Post-production - audio cleaning and editing
3. Content extraction - transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapters, titles, clips
4. Review and approval - fact checking, sponsor language, names, links
5. Distribution - final package to hosting platform, newsletter, social channels, archives

**[00:02:33]** Common pain points for small teams:
- Handoffs between team members without single source of truth
- Inconsistent naming and asset storage
- Transcript-driven tasks requiring more judgment than expected

**[00:03:03]** The transcript as "rich input, not final truth" - why raw transcripts need cleaning and verification before becoming publish-ready content.

**[00:04:26]** Lightweight pipeline recommendations for small teams (2-3 people):
- One folder or workspace per episode
- Clear naming conventions
- Checklist with defined handoff points
- Fixed output bundle (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log)

**[00:04:54]** Importance of maintaining both raw and cleaned transcripts for traceability and quality.

**[00:05:49]** Where teams overestimate automation:
- Editorial judgment (titles, positioning)
- Exception handling (guest name corrections, legal concerns, sensitive content)

**[00:06:52]** Title strategy: generate multiple candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) and compare against audience promise.

**[00:08:13]** High-value pre-publish checks:
1. Names and affiliations
2. Links and references
3. Sponsor compliance
4. Sensitive edits
5. Metadata consistency across all surfaces

**[00:09:13]** Practical takeaways for improving pipelines:
1. Define standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with clear ownership
3. Separate raw and cleaned transcripts
4. Define required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep lightweight episode log to track delays and errors

**[00:10:05]** Closing thoughts: Use automation for acceleration (drafts, suggestions) not abdication (approvals, final decisions).
