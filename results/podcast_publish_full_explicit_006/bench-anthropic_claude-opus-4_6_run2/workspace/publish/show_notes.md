## Timestamped Sections

### [00:00:00] Introduction — Why the Pipeline Is the Product
The host frames the episode: how a raw recording becomes a polished, discoverable episode. Maya joins and introduces the idea that production infrastructure shapes listener experience as much as the conversation itself.

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Production Pipeline
Maya outlines five stages: (1) Capture, (2) Post-production, (3) Content extraction, (4) Review and approval, (5) Distribution. The host draws an analogy to software build pipelines.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Common pain points: unclear handoffs between team members, inconsistent file naming and storage, and the false assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready content.

### [00:03:03] Transcripts Are Inputs, Not Truth
Discussion of why treating a transcript as clean structured truth leads to low-quality derivative content. Transcripts contain filler, false starts, unverified links, and corrected statements. Key quote: "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."

### [00:04:15] Building a Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical advice: one folder per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, publish log).

### [00:04:54] Raw vs. Clean Transcripts
Why teams should maintain both a raw transcript (for traceability) and a cleaned transcript (for summarization and repurposing). Separating them prevents downstream noise.

### [00:05:49] Where Automation Gets Overestimated
Two areas where teams overestimate automation: editorial judgment (titles, positioning) and exception handling (guest corrections, legal review, sensitive content). Automation should accelerate drafts, not replace approvals.

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Generate multiple title candidates from different angles — problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven — then compare against audience intent. "Frequency is not strategy."

### [00:08:13] The Five Pre-Publish Checks
Highest-value review steps: (1) Names and affiliations, (2) Links and references, (3) Sponsor compliance, (4) Sensitive edits confirmed, (5) Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy.

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Five actions for improving your pipeline this month: standardize episode folder structure, create an owned publish checklist, separate raw and clean transcripts, define a required output bundle, and keep a lightweight episode log for spotting delays and errors.
