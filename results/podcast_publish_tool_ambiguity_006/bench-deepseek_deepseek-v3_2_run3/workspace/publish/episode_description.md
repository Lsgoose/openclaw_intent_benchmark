What does it take to turn a raw podcast recording into a polished episode that listeners can find, navigate, and share? In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen joins us to unpack the podcast production pipeline—the often-invisible system that shapes the entire listener experience.

We break down the five essential stages of production, from capture through distribution, and identify where small teams most commonly struggle. Learn why transcripts should be treated as "rich input, not final truth," how to avoid common automation pitfalls, and what to check before hitting publish.

Maya shares practical takeaways for shows of any size, including how to structure episode folders, create reliable checklists, separate raw from cleaned transcripts, and maintain metadata consistency. Whether you're a solo creator or part of a small team, this episode will help you build a production system that delivers quality without complexity.

Key topics: production workflows, transcript management, automation strategy, title generation, review checklists, and practical systems for consistent podcast publishing.
