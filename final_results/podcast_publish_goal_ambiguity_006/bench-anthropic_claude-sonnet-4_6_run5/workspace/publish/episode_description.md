# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline (with Maya Chen)

Most people think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen thinks the pipeline is the product.

In this episode, Maya — who advises media teams and creator businesses on production systems — walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams break down (handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and over-trusting raw transcripts), what a lightweight but dependable workflow looks like for a two- or three-person team, and how to think about automation without giving up editorial responsibility.

The conversation covers title strategy, the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, the five highest-value pre-publish checks, and why metadata consistency across all surfaces matters more than most teams realize.

Key line: "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."

If you own any part of editing, publishing, or content ops for a podcast, this one is for you.
