# Launch Posts

## Key Takeaway

Your transcript is a rich input—not the final source of editorial truth. Raw transcripts contain filler, false starts, jokes that don't read well, and promises like "we'll put the link in the show notes" that no one has verified yet. If your pipeline treats transcripts as clean structured data, your downstream content suffers. The fix: maintain both a raw transcript for traceability and a cleaned version for summarization and repurposing. One preserves the original. The other reduces noise before any tool or teammate builds on it. Consistency at this stage compounds across everything you publish.

Most podcast teams invest heavily in recording quality but treat post-production as an afterthought. Where does your pipeline currently depend on memory and heroics—and what's the first check or handoff you could standardize to make it reproducible?
