# Episode Description — Episode 14

What separates a polished podcast episode from a raw recording is invisible to most listeners — until it breaks. In this episode, Maya Chen, an advisor to media teams and creator businesses, walks through the five-stage production pipeline that takes an episode from capture to distribution: recording and asset collection, audio post-production, content extraction, editorial review, and final deployment.

Maya explains why small teams typically stumble on handoffs, file naming, and transcript-driven tasks — and why a raw transcript is a rich input, not final editorial truth. She shares a practical framework for small teams to standardize outputs, separate raw from cleaned transcripts, and build a publish checklist that prevents small mistakes from compounding.

The conversation covers how to generate strong episode titles (hint: frequency isn't strategy), where automation genuinely helps versus where it overreaches, and a five-item pre-publish review checklist that catches the errors listeners notice most. Maya and the host close with a simple principle: automate drafts, not approvals.
