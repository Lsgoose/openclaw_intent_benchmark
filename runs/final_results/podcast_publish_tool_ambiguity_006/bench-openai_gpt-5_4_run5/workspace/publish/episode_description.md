# Episode Description

Episode 14 explores what it really takes to build a reliable podcast production pipeline. Host and guest Maya Chen break the process into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. They unpack where small teams usually struggle, including messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the mistake of treating transcripts like publish-ready truth.

The conversation also covers why teams should keep both raw and cleaned transcripts, what automation is good at, where human judgment still matters, and which pre-publish checks deliver the most value. From title strategy to metadata consistency, this episode is a practical guide to making podcast releases more dependable without overcomplicating the system.

If a show is growing and the release process still depends on memory, improvisation, or last-minute cleanup, this episode offers a better way to think about production discipline.
