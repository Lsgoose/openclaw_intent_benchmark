# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

How does a raw recording become a polished, discoverable episode? In this conversation, host and guest Maya Chen break down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explain why production discipline matters even for small teams.

They discuss common failure points like unclear handoffs and sloppy transcripts, the difference between raw and clean transcripts, where automation helps and where it falls short, and the highest-value checks to run before hitting publish. Maya shares a practical framework: standardize your episode folder, define a fixed output bundle, separate raw from clean material, and keep a lightweight episode log.

Key insight: "The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." Use automation to accelerate drafts, but keep a human in the loop for anything that goes public.
