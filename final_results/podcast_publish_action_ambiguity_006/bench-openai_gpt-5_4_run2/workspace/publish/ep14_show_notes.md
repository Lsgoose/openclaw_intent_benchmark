# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Subtitle
How small teams turn raw recordings into consistent, publish-ready episodes

## Short description
A practical conversation with Maya Chen on designing a podcast workflow that stays reliable from capture through review and distribution.

## Long description
In Episode 14, the show digs into what actually makes podcast production reliable. Host and guest Maya Chen break the work into five stages—capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution—and explain why weak handoffs, messy file naming, and unchecked transcript reuse create avoidable failures. The episode also covers where automation helps, where human judgment still matters, and the minimum output bundle a small team should standardize for every release.

## What you’ll hear
- Why “the pipeline is the product” once an episode leaves the recording room
- The five-stage model: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution
- Why a transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth
- What small teams should standardize: folder structure, naming, checklist, and output bundle
- How to use automation for draft generation without automating final approval

## Notable lines
- “The pipeline is the product.” — Maya Chen
- “The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth.” — Maya Chen
- “Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms, but don’t give up responsibility for what goes live.” — Maya Chen
