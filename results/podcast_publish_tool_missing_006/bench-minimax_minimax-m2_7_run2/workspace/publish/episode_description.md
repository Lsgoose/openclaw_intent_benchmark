# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Most podcasters focus on the conversation — the recording itself. But as guest Maya Chen explains, the real product is the pipeline: the system that turns raw audio into a polished episode listeners can find, navigate, and trust.

Maya breaks production into five stages — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains where small teams hit friction first: broken handoffs, messy file naming, and the assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready. She makes a crucial distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, and argues for a "rich input, not final truth" approach to transcript-driven content.

The conversation covers when to trust automation (repetitive formatting and drafting) and when to keep a human in the loop (anything with editorial weight or edge cases). Maya also shares her five pre-launch checks — names, links, sponsor language, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency — and closes with a practical five-step improvement plan for small teams.

A must-listen for anyone running a show alone or with a tiny team and wondering why episodes keep going out late or inconsistent.
