# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

**Guest:** Maya Chen — media systems operator advising creator teams on production workflows

## Overview

A conversation about the invisible infrastructure behind every polished podcast episode. Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a production pipeline, where small teams stumble, and how to build consistency without over-engineering.

## Key Topics

- **Five pipeline stages:** capture → post-production → content extraction → review & approval → distribution
- **Common pain points:** handoff gaps, inconsistent file naming, treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content
- **Raw vs. cleaned transcripts:** keep both; cleaned versions drive downstream quality
- **Automation boundaries:** automate drafts, not approvals — "make the machine produce options, make the human approve what becomes public"
- **Title strategy:** generate candidates from multiple angles (problem, guest, outcome, curiosity); frequency is not strategy
- **Pre-publish checklist:** names & affiliations, links & references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, metadata consistency

## Notable Quotes

> "The pipeline is the product." — Maya Chen

> "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth." — Maya Chen

> "Frequency is not strategy." — Maya Chen

> "A good pipeline is partly about reducing noise before you ask another tool or another teammate to build on the result." — Maya Chen

## Practical Takeaways

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to spot recurring delays or errors
6. Start by automating drafts, not approvals
