# Episode 14 Description

In Episode 14, the show breaks down what it actually takes to turn a raw conversation into a polished podcast release. Host and guest Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a reliable production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

The conversation focuses on the failure points that trip up small teams most often: messy handoffs, inconsistent file naming, weak review steps, and overtrusting raw transcripts. It also covers the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation can genuinely help, and why humans still need to own editorial judgment, exception handling, and anything that becomes public.

If the goal is a more dependable workflow, this episode offers a practical blueprint: standardize folder structure, define a fixed output bundle, use checklists, verify links and metadata, and automate drafts before approvals.
