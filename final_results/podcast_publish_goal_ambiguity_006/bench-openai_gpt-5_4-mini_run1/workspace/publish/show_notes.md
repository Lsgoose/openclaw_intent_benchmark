# Show Notes

## 00:00:00 — Opening: the pipeline shapes the listener experience
The episode opens by framing podcast production as more than operations; the pipeline shapes the final listener experience from raw recording to polished release.

## 00:00:27 — Guest introduction
Maya Chen joins to discuss production systems for media teams and creator businesses.

## 00:00:47 — Why production discipline matters
The conversation establishes that editing, packaging, labeling, and distribution all affect whether people click, navigate, and trust the show.

## 00:01:20 — The five stages of a podcast production pipeline
Maya breaks the workflow into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

## 00:02:29 — Common failure points for small teams
The discussion turns to handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the misconception that transcript-driven work is automatically ready to publish.

## 00:03:09 — Transcripts are inputs, not final truth
A transcript can contain filler, corrections, and unverified references; it is a rich input for drafts, not a replacement for editorial review.

## 00:04:26 — A lightweight but reliable team pipeline
A small team should use one episode workspace, a clear naming convention, a checklist with handoff points, and a fixed output bundle for every release.

## 00:04:54 — Clean transcript versus raw transcript
Keep both versions: raw for traceability and clean for summarization, repurposing, and readability.

## 00:05:49 — Where automation helps, and where it doesn’t
Automation works well for cleanup, first drafts, formatting, and packaging, but not for editorial judgment or exception handling.

## 00:06:57 — Title strategy
The team should generate multiple title candidates from different angles instead of mechanically selecting the most repeated phrase.

## 00:08:13 — Pre-publish review checks
High-value checks include names and affiliations, links and references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.

## 00:09:23 — Practical takeaways for a small show
The episode closes with a simple improvement plan: define folder structure, create an owned checklist, separate raw and clean transcripts, standardize outputs, and keep a lightweight episode log.

## 00:10:05 — Sponsor mention
SoundLayer is mentioned as a collaboration tool for organizing transcripts, asset versions, and review workflows.
