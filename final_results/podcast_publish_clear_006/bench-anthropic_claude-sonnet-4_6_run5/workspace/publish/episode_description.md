# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What does it actually take to turn a raw recording into a polished, publish-ready episode? In this conversation, host and guest Maya Chen — a media production systems advisor — walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya explains why small teams break down at handoffs, why raw transcripts are a rich input but not editorial truth, and where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable. The episode covers practical advice for teams of two or three: standardizing folder structures, separating raw and clean transcripts, defining a fixed output bundle, and running five high-value pre-publish checks before anything goes live.

The core message: production discipline is not overhead — it shapes the listener experience at every touchpoint. A polished pipeline creates alignment across audio, metadata, show notes, and social copy, not just clean audio.

If you run a small show and want a more consistent, less stressful release process, this episode is a practical starting point.
