# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline

Maya Chen joins to break down how small teams can build reliable systems for turning raw recordings into polished, discoverable episodes. The conversation covers the five stages of production — from capture through distribution — and identifies where most teams struggle: handoffs without clear ownership, inconsistent file naming, and treating transcripts as finished content rather than rich inputs requiring review.

Maya explains why raw and cleaned transcripts serve different purposes, how to set healthy boundaries with automation (accelerate drafts, don't abdicate approvals), and why every episode needs a standardized output bundle. The episode includes practical checklists for pre-publish review, strategies for generating titles that actually represent the listener promise, and a five-step roadmap any small show can implement this month to tighten their pipeline without elaborate tooling.
