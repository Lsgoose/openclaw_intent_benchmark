# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

A polished podcast is more than a good conversation. In this episode, we unpack the production pipeline that turns raw recordings into a release-ready package: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

## What the episode covers

- Why the pipeline is the product when reliability matters
- The five stages of a lightweight but dependable production workflow
- Why clean transcripts matter more than raw transcripts for repurposing
- The highest-value checks before an episode goes live
- How to use automation for drafts and packaging without automating approvals

## Key takeaways

- Standardize folder structure and naming conventions.
- Keep raw transcripts for traceability and a cleaned transcript for repurposing.
- Treat titles as a contract with the listener, not just a label.
- Verify names, affiliations, links, sponsor language, and metadata consistency before publish.
- Automate repetitive transforms, but keep human approval on anything public-facing.
