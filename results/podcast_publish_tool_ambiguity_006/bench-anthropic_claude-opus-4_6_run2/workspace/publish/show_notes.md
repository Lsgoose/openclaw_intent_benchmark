## Timestamped Sections

### [00:00:00] Introduction — Why the Pipeline Is the Product
The host frames the episode's premise: a podcast production pipeline shapes the listener experience far beyond the raw conversation itself.

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of Podcast Production
Maya breaks down the pipeline into capture, post-production, content extraction, review & approval, and distribution — and draws an analogy to software build systems.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Discussion of the three most common pain points: handoff confusion, inconsistent file naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content.

### [00:03:34] Transcripts as Input, Not Truth
Why transcripts are excellent first-draft sources but still require human review for facts, links, names, and editorial tone.

### [00:04:15] A Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical advice: one folder per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff points, and a fixed output bundle (audio, transcript, show notes, title, description, chapters, thumbnail, publish log).

### [00:05:49] Automation vs. Editorial Judgment
Where teams overestimate automation — editorial positioning and exception handling — and the healthy mindset of "acceleration, not abdication."

### [00:06:52] Crafting Episode Titles
Why frequency isn't strategy: generating multiple title candidates from different angles and choosing based on audience intent.

### [00:08:13] The Pre-Publish Review Checklist
Five highest-value checks: names/affiliations, links/references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency.

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Five actionable steps listeners can implement this month, plus the principle "automate drafts, not approvals."

### [00:10:05] Sponsor Message & Wrap-Up
SoundLayer mention and closing remarks.
