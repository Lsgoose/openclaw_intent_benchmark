# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

## Episode Description

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. But the production pipeline — how a recording becomes a polished, distributed episode — shapes the listener experience just as much as the content itself.

In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen, a media production systems advisor, break down the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. They cover where small teams most often break down (handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and over-relying on raw transcripts), how to build a lightweight but consistent workflow, and where automation genuinely helps versus where human judgment is irreplaceable.

Key themes include the difference between raw and clean transcripts, how to write titles that serve as a contract with the listener, and the five highest-value pre-publish checks every team should run. The episode closes with a practical five-step plan any small show can act on immediately.
