# Episode 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline — Episode Description

A raw recording is only the beginning. In this episode, host and guest Maya Chen break down the five stages every podcast goes through before it reaches listeners: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya explains why small teams struggle most with invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the dangerous assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready content. She shares a practical framework for building a lightweight but reliable production pipeline — including folder structure, naming conventions, output bundles, and a publish checklist with clear ownership.

The conversation also covers where automation helps (drafting summaries, suggesting chapters, prefilling metadata) and where it falls short (editorial judgment and edge-case handling). Key advice: automate drafts, not approvals. Let the machine produce options; let the human approve what becomes public.

Whether you run a two-person show or manage a small network, this episode offers a concrete starting point for tightening your production workflow this month.
