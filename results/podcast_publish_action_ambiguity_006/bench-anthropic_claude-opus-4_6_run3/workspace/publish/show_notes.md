# Episode 14 — Show Notes

## Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

How does a raw recording become a polished episode with clean audio, a transcript, chapter markers, social clips, and all the metadata listeners expect? In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a podcast production pipeline and shares practical advice for small teams that want consistency without complexity.

## Highlights

- **"The pipeline is the product."** — Maya Chen on why production systems shape listener experience as much as the conversation itself.
- **"The transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."** — On the danger of treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content.
- **"Frequency is not strategy."** — Why the most repeated phrase in a conversation is rarely the best episode title.
- **"Use automation to accelerate drafts, not approvals."** — The healthy mindset for teams experimenting with AI tools.

## Five Pre-Publish Checks

1. Names and affiliations — get the guest's name, title, and company right
2. Links and references — verify every destination mentioned on air
3. Sponsor compliance — confirm required language is present
4. Sensitive edits — ensure anything the guest wanted removed is gone
5. Metadata consistency — title, description, transcript, chapters, and social copy should all describe the same episode

## Five Steps to Improve Your Pipeline This Month

1. Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist that one person owns
3. Separate raw transcript and cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors
