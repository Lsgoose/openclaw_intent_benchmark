# Show Notes

[00:00:00] Opening: why the production pipeline shapes the listener experience, from raw recording to polished episode.

[00:01:20] Major stages of the pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review/approval, and distribution.
- Transcript-driven work includes summaries, quotes, chapter markers, titles, clips, and social copy.
- A good analogy is software build pipelines: source, transforms, validation, packaging, deployment.

[00:02:29] Common failure points for small teams.
- Handoffs without a single source of truth.
- Inconsistent naming and asset storage.
- Transcript-derived content that still needs judgment.

[00:03:09] Why transcripts are not final truth.
- Raw transcripts can include filler, false starts, corrected statements, and unverified link references.
- Transcripts are useful inputs, but they still require review for facts, links, names, dates, affiliations, and claims.

[00:04:26] Lightweight pipeline design for small teams.
- Use one folder or workspace per episode.
- Standardize naming conventions and handoff checklists.
- Keep a fixed output bundle: final audio, clean transcript, show notes, title, description, chapter list, thumbnail, and publish log.
- Keep both raw and cleaned transcripts.

[00:05:49] Automation and judgment.
- Automation helps with repeated transforms like cleanup, formatting, packaging, and draft generation.
- Human ownership is still required for correctness, tone, and release quality.
- Edge cases remain the main failure mode for fully automated systems.

[00:06:57] Title strategy.
- Generate several candidates: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, and curiosity-driven.
- Frequency is not strategy; framing should match audience intent.

[00:08:20] Highest-value review checks before publishing.
- Names and affiliations.
- Links and references.
- Sponsor compliance.
- Sensitive edits.
- Metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapter list, and social copy.

[00:09:13] Practical takeaway for small shows.
- Standardize folder structure and naming.
- Assign one owner to the publish checklist.
- Separate raw and cleaned transcripts.
- Define a required output bundle.
- Keep a lightweight episode log to catch delays and repeated errors.
