How does a podcast go from raw recording to polished episode? In this conversation with production systems advisor Maya Chen, we break down the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

We explore why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the misconception that transcripts equal publish-ready content. Maya explains the critical distinction between raw transcripts (for traceability) and cleaned transcripts (for repurposing), and shares practical advice for teams of 2-3 people.

The discussion covers where automation accelerates workflows (draft generation, formatting, suggestions) versus where human judgment remains essential (editorial decisions, exception handling, final approvals). We also dive into title strategy, pre-publish checks, and how to create consistency across all episode surfaces.

Whether you're a solo creator or small team, this episode provides actionable steps to build a pipeline that reduces errors, saves time, and maintains quality—without overcomplicating your process.
