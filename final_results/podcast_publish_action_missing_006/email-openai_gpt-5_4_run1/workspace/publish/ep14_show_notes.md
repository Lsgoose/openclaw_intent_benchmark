Episode 14: How Small Teams Build a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

In this episode, the host talks with Maya Chen about what it takes to move from a raw recording to a polished, publish-ready podcast episode. The discussion covers the five major stages of a reliable production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

Maya explains why small teams often struggle at handoff points, why inconsistent naming creates version confusion, and why transcript-driven work still needs editorial judgment. A clean transcript can support summaries, chapter suggestions, quote pulls, and social drafts, but names, links, sponsor language, dates, and factual claims still need review before anything goes live.

The conversation also covers the role of automation in podcast operations. The practical advice is to automate repetitive transforms and draft generation, not final approvals. Teams still need a human owner for correctness, tone, and release quality.

Key takeaways:
- Define a standard episode folder structure and naming convention.
- Keep both a raw transcript and a cleaned transcript.
- Standardize the required output bundle for every episode.
- Use a publish checklist with clear ownership.
- Maintain a lightweight episode log to spot delays and repeated errors.
