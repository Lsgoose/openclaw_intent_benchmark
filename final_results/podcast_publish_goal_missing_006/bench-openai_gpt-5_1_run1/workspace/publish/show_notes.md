# Episode 15 – Why Title Tests Fail

## [00:00:00] Why title tests fail
- Framing the problem: teams confuse click-through rate with audience fit.
- Title tests only help if each option reflects a real promise to a real listener.

## [00:00:34] What makes a useful title test matrix
- A matrix works when each title variant maps to a specific audience promise.
- If options are random, you’re just measuring novelty instead of learning.
- Title testing is not a slot machine.

## [00:01:04] Over-focusing on CTR and thumbnails
- Common pattern: teams over-index on packaging, thumbnails, and CTR.
- Listener intent and episode fit get ignored.

## [00:01:33] What to track in a title testing workflow
- Keep a test log for every experiment.
- Capture: hypothesis, audience segment, winning frame, and why it won.

## [00:02:04] Building a healthy title testing workflow
- Use one shared title board across the team.
- Maintain a short review loop and approve a final headline before distribution.
- The lesson isn’t “test everything forever” but “learn which promise matches the episode.”

## [00:02:36] Common mistakes Jordan sees every week
- Running tests without recording the context.
- Reusing a past winning headline in the wrong place and killing performance.

## [00:03:05] A simple plan for small teams this month
- Start a lightweight experiment log.
- Write down the audience promise behind each title option.
- Stop treating click-through rate as the whole strategy.
