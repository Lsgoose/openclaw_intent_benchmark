# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

A reliable podcast production pipeline is what separates amateur shows from professional ones — yet most teams never design one intentionally. In this episode, Maya Chen breaks the pipeline into five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. She explains where small teams struggle most — handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready truth — and offers a lightweight framework anyone can adopt: one folder per episode, a clear naming convention, a single-owner publish checklist, and a standardized output bundle. The conversation also covers where automation helps (drafts, formatting, metadata) and where it falls short (editorial judgment, exception handling), plus a practical pre-publish review checklist and title strategy. The takeaway: make the machine produce options, make the human approve what goes live.
