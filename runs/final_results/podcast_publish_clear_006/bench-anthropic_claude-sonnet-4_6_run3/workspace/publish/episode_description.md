# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the quality of the conversation — it's the production pipeline behind it.

In this episode, host and media systems advisor Maya Chen walk through the five stages of a reliable podcast pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. Maya explains why small teams struggle most with handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the mistaken assumption that a raw transcript is publish-ready content.

The conversation covers how to structure a standard output bundle for every episode, when automation genuinely helps (first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, metadata prefill) and where it falls short (editorial judgment, edge cases), and how to write titles that serve as a real contract with the listener rather than just a label.

Maya closes with five concrete steps any small show can take this month to build a more consistent, reliable pipeline — no elaborate system required.
