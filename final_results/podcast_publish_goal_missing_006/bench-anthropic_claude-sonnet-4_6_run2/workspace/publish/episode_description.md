# Episode 14 – Episode Description

What actually happens between hitting "stop" on a recording and publishing a polished episode? In this conversation, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains why the pipeline itself is the product.

Maya and the host dig into where small teams break down: invisible handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the trap of treating raw transcripts as publish-ready content. They cover how to design a lightweight but reliable system for a two- or three-person team, where automation genuinely helps (first drafts, formatting, metadata prefill) and where it falls short (editorial judgment, edge cases). The episode closes with a six-step checklist any small show can act on immediately.

If you own editing, publishing, or content ops for a podcast, this one is for you.
