# Key Quotes from Episode 14

## Maya Chen

### On transcripts
> "A transcript is a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth."

> "If you don't separate raw from cleaned material, your downstream system inherits every little mess from the source."

> "A raw transcript is not automatically publish-ready copy."

### On automation
> "Use automation to accelerate repetitive transforms: transcription cleanup, first-draft notes, chapter suggestions, formatting, asset packaging, metadata prefill. But don't give up responsibility for what goes live."

> "They usually overestimate automation in two places. One is editorial judgment. The second is exception handling."

> "Fully automated systems tend to fail on edge cases, and media teams live on edge cases."

### On production pipelines
> "The pipeline is the product."

> "A good pipeline is partly about reducing noise before you ask another tool or another teammate to build on the result."

> "Most workflow debt is really state-management debt."

### On titles and metadata
> "Frequency is not strategy. The most repeated phrase in a conversation is often not the most compelling framing."

> "A title is not just a label. It's a contract with the listener."

> "Metadata drift is one of the quietest ways to degrade a show."

### On small teams
> "You don't need an elaborate system to get meaningful gains. You need consistency."

> "First pain point is usually handoffs. Second is inconsistent naming and asset storage."

## Host

### On automation mindset
> "Use automation for acceleration, not abdication."

> "Make the machine produce options. Make the human approve what becomes public."

### On practical takeaways
> "If you're experimenting with automation, start by automating drafts, not approvals."
