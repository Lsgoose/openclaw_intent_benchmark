# Episode 14 Description

A great podcast is not just a great conversation. It is the result of a production pipeline that turns raw recordings into a consistent, trustworthy release.

In this episode, Maya Chen joins the show to break down the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The conversation covers where small teams usually run into trouble, why transcripts need cleanup before they become reusable content, how to think about automation without giving up editorial responsibility, and which pre-publish checks deliver the most value.

If a show has ever suffered from messy handoffs, vague titles, broken links, or inconsistent metadata, this episode offers a practical framework for fixing the process. The takeaway is simple: standardize your outputs, separate drafts from approvals, and build a system that is reliable even when the team is small.
