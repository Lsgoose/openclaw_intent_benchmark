# Show Notes

## 2026-04-28 07:36 UTC — Why the pipeline is the product
Today’s episode opens by reframing podcast production as the thing that shapes the listener experience, not just the thing that happens after recording. The conversation sets up the idea that clean packaging, labeling, and distribution matter as much as the conversation itself.

## 2026-04-28 07:36 UTC — Five stages of a reliable production pipeline
Maya Chen breaks the process into capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. The episode compares the workflow to a software build pipeline: source material, transformations, validation, packaging, and deployment.

## 2026-04-28 07:36 UTC — Where small teams break down
The discussion focuses on handoff failures, inconsistent naming, and the limits of treating transcripts as publish-ready truth. A raw transcript is described as a rich input, not the final source of editorial truth.

## 2026-04-28 07:36 UTC — Clean transcript versus raw transcript
The episode recommends keeping both versions: a raw transcript for traceability and a cleaned transcript for readability, summarization, and repurposing. That separation helps downstream tools avoid inheriting noise from the original recording.

## 2026-04-28 07:36 UTC — Automation with human judgment
The episode argues for using automation to accelerate repetitive work such as transcript cleanup, chapter suggestions, formatting, and metadata prefill, while keeping human ownership over what actually goes live.

## 2026-04-28 07:36 UTC — Titles, review, and release quality
The final stretch covers title strategy, pre-publish review checks, and metadata consistency across title, description, transcript, chapter list, and social copy. The episode closes with a practical takeaway: automate drafts, not approvals.
