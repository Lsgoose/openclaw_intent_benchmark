What does it take to turn a raw podcast recording into a polished episode that reaches listeners? In this episode, we break down the podcast production pipeline with Maya Chen, who advises media teams on production systems.

We explore the five essential stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. Maya explains why "the pipeline is the product" and how small teams can avoid common pitfalls like inconsistent naming, unclear handoffs, and over-reliance on raw transcripts.

Learn practical strategies for creating lightweight but reliable workflows, including the crucial distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts, how to generate effective titles, and which pre-publish checks matter most. We also discuss where automation accelerates production versus where human judgment remains essential.

Whether you're a solo creator or part of a small team, this episode provides actionable steps to build a production pipeline that delivers consistent quality while reducing last-minute stress and preventable errors.
