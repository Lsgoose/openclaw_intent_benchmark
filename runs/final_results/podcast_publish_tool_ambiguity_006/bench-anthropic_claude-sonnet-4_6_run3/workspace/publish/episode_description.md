# Episode 14 — Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

What separates a polished podcast from a chaotic one isn't just the quality of the conversation — it's the system behind it.

In this episode, media systems advisor Maya Chen walks through the five stages of a podcast production pipeline: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution. She explains where small teams most often break down — inconsistent handoffs, messy asset naming, and the mistaken belief that a raw transcript is publish-ready copy.

Maya makes a clear case for keeping raw and cleaned transcripts separate, defining a standard output bundle for every episode, and treating automation as a tool for accelerating drafts rather than replacing editorial judgment. She also covers what a pre-publish review checklist should actually include, and why metadata consistency across title, description, and social copy matters more than most teams realize.

If your show runs on memory and heroics instead of a repeatable system, this episode is the place to start.
