# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline — Show Notes

## Guest

Maya Chen — production systems advisor for media teams and creator businesses  
[Maya Chen bio](https://creators.example.com/guests/maya-chen)

---

## Timestamped Sections

### [00:00:00] Introduction & Why the Pipeline Is the Product
The host frames the episode's focus: how a raw recording becomes a polished, discoverable episode. Maya Chen joins and argues that the production pipeline itself shapes the listener experience.

### [00:01:20] The Five Stages of a Podcast Production Pipeline
Maya outlines five stages: capture, post-production, content extraction, review & approval, and distribution. The host draws an analogy to software build pipelines.

### [00:02:29] Where Small Teams Struggle First
Discussion of common pain points: unclear handoffs, inconsistent file naming, and the false assumption that a raw transcript equals publish-ready content.

### [00:03:34] What to Extract from Transcripts vs. What Needs Human Review
Transcripts are useful for first-draft summaries, chapter suggestions, and quote candidates, but facts, links, names, and sponsor language still require human verification.

### [00:04:15] Designing a Lightweight Pipeline for Small Teams
Practical advice: one workspace per episode, clear naming conventions, defined handoff checklists, and a standardized output bundle (audio, transcript, show notes, chapters, description, publish log).  
[Podcast release checklist](https://creators.example.com/checklists/podcast-release)

### [00:05:49] Automation & Agents — Where Teams Overestimate
Two areas where automation falls short: editorial judgment (audience positioning) and exception handling (guest corrections, legal concerns, sensitive content).

### [00:06:52] Crafting Effective Episode Titles
Generate multiple title candidates from different angles (problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven) and compare against audience intent. Frequency is not strategy.

### [00:08:13] The Highest-Value Pre-Publish Checks
Five priority checks: names/affiliations, links/references, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency across all surfaces.

### [00:09:13] Practical Takeaways
Actionable steps: standardize folder structure, create an owned publish checklist, separate raw and clean transcripts, define a required output bundle, and keep an episode log. Automate drafts, not approvals.

### [00:10:05] Sponsor — SoundLayer
Collaboration tool for small media teams: transcripts, asset versions, and review workflows.
