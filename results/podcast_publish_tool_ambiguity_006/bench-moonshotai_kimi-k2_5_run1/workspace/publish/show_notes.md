## The Five Stages of a Production Pipeline (00:01:27 - 00:02:07)
1. **Capture** — Recording plus companion materials (bios, sponsor copy, links, references)
2. **Post-production** — Audio cleanup, trimming, editorial decisions
3. **Content extraction** — Transcripts, summaries, quotes, show notes, chapters, titles, clips
4. **Review and approval** — Fact checks, sponsor compliance, legal review, sensitive edits
5. **Distribution** — Hosting platform, newsletter, social channels, archives

---

## Where Small Teams Struggle (00:02:33 - 00:03:03)
- **Handoffs** — No single source of truth across record/edit/write workflow
- **Naming and storage** — Version confusion ("final_v2_real_final.wav")
- **Transcript judgment** — Raw transcript is not automatically publish-ready

---

## Transcript Strategy: Raw vs. Clean (00:03:09 - 00:05:49)
- Transcripts contain filler, false starts, jokes that don't read, corrected statements
- Keep raw transcript for traceability; produce cleaned version for downstream use
- Cleaned transcript removes disfluencies, fixes speaker labels, improves readability
- "Rich input, not final source of editorial truth" — what to automate vs. what needs human review
- Anything authoritative (facts, links, sponsor language, names, dates, claims) requires review

---

## Titles as a Contract (00:06:52 - 00:08:13)
- Generate multiple candidates: problem-oriented, guest-oriented, outcome-oriented, curiosity-driven
- Frequency is not strategy — the most repeated phrase isn't always the best framing
- A title is a contract with the listener about what the episode delivers

---

## Automation vs. Abdication (00:05:57 - 00:06:52)
- Two common overestimates: editorial judgment and exception handling
- Agents can propose titles but don't know audience positioning unless encoded
- Edge cases (name corrections, legal reviews, sensitive anecdotes) break fully automated systems
- Healthy mindset: automation for acceleration, not abdication

---

## Pre-Publish Review Checklist (00:08:20 - 00:08:54)
1. Names and affiliations — verify guest details
2. Links and references — confirm actual destinations
3. Sponsor compliance — required language present and correctly placed
4. Sensitive edits — confirm requested removals
5. Metadata consistency — title, description, transcript, chapters, social copy aligned

---

## Five First Steps for Small Teams (00:09:23 - 00:09:50)
1. Define standard episode folder structure and naming convention
2. Create a publish checklist with a single owner
3. Separate raw transcript from cleaned transcript
4. Define the required output bundle for every episode
5. Keep a lightweight episode log to track delays and repeated errors
