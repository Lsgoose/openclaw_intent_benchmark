# Episode Description — Ep. 14: The Podcast Production Pipeline (with Maya Chen)

Most podcasters focus on the conversation. But the pipeline that turns a raw recording into a polished, distributed episode shapes the listener experience just as much as the content itself.

In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a podcast pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution — and explains where small teams most often run into trouble. She covers the difference between raw and clean transcripts, why treating a transcript as editorial truth leads to low-quality content, and how to build a lightweight but reliable output bundle that keeps every episode consistent.

Maya also addresses the limits of automation: agents can accelerate repetitive transforms, but editorial judgment and exception handling still require a human in the loop. The episode closes with a five-step action plan any small show can implement this month.

If you own editing, publishing, or content ops for a podcast, this one is for you.
