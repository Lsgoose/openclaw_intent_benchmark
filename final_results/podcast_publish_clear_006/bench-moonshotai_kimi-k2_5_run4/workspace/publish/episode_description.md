Production pipelines shape every listener's experience, yet teams often notice them only when something breaks. In this episode, media systems advisor Maya Chen unpacks the five stages of podcast production—from capture through distribution—and explains why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and the myth that transcripts are automatically publish-ready. She shares a lightweight framework for 2–3 person teams, including folder structures, checklists, and the critical distinction between raw and cleaned transcripts. The conversation explores where automation helps (repetitive transforms) versus where humans must stay in control (editorial judgment and exception handling). Maya also offers practical tactics for writing titles that serve as contracts with listeners, a five-point pre-publish review checklist, and a one-month action plan to tighten any pipeline. Whether you're refining an existing workflow or building from scratch, this episode provides the systems thinking to make your production reliable.
