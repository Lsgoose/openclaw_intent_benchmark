# Episode Description

What happens between hitting record and publishing a polished episode? This week we're joined by Maya Chen to unpack the podcast production pipeline—from capture and post-production to review and distribution.

We discuss why small teams struggle with handoffs, inconsistent naming, and treating raw transcripts as publish-ready truth. Maya breaks down her five-stage pipeline framework and explains where automation helps (first drafts, formatting, transcription cleanup) versus where humans must stay in control (editorial judgment, exception handling, final approvals).

You'll also hear practical advice on creating standardized output bundles, separating raw and clean transcripts, generating strategic titles, and running a five-point pre-publish checklist. The goal isn't an elaborate system—it's consistency.

Whether you're a solo creator or a small team, this episode offers a framework for building production discipline that listeners notice, even when they can't name it.
