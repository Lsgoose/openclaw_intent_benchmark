Episode 14 explores how a reliable podcast production pipeline turns a raw recording into a polished, discoverable release. Maya Chen breaks the workflow into capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution, then shows where small teams usually stumble: handoffs, naming chaos, and treating transcripts as final truth. The episode argues for keeping both raw and cleaned transcripts, standardizing output bundles, and using automation to speed up drafts, formatting, and packaging without surrendering editorial judgment. It also covers title strategy, pre-publish checks, and the importance of metadata consistency across every surface. The practical takeaway is simple: build repeatable habits, keep humans responsible for what goes live, and automate drafts rather than approvals.
