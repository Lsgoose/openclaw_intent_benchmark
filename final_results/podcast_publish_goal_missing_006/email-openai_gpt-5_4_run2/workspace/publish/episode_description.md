# Episode Description

A polished podcast is not just a great conversation. It is the result of a reliable production pipeline.

In this episode, Maya Chen breaks down the five stages of a strong podcast workflow: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution. The discussion covers where small teams usually struggle, why handoffs and asset naming matter more than people expect, and why transcript-driven content still needs editorial judgment.

The episode also explores the difference between raw and clean transcripts, where automation can genuinely help, and where it still fails on tone, correctness, and edge cases. Along the way, Maya shares practical advice on output bundles, title generation, review checklists, metadata consistency, and lightweight ways to make a small team more reliable without adding unnecessary process.

If the goal is better releases, fewer avoidable mistakes, and a workflow that scales, this conversation maps out the essentials.
