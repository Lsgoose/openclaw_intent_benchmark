# Episode Description

What actually makes a podcast feel polished? In this episode, Maya Chen explains why the production pipeline is part of the product itself. The conversation walks through the five stages of a reliable workflow: capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution.

Along the way, Maya breaks down where small teams usually struggle first, why clean transcripts should be treated differently from raw transcripts, and where automation helps most without replacing editorial judgment. The episode also covers title strategy, metadata consistency, review checkpoints, and the minimum output bundle that keeps releases organized and repeatable.

If a show depends too much on memory, heroics, and scattered files, mistakes compound fast. This episode is a practical guide to building a lighter, calmer system that helps small teams publish with more confidence.
