# Episode 14 Metadata Drafts

## Short description
A practical conversation on how small teams build a reliable podcast production pipeline, from recording and transcript cleanup to review, packaging, and release quality.

## Long description
What turns a raw recording into a polished podcast episode people can trust? In Episode 14, we break down the production pipeline behind reliable releases: capture, post-production, transcript cleanup, content extraction, review, and distribution.

Maya Chen joins the show to explain why small teams struggle with handoffs, messy file naming, and transcript-driven workflows that skip too much judgment. We talk about the difference between raw and cleaned transcripts, where automation actually helps, and why editorial responsibility still matters even when the tools get smarter.

If you manage editing, publishing, or content ops for a podcast, this episode offers a straightforward blueprint for making your release process more consistent without overengineering it.
