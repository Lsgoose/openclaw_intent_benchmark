# Episode 14: Building a Reliable Podcast Production Pipeline

Your podcast isn't just the conversation — it's everything that happens between "stop recording" and "publish." In this episode, production systems advisor Maya Chen breaks down the five stages every podcast moves through: capture, post-production, content extraction, review, and distribution.

We dig into where small teams break down first (messy handoffs, inconsistent naming, treating raw transcripts as finished content) and how to fix it without overengineering. Maya explains why transcripts are a rich input but never the final editorial truth, and where automation helps versus where it quietly creates new problems.

Practical takeaways include building a standard episode folder structure, separating raw and clean transcripts, defining a fixed output bundle, and keeping a lightweight episode log to catch recurring issues. The core principle: automate drafts, not approvals. Let machines produce options; let humans own what goes live.
