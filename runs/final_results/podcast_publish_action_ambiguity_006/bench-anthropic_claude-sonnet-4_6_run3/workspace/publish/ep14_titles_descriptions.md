## Short Description (≤ 100 words)

---

## Long Description

Most podcast teams think the product is the conversation. Maya Chen argues the pipeline *is* the product.

In this episode, Maya walks through the five stages of a reliable podcast production pipeline — capture, post-production, content extraction, review and approval, and distribution — and explains why small teams that skip production discipline end up with compounding, invisible errors.

Key topics:

- Why handoffs, inconsistent naming, and transcript assumptions are the first places small teams break down
- The difference between raw and clean transcripts, and why both matter
- Why transcripts are rich inputs but not final editorial truth — and what that means for automation
- How to generate title candidates from multiple angles and choose based on audience intent
- The five highest-value pre-publish checks: names, links, sponsor compliance, sensitive edits, and metadata consistency
- A practical five-step pipeline improvement plan any small show can act on this month

Guest: Maya Chen, media systems advisor to creator businesses and small media teams.
